### Spontaneous Divine Casters (p.64–65)

Clerics and druids can cast spontaneously (like sorcerers) rather than preparing spells. They know a limited number of spells per level (using Table 2–2, similar to sorcerer spells known), but domain spells / *summon nature's ally* spells still count as known. Bonus spells from Wisdom still apply. Trades versatility for power; characters must choose spells known carefully.

**Turning variant — Level Check (p.66):** Replace the standard turning check with a level check (class level + Cha modifier vs. DC 10 + HD + turn resistance + target's Cha mod). Success "paralyzes" undead for up to 10 rounds of concentration; beat by 5 = turn/rebuke; automatic success vs. creatures with ≤½ your effective cleric level.

**Turning variant — Planar Banishment (p.67):** Channel energy to banish outsiders to home plane (10-round lockout) instead of turning undead. Good-energy clerics banish evil outsiders; negative-energy clerics banish good outsiders. Optional: lawful/chaotic deity clerics banish chaotic/lawful outsiders.

## Gestalt Rules

*(Chapter 2, p.72–75)*

Gestalt characters advance in two classes simultaneously at every level. At each level, choose two classes and take the **best** of each overlapping feature: use the higher Hit Die, the better BAB progression, and the better save progression for each save independently. Skill points come from whichever class grants more, and skills from both class lists are class skills. Class features from both classes are gained in full; where two classes share a feature (e.g., uncanny dodge), it accrues at the faster class's rate. Spellcasting from two classes is tracked separately. Restrictions from each class (arcane spell failure, druid metal armor prohibition, etc.) apply regardless of the other class. Characters cannot combine two prestige classes at any level, and combo prestige classes (arcane trickster, mystic theurge, eldritch knight) are banned. The variant is intended for small groups (3 or fewer players) or high-powered campaigns where every PC uses it; it significantly increases durability and versatility without doubling action economy.

| Strength | Bloodline Levels Required | Traits Gained by 20th Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | 1 (must take before 12th level) | 5 traits: 1 skill boost, 1 special ability, 1 ability boost, 1 special, 1 affinity bonus |
| **Intermediate** | 2 (before 6th and 12th level) | 10 traits: 2 skill boosts, 3–4 specials, 2 ability boosts, affinity bonuses at +2/+4 |
| **Major** | 3 (before 3rd, 6th, and 12th level) | 20 traits: 4 skill boosts, 3 ability boosts, 10 specials, affinity +2/+4/+6 |

Bloodline levels cost one character level and grant no HP, BAB, saves, or class features — they do boost caster level and ability DCs for level-based calculations across all classes. Missing the bloodline level deadline imposes a 20% XP penalty until caught up.

## Action Points

*(Chapter 4, p.122–124)*

**Acquiring points:** A 1st-level character starts with 5 action points. At each level-up, gain 5 + ½ new level points. A character entering a campaign above 1st level starts with 5 + ½ current level.

**Spending:** 1 point per round (can't combine a roll boost and a special action in the same round).

**Uses:**

- **Boost a d20 roll** — After rolling but before DM reveals result, add the result of a die roll: 1d6 (levels 1–7), 2d6 keep best (levels 8–14), 3d6 keep best (levels 15–20). Works on attacks, saves, skills, any d20.
- **Activate class ability** — Gain one extra use of a limited-use class ability (stunning fist, smite evil, etc.).
- **Boost defense** — Fighting defensively: double the dodge bonus (+4 AC, or +6 with 5+ Tumble ranks).
- **Emulate feat** — Gain a feat's benefit for 1 round (must meet prerequisites).
- **Extra attack** — During full attack, make one extra attack at highest BAB.
- **Spell boost** — +2 caster level to one spell (declare before casting).
- **Spell recall** — Prepared casters: re-prepare a just-cast spell. Spontaneous casters: cast a spell without using a daily slot.
- **Stabilize** — Immediately stabilize when dying.

**With feats:** Spending a point can also double the bonus from Combat Expertise, increase Dodge to +2 for full encounter, double threat range with Improved Critical, double Improved Initiative bonus, or add a metamagic effect to a spell at no level adjustment.

**NPCs:** Most shouldn't have action points; significant villains can have ½ their level in points.
