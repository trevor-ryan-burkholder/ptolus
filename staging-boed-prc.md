# Book of Exalted Deeds — Prestige Classes

Source: BoED Chapter 5 (pp. 49–82)

All prestige classes in this chapter share the following rule: A character who ceases to be of good alignment or willfully commits an evil act loses all special abilities and spells, and may not gain new levels in the class. She may regain them through atonement.

---

### Anointed Knight
- **Source**: BoED p. 49
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; BAB +5; Craft (alchemy) 5 ranks, Knowledge (arcana) 3 ranks, Spellcraft 3 ranks; Ancestral Relic feat
- **HD**: d10 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Will good, Ref poor
- **Spellcasting**: None
- **Key features**: Crafts magical oils (100 gp each, 8-hour ceremony) to anoint herself (supernatural powers at 1st/4th/7th/10th level, chosen from a list scaled by level + Cha modifier: holy sight, celestial strategy, unbroken flesh [DR 3/–], divine clarity [+1 to Int/Wis/Cha], call to battle [+3 charge], deep strike [+2d6], inspired strike [extra attack]); also anoints her Ancestral Relic weapon at 2nd/5th/8th level. Bonus combat feats at 3rd/6th/9th level.
- **Ptolus note**: Thematic fit for Knights of the Pale or Order of the Dawn paladins who combine martial skill with alchemical/divine scholarship; the Ancestral Relic prerequisite ties nicely to hereditary knightly lineages common in Ptolus noble houses.

### Apostle of Peace
- **Source**: BoED p. 51
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; Will save base +5; Concentration 10 ranks, Diplomacy 6 ranks; Sacred Vow, Vow of Nonviolence, Vow of Peace, Vow of Poverty
- **HD**: d4 | **BAB**: Slow (+5 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref good, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: Full divine spellcasting progression from 1st level (0–9th level spells over 10 levels), Wis-based; spontaneous cure casting; caster level = apostle levels + ½ other spellcaster levels
- **Key features**: Turn undead (as cleric); 2nd-level Pacifying Touch (calm emotions by touch, no save, no SR); 4th-level Censure Fiends (banish or compel fiends); spells are exclusively healing/pacifying/abjuring (no damage spells on spell list). Cannot wear armor (loses spells for 24 hours if worn).
- **Ptolus note**: Rare NPC class in Ptolus — a wandering holy hermit dedicated to Lothian's merciful aspect, or a fringe sect of the Church of Lothian. The extreme requirements (four Sacred Vows) make this essentially non-viable for most PCs in a combat-heavy campaign.

### Beloved of Valarian
- **Source**: BoED p. 53
- **Requirements**: Female; any good alignment; BAB +7; Knowledge (nature) 5 ranks, Ride 10 ranks; Mounted Combat, Sacred Vow, Vow of Chastity; Special: must meditate at a druid's grove and save the life of an animal in danger
- **HD**: d8 (implied) | **BAB**: Good (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Partial divine progression (4 levels of spells, Wisdom-based, starting at 1st level with 0-level slots)
- **Key features**: Unicorn companion (celestial unicorn bond via Call Unicorn); ethereal jaunt (2nd level); forest dweller (nature camouflage, 3rd level); uncanny dodge (4th); celestial charger mount (6th); nature's understanding (7th); holy smite 3/day (8th); wood repulsion (9th); mass baleful polymorph (10th). Essentially a unicorn-bonded divine ranger.
- **Ptolus note**: No direct Ptolus fit; most applicable to a druid or ranger with strong natural world ties. Could appear as an NPC in wilderness areas outside Ptolus. Not relevant to Church of Lothian or Knights of the Pale.

### Celestial Mystic
- **Source**: BoED p. 55
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; Knowledge (arcana) 4 ranks, Knowledge (the planes) 4 ranks, Knowledge (religion) 6 ranks, Spellcraft 6 ranks; Servant of the Heavens, Sacred Vow, Vow of Abstinence; Able to cast 4th-level spells
- **HD**: d4 | **BAB**: Slow (+5 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing spellcasting class at every level except 1st (essentially full advancement for 9 of 10 levels)
- **Key features**: Studies the mystery of the seventh (illuminated) heaven of Celestia. Each class level grants a "Blessing of the [Heaven]": Silver (1st: electricity resist 10, +2 sacred vs. disease/poison/petrification, magic circle vs. evil); Gold (3rd: immune to magical death); Pearly (5th: never flat-footed, bardic-style planar knowledge check); Crystal (7th: immune to mind-affecting effects from evil outsiders); Platinum (8th: immune to petrification; good spells gain +1 caster level); Glittering (9th: +2 exalted to all saves); Illuminated (10th: considered celestial; gains archon/eladrin/guardinal subtype as appropriate; can cast celestial-component spells freely). At 2nd level gains "Celestial Spells" (can cast spells with celestial/archon component). No armor proficiency.
- **Ptolus note**: Prime fit for a cleric or wizard devoted to the Church of Lothian's scholarly/mystical wing, or a paladin who multiclasses into wizard. The LG alignment and Servant of the Heavens prerequisite (archon allegiance) maps well to Lothian's association with order and heavenly law.

### Champion of Gwynharwyf
- **Source**: BoED p. 56
- **Requirements**: Chaotic good; BAB +6; Intimidate 9 ranks; Knight of Stars, Righteous Wrath
- **HD**: d12 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Grants new divine spells from 1st level (1st–4th level spells over 10 levels), Wis-based; can cast champion spells while raging (furious casting)
- **Key features**: Rage (stacks with barbarian rage for frequency and improvements); detect evil at will; smite evil 1/day (more at 5th and 9th); divine grace at 2nd; damage reduction (2/– at 3rd, scaling to 5/– at 10th); resist enchantment (3rd); fearsome fury (4th — shaken effect on enemies during rage); energy resistance 5→10 (5th/10th); immune to charm and compulsion (7th).
- **Ptolus note**: Not relevant to Church of Lothian or Knights of the Pale (both lawful good institutions). Could appear as a CG barbarian NPC adversary or unexpected ally. The chaotic good requirement makes this incompatible with most formal knightly orders in Ptolus.

### Defender of Sealtiel
- **Source**: BoED p. 58
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment (typically lawful good given flavor); Craft (Int) or similar; (no skill prerequisite listed in text) — implied good saves/BAB for fighter types
- **HD**: d12 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: None
- **Key features**: Defensive stance (1/day at 1st, scaling to 5/day at 9th — +2 Str, +4 Con, +2 resistance on saves, +4 dodge AC, cannot move; lasts 3 + Con rounds; similar to barbarian rage but defensive); shield of faith 3/day (1st); magic circle vs. evil (constant at 2nd); shield other 1/day (2nd); blood of the martyr 1/day (4th); protective aura 1/day → 3/day (6th/10th); glory of the martyr 1/day (8th).
- **Ptolus note**: Strong fit for a Knight of the Pale or Lothianite paladin whose calling is specifically to protect rather than to crusade offensively. "Sealtiel" can be re-flavored as a Lothianite archon patron without changing mechanics.

### Emissary of Barachiel
- **Source**: BoED p. 59
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; Diplomacy 8 ranks, Knowledge (the planes) 4 ranks; Servant of the Heavens, Words of Creation
- **HD**: d6 | **BAB**: Medium (+7 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: Grants new divine spells from 1st level (1st–4th level, bard-style known/day; Cha-based spontaneous casting)
- **Key features**: Calling — celestial call ability 1/day per class level (full-round action; affects humanoids within 30 ft.; Diplomacy check vs. Will save; LG = inspired, NG/CG = blessed, LN = enthralled, N/CN = enthralled+disheartened, evil = stunned+demoralized); Conversion (3rd — affected evil humanoids may be persuaded to change alignment); Sustenance (5th — no need to eat/drink). Spell list is purely persuasive/healing/support.
- **Ptolus note**: Excellent fit for a cleric or paladin of Lothian operating as a church diplomat, evangelist, or legate. The Words of Creation prerequisite connects to Lothian's emphasis on celestial law and divine utterance. A Lothianite "herald" sent to negotiate with neutral or hostile factions would fit this class perfectly.

### Exalted Arcanist
- **Source**: BoED p. 61
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; Will save base +5; Knowledge (arcana) 9 ranks, Knowledge (religion) 4 ranks; Consecrate Spell, Purify Spell; Able to spontaneously cast 3rd-level arcane spells (sorcerer or bard only)
- **HD**: d4 | **BAB**: Slow (+2 at 5th — 5-level PRC) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing arcane spellcasting class at every level after 1st (4 of 5 levels)
- **Key features**: 5-level prestige class. Gains access to an expanded "exalted spell list" (divine good spells as arcane spells: bless, holy smite, hallow, holy word, holy aura, planar ally, etc.); spell knowledge bonus (2 spells at 1st); faster consecrate (2nd — cast consecrated spells without extended casting time); faster purify (4th); sanctified spells (5th — can use sanctified spells as spells known); bonus exalted feats at 3rd and 5th.
- **Ptolus note**: Rare — a LG sorcerer or bard devoted to Lothian's celestial aspect. Could exist as a church-affiliated court wizard or bard in Ptolus's noble circles. More likely an NPC than a PC class in most Ptolus campaigns.

### Fist of Raziel
- **Source**: BoED p. 62
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; BAB +6; Diplomacy 5 ranks, Knowledge (religion) 5 ranks; Power Attack, Servant of the Heavens; Able to cast divine favor
- **HD**: d10 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing divine spellcasting class at every level (full advancement — essentially paladin or cleric levels continue)
- **Key features**: Constant magic circle vs. evil (1st); smite evil (1/day at 1st, scaling to 5/day at 10th) with escalating special properties: 1st = good-aligned weapon, 3rd = confirming (auto-confirm crits), 5th = holy (+2d6), 7th = fiendsmite (+4d6 vs. evil outsiders), 9th = chain (chain lightning-style bounce). Free Sanctify Martial Strike at 4th; sunder evil item at 6th; holy martial strike at 10th. Proficient with all weapons, armor, shields except tower shields.
- **Ptolus note**: Near-perfect fit for a Knight of the Pale paladin. The Fist of Raziel is essentially a crusader-paladin who escalates smite evil into a supernatural weapon. "Raziel" can be re-flavored as a Lothianite celestial captain without mechanical changes. Among the best pure-combat PRC options for a Ptolus paladin PC.

### Initiate of Pistis Sophia
- **Source**: BoED p. 64
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; Fort +5/Ref +5/Will +5 (base saves); Concentration 9 ranks; Feats: requires at least three Sacred Vows (Sacred Vow + two vows of your choice) — implied by text describing "great sacrifices in the form of at least three sacred vows"; Special: must have monk levels (all but exceptional characters are pure monks first)
- **HD**: d8 | **BAB**: Medium (+7 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref good, Will good (all three)
- **Spellcasting**: None
- **Key features**: Adds initiate levels to monk levels for AC bonus, unarmed damage, flurry penalty, speed, and ki strike. Fist of the Heavens (bonus feat at 1st); detect evil at will (2nd); smite evil 1–3/day (2nd/5th/8th); uncanny dodge (3rd); electricity resistance 5 → 10 (4th/7th); improved uncanny dodge (6th); improved evasion (9th); Sacred Vow bonus at 4th/7th/10th; Celestial Transformation at 10th (become celestial type, AC/damage scale as if 1 level higher monk indefinitely). Effectively a celestial monk PRC.
- **Ptolus note**: Applicable to a rare LG monk in Ptolus — perhaps a monk of the Order of Iron Might who has converted to Lothianism, or a monastic order loosely affiliated with the Church of Lothian. The triple-vow requirement makes this the most demanding PRC in the book.

### Lion of Talisid
- **Source**: BoED p. 65
- **Requirements**: Neutral good; BAB +4; Knowledge (nature) 9 ranks, Survival 9 ranks; Favored of the Companions; Able to cast summon nature's ally II; Must have animal companion class feature
- **HD**: d8 | **BAB**: Medium (+7 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing divine spellcasting at every level (full progression)
- **Key features**: Animal companion stacks with druid level; lion's courage (immune to fear, +4 sacred vs. mind-affecting at 1st); scent (2nd); wild shape 1–4/day (3rd+), including Large (6th) and Tiny (9th) forms; Exalted Companion bonus feat (4th); lion's pounce — full attack at end of charge (5th); lion's swiftness — haste 1 round/level per day (7th); leonal's roar 3/day at 10th (sonic burst).
- **Ptolus note**: No strong Ptolus tie. A neutral good druid/ranger in the wilderness outside Ptolus could take this. Not relevant to Church of Lothian or Knights of the Pale (NG alignment restriction excludes most LG characters). Possible NPC in wilderness encounters near Ptolus.

### Prophet of Erathaol
- **Source**: BoED p. 66
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; Knowledge (arcana) 5 ranks, Spellcraft 10 ranks; Sacred Vow, Servant of the Heavens, Spell Focus (Divination), Vow of Chastity; Able to cast 4th-level arcane or divine spells
- **HD**: d6 | **BAB**: Slow (+3 at 6th — unclear if 6 or 10 levels) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing spellcasting at every level except 1st (strong advancement)
- **Key features**: Ecstasy 1/day per class level (channel a celestial to speak through you for 1 round; can produce: any Knowledge check +level+5, divination spell, detect evil, enthrall, good hope, atonement, commune at 500 XP cost; risk: vulnerability to fiend possession during the round of ecstasy); gains prophetic insight as class levels increase. Pure divination/support caster.
- **Ptolus note**: Moderate fit — a LG cleric of Lothian who serves as a church oracle or prophet, channeling celestial will to guide the faithful. The ecstasy mechanic (risk of fiend possession) creates interesting dramatic tension in a city like Ptolus with abundant demonic activity.

### Risen Martyr
- **Source**: BoED p. 68
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; Fort +2/Ref +2/Will +2 (base); any one skill 9 ranks, Speak Language (Celestial); Nimbus of Light, plus any one other exalted feat; Special: must have died in martyrdom and NOT been brought back to life
- **HD**: d12 | **BAB**: Slow (+5 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: None
- **Key features**: Deathless type (not living, no Con score, immune to death/energy drain/etc.). Must advance only in Risen Martyr after rising — cannot take other class levels. 0-level ability: spiritual body (reroll HP as d12s). Charisma +2 at 1st and +2 again at 5th; deflection bonus = Cha bonus; holy purpose +1→+4 sacred bonus on relevant rolls (1st/3rd/6th/9th); bless 3/day (2nd); magic circle vs. evil (2nd); daylight at will (3rd); acid immunity (4th); detect evil at will (4th); protective aura (5th); shield other 3/day (6th); celestial brilliance 1/day (7th); cold immunity (7th); holy aura (8th); electricity immunity (9th); perfection (10th — treated as celestial); final ascension (10th — taken bodily to Upper Planes at next level-up). Villain who kills the martyr may inadvertently create a powerful adversary.
- **Ptolus note**: Strong narrative fit for Ptolus, where martyrdom in the fight against the Cults of Chaos or the Vladaam family is quite plausible. A PC who dies fighting Chaos could return as a Risen Martyr to finish their mission. The 10-level cap and mandatory ascension at the end gives the class a natural story arc.

### Skylord
- **Source**: BoED p. 71
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; Elf or half-elf race; BAB +7; Handle Animal 10 ranks, Ride 10 ranks; Mounted Combat, plus either Mounted Archery or Ride-By Attack, plus one exalted feat; Special: must spend 1 week meditating atop a mountain
- **HD**: d10 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref good, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Advances existing spellcasting at every other level (5 out of 10 levels)
- **Key features**: Celestial flying mount (giant eagle, giant owl, or pegasus — improves in power with skylord level: bonus HD/str/AC/abilities including deadly charge, spell resistance, immunities, bonus feat); eyes of the eagle (permanent long-distance vision); ride the wind (2nd — move at 2× mount speed in open air); improved mounted combat +2/+4/+6/+8 (3rd/5th/7th/9th); deadly charge (4th — charge attack for extra damage); storm kinship (6th — electricity/air spells enhanced); summon tempest (10th).
- **Ptolus note**: Niche class requiring elf race and aerial combat focus. Could appear as an NPC in Ptolus's aerial patrol forces (if any), or as an elvish guardian from the forests beyond the city. Not relevant to Church of Lothian or Knights of the Pale.

### Slayer of Domiel
- **Source**: BoED p. 73
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; Hide 8 ranks, Move Silently 8 ranks; Sanctify Martial Strike, Servant of the Heavens; Special: sneak attack +3d6, evasion class ability
- **HD**: d6 | **BAB**: Medium (+7 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref good, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Grants new divine spells from 1st level (1st–4th level, Wis-based, prepared like cleric, no spontaneous cures)
- **Key features**: Death touch 1–4/day (touch attack; roll 1d6/slayer level vs. target's current HP — if equal or greater, target dies; death effect; only works on evil creatures); detect evil at will; sneak attack +1d6 at 1st, +1d6 per 2 levels thereafter; divine grace (2nd — Cha bonus to all saves); improved evasion (6th). A holy assassin archetype — rogue-based with divine spellcasting and anti-evil death touch.
- **Ptolus note**: Fits a Lothianite spy or inquisitor archetype — a rogue who works in the shadows for the Church of Lothian hunting undead, demons, or cult agents. The Sanctify Martial Strike requirement means their weapon strikes deal holy damage to evil, fitting the "righteous executioner" flavor. Relevant to the criminal/underground aspects of Ptolus.

- **Source**: BoED p. 75
- **Requirements**: Neutral good; Hide 8 ranks, Move Silently 8 ranks; Alertness, Favored of the Companions, Track
- **HD**: d8 (implied) | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref good, Will poor

Effectively a NG ranger/tracker focused on hunting evil.
- **Ptolus note**: Could fit a NG ranger or rogue operating in Ptolus's underworld, tracking cult members or monsters through the sewers and undercity. The NG alignment requirement makes them unlikely Church of Lothian members, but they could be freelance allies of the Knights of the Pale.

### Sword of Righteousness
- **Source**: BoED p. 77
- **Requirements**: Any good alignment; BAB +6; Favored of the Companions, Knight of Stars, or Servant of the Heavens (one of three), plus one additional exalted feat
- **HD**: d8 | **BAB**: Full (+3 at 3rd — 3-level PRC) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Spellcasting**: None
- **Key features**: 3-level prestige class. Grants one bonus exalted feat per level (3 total). That's it — this is a feat-accelerator class for martial characters (paladins, fighters, rangers, barbarians, monks, rogues) who want more exalted feats faster. No spellcasting lost. Paladins and LG monks can freely multiclass in and out.
- **Ptolus note**: Directly relevant for Knights of the Pale paladins and fighters who want to stack exalted feats (Vow of Poverty, Vow of Obedience, Fist of the Heavens, etc.) without committing to a full 10-level prestige class. A 3-level dip that nets 3 bonus exalted feats is excellent value for any martially-focused LG character in Ptolus.

### Vassal of Bahamut
- **Source**: BoED p. 80
- **Requirements**: Lawful good; BAB +7; Craft (armorsmithing) 5 ranks, Diplomacy 5 ranks; Sacred Vow, Vow of Obedience; Special: must have single-handedly slain a juvenile (or older) red dragon
- **HD**: d10 | **BAB**: Full (+10 at 10th) | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Spellcasting**: Grants new divine spells from 2nd level (1st–4th level, Wis-based; 0 slots at 2nd, 1 slot at 3rd, etc.)
- **Key features**: Imperious aura (1st — aura that imposes penalties on evil dragons nearby); platinum armor (1st — armor becomes silver/platinum equivalent, gains properties vs. chromatic dragons); dragon senses (2nd — blindsense or tremorsense); shared trove (2nd/5th/8th — share portion of metallic dragon magic with allies); dragonwrack (4th/7th/10th — weapon deals extra damage to evil dragons, scaling +2d6/1d6 → +4d6/2d6); bonus feats at 3rd/6th/9th. Bonus feats can be dragonslaying/dragon-related.
- **Ptolus note**: Extremely niche. Requires killing a red dragon — unlikely in early Ptolus play. Could be relevant in campaigns where dragon activity is featured. The LG requirement and Vow of Obedience make this a possible Church of Lothian warrior-champion class if Bahamut is treated as a Lothianite celestial patron, but the dragon-slaying requirement makes it impractical for most campaigns.
