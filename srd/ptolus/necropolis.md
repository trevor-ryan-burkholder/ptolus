# The Necropolis

The walled city of the dead, north of Ptolus across the Bull Run River. Officially the city's sanctioned burial ground; in practice a small district unto itself.

## Geography

The Necropolis is enclosed by its own wall — somewhat shorter than the city wall, but sturdy. Access from Ptolus is via the **Necropolis Bridge** in the Rivergate District; access from the country is via the **North Gate** through agricultural villages.

Inside the wall: row upon row of mausoleums, family crypts, monuments, statues, sanctified groves, and official Mortuary buildings. The **Church of Lothian** oversees burial and the city's official end-of-life rites. *(Canon note: Ptolus's only true death-gods are the evil twin **Sisters of Death** — Heiran and Nareis — a minor, barely-tolerated cult, not the city's sanctioned morticians. "Eslathagos Malkith" is the Dread One, not a death-god — see the-spire.md / banewarrens.md.)*

## Inhabitants

- **Living inhabitants**: about 200 people — mortuary workers, Church of Lothian clergy and lay morticians, gravekeepers, and a few oddballs who chose to live among the dead.
- **Undead**: officially, none. In practice, plenty. Wraiths, shadows, allips, ghouls, wights, and occasional more dangerous things drift through tomb-quarters the priests can't keep cleansed. The Knights of the Pale and the Sisterhood of Silence run periodic purges.
- **Living non-undead threats**: necromancers operating in secret, body-snatchers, the occasional vampire taking advantage of permanent darkness in deep crypts.

## Politics

The Necropolis answers to the Church of Lothian (which oversees the dead), the Commissar (via the Watch detachment that mans the gates), and itself (via the Mortuary Guild that runs day-to-day burials). These three powers don't always agree.

Necromantic magic on the *premises* is restricted to Church-sanctioned clergy only. Unsanctioned animation is a hanging offense. Sanctioned animation (e.g., of skeletons for guardian duty) requires Church and Commissar sign-off, which is rare.

## Tunnels

The Necropolis sits over a network of catacombs and Imperial-era tombs that connect, eventually, to the Dungeon below. Some of the oldest crypts are sealed for very good reason. The Mortuary Guild maintains a list of "do not open" tombs that adventurers sometimes find more interesting than they should.

## Encounter notes

The encounter generator's `ptolus_necropolis` environment tag pulls undead-heavy results: skeletons, zombies, ghouls, shadows, allips, wights, wraiths, mummies, spectres, vampire spawn, will-o'-wisps.

## Hooks

- A noble family's crypt has been broken into. They want it found and the looters punished.
- The Necropolis clergy need help: a tomb has been emanating cold and noise for a week. They've lost a novice already.
- A wealthy merchant wants to be buried with his fortune. He has hired guards to ensure his tomb is never disturbed. He hires the PCs as those guards. (Or as the *next* layer of guards. Or as the thieves who clear the previous guards.)
- A wraith haunting the Necropolis was a person the PCs knew. It wants something from them — and has unsettling knowledge about why it died.
- The Knights of the Pale call for adventurers: a vampire spawn cell has been confirmed in the deep crypts. They need flesh-and-blood expendables — er, allies.
