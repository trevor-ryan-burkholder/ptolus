## The Docks

**Location:** At the base of the Cliffs of Lost Wishes, below the rest of the city on a narrow strip of man-made land. Accessible only via cliff lifts or stairs from the city above; feels like its own community.
*(Ptolus p. 170)*

**Character:** Salt-crusted, sinking, and rough. The wooden buildings lean at odd angles as the artificial beach slowly washes away. Sailors, dockworkers, and maritime tradespeople fill the streets from sunup past midnight. The district has been rebuilt four times; mages from the Inverted Pyramid reinforced the land with magic in 702 IA.

**Stats:** Area 16 acres. Population ~2,000. Primary function: Commercial. Primary social class: Low.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Pier Five** — a mystery locale; the strange Dockmaster's domain. (*Ptolus p. 170*)
- **The Sailor's Rest** — Salt Spray Street #227. The nicest bar in the Docks; caters to captains and wealthier visitors. Owner Vala Ivansk lives in the Nobles' Quarter. (*Ptolus p. 175*)
- **The Savage Shark** — rough alehouse; brawls nearly every night. Secret cellar used by smugglers. Halfling pickpocket Ronam Tumblefoot (Longfingers Guild) works the crowd. (*Ptolus p. 176*)
- **Larel's Implements** — Fisherman Street #229. Alchemist and nautical equipment shop; sells compasses, sea charts, sextants, fire-resistant coatings. (*Ptolus p. 174*)
- **The Lighthouse** — on a promontory off Beacon Island; three staff, one always absent due to isolation. Older ruined lighthouse on the island is haunted. (*Ptolus p. 172*)

**Dominant Population:** Sailors, fishermen, dockworkers, shipwrights, sailmakers, ropemakers; rough and hard-working.

**Factions Present:** Longfingers Guild (thieves active in the Docks), Balacazars (smuggling). The Covenant of Blood (vampires) has a pact with sahuagin operating in the bay. (*Ptolus p. 173*)

**Typical Dangers:** Bar brawls, pickpockets, smugglers. Sahuagin from the Whitewind Sea raid the Docks at night, usually seeking magic items or to waylay lone targets. The City Watch is well-staffed here due to the importance of trade, but quietly ignores some illegal establishments. Lizardfolk (assarai) are repeatedly chased off by the Watch.

*(Ptolus pp. 170–178)*

## Guildsman District

**Location:** Southeastern Ptolus, bounded by the Cliffs of Lost Wishes (east), city wall (south), Iron Street (north), and Carriage Row (west).
*(Ptolus p. 179)*

**Character:** "This is where the work gets done." Bells and whistles mark the workday shifts. Soot-stained workers, tanneries, foundries, sawmills, ironworks, and slaughterhouses. The streets are dirty; squatters occupy abandoned warehouses. Second-most dangerous residential area after the Warrens.

**Stats:** Large area. Population varies (guild members and laborers). Primary function: Industrial/Commerce. Primary social class: Low to middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Foundry** — Smith Street #305. Massive metal-working facility; former aeroship mooring tower. Headquarters of the Shuul and the Temple of the Iron God. Over 100 workers. (*Ptolus p. 185*)
- **Darkbirth Madhouse** — Madhouse Street #302. City asylum for the non-magically insane, ~100 patients. A hidden passage from its cellars leads down to the Prison far below. (*Ptolus p. 184*)
- **Grand Guildhall** — Lower Rachen Street #294. Multi-guild meeting hall; the annual Grand Guild Assembly governs the district like a city-within-a-city. (*Ptolus p. 186*)
- **Herbalists' Guildhall** — Herbal Lane #303. Includes physickers, dentists, healers, barbers; rivals with the Healers of the Sacred Heat. (*Ptolus p. 187*)
- **Masons' Guildhall** — Ring Road #309. The Masons' Guild is controlled by the Balacazars. Rival of the Ironworkers' Guild. (*Ptolus p. 188*)
- **Longdraught Brewery** — Coldheart Street #299. Largest brewery in Ptolus; gnome-owned. (*Ptolus p. 188*)
- **Star Jewelers (Gurhorond)** — Storm Street #296. Last dozen Stonelost dwarves; renowned jewelers with an extensively protected workshop. (*Ptolus p. 191*)
- **The Stockyards** — Large livestock-processing operation. (*Ptolus p. 191*)
- **The Skull and Sword** — Iron Street #293. Rough tavern with a secret sex club (The Back Room) in the basement. (*Ptolus p. 190*)
- **Midden Heaps** — Vast refuse piles; home to ratmen, goblins, otyughs, and dangerous oozes. Magical items occasionally found amid the trash. Hidden entrance leads to the sewer system below. (*Ptolus p. 188*)
- **Ullar's Sons' Tannery** — Tannery Street #287. Largest tannery in Ptolus; controls the Leatherworkers' Guild. (*Ptolus p. 193*)
- **Tabby's Den** — Stable Lane #304. Workers' bar; no outsiders welcome. (*Ptolus p. 192*)

**Dominant Population:** Guild members of all ranks; laborers, artisans, craftspeople. Dwarves heavily represented.

**Factions Present:** Ironworkers' Guild (allied with the Shuul and Temple of the Iron God), Balacazars (control the Masons' Guild), Killraven Crime League (extortion of guild workers, trying to move in further). Corrupt City Watch captain Everard Wibert works for Killraven. (*Ptolus p. 152, p. 179*)

**Typical Dangers:** Muggers prey on workers carrying daily wages. Gambling dens, drug parlors, and brothels throughout. Guild rivalries occasionally turn violent (Ironworkers vs. Masons). Midden Heaps encounters: ratmen, goblins, otyughs, CR 1–4; oozes, CR 3–6. The Balacazar-aligned Hadrien Runihan (vampire) operates here. (*Ptolus pp. 179–195*)

## Midtown

**Location:** The largest district, bounded by the King's River (north), Necropolis wall (northeast), Cliffs of Lost Wishes (east), Iron Street (south), and Dalen's Cliffs (west). Twice the size of any other district.
*(Ptolus p. 196)*

**Character:** The heart of Ptolus. Where everyone goes, everyone ends up, and everything is available. Adventurers, commoners, nobles passing through, and exotic creatures all mix here. "If one of the players has a minotaur PC, people should be surprised to see the minotaur, but not completely thrown for a loop."

**Stats:** Area 473 acres. Population ~14,000. Primary function: Residential. Primary social class: Low to Middle.

**Neighborhoods:**
- **Delver's Square (Runihan Square)** — Center Street between Tavern Row and Lower God Row. The adventurer hub of Ptolus; statue of Abesh Runihan at center with main entrance to the Undercity Market behind it. (*Ptolus p. 198*)
- **Emerald Hill** — Elf neighborhood; tree-lined boulevards, elven-style wooden buildings; mostly elves and half-elves. (*Ptolus p. 199*)
- **Fairbriar / Katterwood** — Halfling neighborhoods; Katterwood's Jurrin's Plaza has traditional halfling pony-wagons. (*Ptolus p. 199*)
- **The Mane** — Litorian (lionfolk) encampment; tents and cookfires; unusual urban feel. (*Ptolus p. 200*)
- **Narred** — Centaur neighborhood. (*Ptolus p. 200*)

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Ghostly Minstrel** — Delver's Square #187. Premier inn, pub, and restaurant for adventurers; 30 rooms; 5 sp–2 gp/night. The true center of the adventuring world in Ptolus. A ghost actually haunts it. (*Ptolus p. 204*)
- **Myraeth's Oddities** — Delver's Square. The only general magic item resale shop in the city; the Dreaming Apothecary has run off all competitors except Myraeth. Buys almost anything; stock changes constantly. (*Ptolus pp. 211–213*)
- **Rastor's Weapons** — Delver's Square. Weapons shop. (*Ptolus p. 218*)
- **Ebbert's Outfitters** — Delver's Square. Adventuring supplies; Delver's Guild members get 10% discount. (*Ptolus p. 214*)
- **Bull and Bear Armory** — Delver's Square. Armor sales; often has +1 armor in stock. (*Ptolus p. 201*)
- **Danbury's** — Delver's Square. Arcanists' bar; Dreaming Apothecary agent always present. (*Ptolus p. 202*)
- **St. Gustav's Chapel** — Delver's Square. Small Lothianite chapel; funded by the Church to keep adventurers out of St. Valien's Cathedral. (*Ptolus p. 219*)
- **Iridithil's Home (Elven Sanctuary)** — Emerald Way #173. Elves-only compound headed by Doraedian Mythlord (elf cleric11/ranger4); includes the Wind's Mystery pub and the Order of the Bow. (*Ptolus pp. 207–209*)
- **The Griffon** — Tavern Row #186. Friendly, safe tavern for common folk; 10 rooms. (*Ptolus p. 206*)
- **The Goat** — Tavern Row #189. Quiet, hard-drinking bar; non-humans treated coldly. (*Ptolus p. 205*)
- **The Black Swan** — Tavern Row #190. Dwarf bar run by a half-elf; serves the Black Swan drink. (*Ptolus p. 201*)
- **The Cloud Theater** — #170. Performance venue. (*Ptolus p. 201*)
- **The Onyx Spider** — #193. Bar with sinister reputation. (*Ptolus p. 215*)

**Dominant Population:** Everyone. Adventurers and delvers cluster here more than anywhere else.

**Factions Present:** Delver's Guild (headquartered here), Inverted Pyramid (visible through Danbury's), Dreaming Apothecary, Longfingers Guild (active), Forsaken (occasionally), most organizations have some presence. (*Ptolus pp. 196–227*)

**Typical Dangers:** Street crime, chaos cultists. Midtown is generally safer than the Warrens or Guildsman District but no place in Ptolus is truly safe. At low level, muggings and cult recruitment; at higher level, political or organizational plots intersect here. Terrek Nal (aberration/wizard) lurks in the district.

*(Ptolus pp. 196–227)*

## The Necropolis

**Location:** Northeast Ptolus, atop several small hills near the cliff edge overlooking the Bay of Ptolus. Entirely surrounded by a 12-foot stone wall, 3 feet thick at base, with spiked iron along the top and four gates.
*(Ptolus p. 228)*

**Character:** The city's cemetery, and much more than that. Daytime: gloomy but navigable; serene rows of gravestones, mausoleums, and crypts. Nighttime: genuinely lethal. Undead and demons emerge after dark; experienced Ptolusites simply do not go here at night.

**Stats:** Area 65 acres. Population 0 (official). Primary function: Cemetery. No Watchhouse. No Firehouse.

**Sections:**
- Darklock Hill (central), Bone Hill (northeast), The Quiet (northwest), The Waiting (southwest), The Howling (east/southeast). (*Ptolus p. 228*)

**Notable Locations:**
- **The Dark Reliquary** — Vast gothic mansion; headquarters of the Fallen (demons) and the Forsaken (their living mortal allies). Three above-ground levels plus extensive dungeons including a Temple of the Galchutt 400 feet below. Permanently *unhallowed*; undead gain *bless* effect within. Key inhabitants: Raguel (demon lord, master of the Fallen), Lilith (demon, Raguel's lieutenant), Zastanix (Minister of Security, insect-demon). (*Ptolus pp. 238–276*)
- **The Siege Tower** — Golden Elm Way #127. Three-story keep; headquarters of the Keepers of the Veil. Watchtower overlooking the Necropolis; passage leads directly into the cemetery. ~24 knights at all times. (*Ptolus p. 237*)
- **Hall of the Valiant** — Pre-law structure in the Necropolis; one of few legitimate buildings. (*Ptolus p. 236*)
- **Tower of Terephon** — Built in defiance of city law; lair of Terephon, a Forsaken necromancer. Headquarters of a faction within the Forsaken. Secret passage connects to Dark Reliquary dungeons. (*Ptolus p. 237*)
- **Clasthamus Isle** — Small island within the Necropolis; tended by druid Andach, who maintains a grove and protects it from undead assault every Godsday night. (*Ptolus p. 232*)
- **Crypt Home of Igor Reichstav** — The Waiting, along the south wall. Human eccentric living in an empty crypt; considered the greatest non-Forsaken expert on the Necropolis and the Dark Reliquary. (*Ptolus p. 235*)

**Dominant Population:** Officially none. Actually: Fallen (demons), Forsaken (living death-cult members who wear skull-face paint), undead of all kinds, and Wintersouled (ancient undead sleeping deep below). A few legitimate visitors during daylight hours — gravediggers, mourners, the Keepers of the Veil.

**Factions Present:** The Fallen (PT3 p. 110), The Forsaken (PT3 p. 112), Keepers of the Veil (defenders, Siege Tower), Deathguild (professional mourners), Covenant of Blood (vampires operating in the city). (*Ptolus pp. 228–276*)

**Typical Dangers:** Daytime: suspicious Forsaken members, occasional wandering undead (ghoul, wight, shadow), CR 2–6. Nighttime: treat as open dungeon — ghouls, ghasts, shadows, wraiths, vampires, demons (babau, barbazu), CR 4–15+. The Dark Reliquary interior begins at EL 8 and reaches EL 24 in Raguel's throne room.

**Special Rule:** The Necropolis is tolerated by city authorities because the undead rarely leave the walls, and when they do, the Keepers of the Veil, Knights of the Pale, and City Watch respond. The real danger is growing — the Fallen and Forsaken have become bolder. (*Ptolus p. 230*)

*(Ptolus pp. 228–276)*

## Nobles' Quarter

**Location:** Westernmost Ptolus, atop the Jeweled Cliffs — literally the highest point in the city. Access is controlled: one road in, through the ancient fortress of Dalenguard, up a winding cliff road. Commoners require legitimate business to enter; entry is checked and papers may be demanded.
*(Ptolus p. 279)*

**Character:** Expansive estates with lush lawns, gardens, ivy, and wide stone-paved streets. The smell of flowers, prepared food, and perfume. Buildings are bigger, cleaner, and more architecturally varied than the rest of the city. Residents are almost entirely human, with some elves and half-elves. Very few nonhumans live here; the least diverse district racially.

**Stats:** Large area. Primary function: Residential (upper class). Primary social class: Upper.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Castle Shard** — King's Road #6. Ten-thousand-year-old castle built around a 40-foot violet crystal shard from another plane. Lords: Zavere (reformed blackguard) and Lady Rill (ex-paladin sorcerer18). Throws legendary parties. The shard grants fast healing 5 and +5 bonuses to those it recognizes. Entrance by invitation only. (*Ptolus pp. 285–291*)
- **The Holy Palace** — Crown Street. Home of the Holy Emperor Rehoboth Ylestos and Prince Kirian Ylestos; the de facto seat of what remains of the Empire of Tarsis. The highest-ranking Lothianite temple in the city is here. (*Ptolus pp. 292–295*)
- **The Aristocrat's Table** — Hin Street #23. Most exclusive restaurant in Ptolus; serves exotic foods (manticore steak, etc.); private back room for nobles. (*Ptolus p. 284*)
- **Crown Theater** — #11. Premier theater in the city. (*Ptolus p. 291*)
- **Imperial Academy of Music (The Conservatory)** — Jeweled Road #16. Considered the finest music school in the Empire. (*Ptolus p. 295*)
- **Rosegate House** — #15. A notable manor; in the original campaign, granted to the PCs by the Holy Emperor. (*Ptolus p. 298*)
- **Swordthrower's Club** — #13. Exclusive social club. (*Ptolus p. 301*)
- **Soaring Idyll** — Notable Nobles' Quarter location. (*Ptolus p. 301*)
- **Noble Estates** — Abanar (#17, owns a flying ship the Vanished Dream), Dallimothan (#10), Kath (#3), Erthuo (#8), Vladaam (#, associated with urban werewolves), Sadar (#2, House of Shadows), Nagel (#9), Rau (#4). (*Ptolus pp. 296–301*)

**Dominant Population:** Nobles, wealthy merchants, Church officials, Imperial court.

**Factions Present:** Noble houses (House Khatru commands private army), Holy Emperor and Church of Lothian, Knights of the Dawn, Twelve Commanders (many reside here). Moneylenders cultivate noble clients. (*Ptolus pp. 279–301*)

**Typical Dangers:** Social, not physical. Political assassination, rival noble intrigues. Some noble estates harbor serious threats (Vladaam family, urban werewolves). The Kadaver's bar (#24) is secretly run by a vampire rogue. No street crime — the Watch here is sharp and partisan.

**Special Rule:** Commoners without legitimate business are turned away at Dalenguard. Those denied access have no legal recourse. Neglecting to bring a house gift on a first noble visit imposes a –2 circumstance penalty on Bluff, Diplomacy, and Gather Information checks with the household. (*Ptolus pp. 280–281*)

*(Ptolus pp. 279–301)*

## North Market

**Location:** Centered around the North Gate; bounded by the Rivergate cliffs (west), Necropolis wall (east), city wall (north), and Golden Elm Way (south).
*(Ptolus p. 302)*

**Character:** A true open market — vendors selling from wagons, carts, tents, and stalls in crowded squares. ~75% of goods are perishables and food. Farmers from north of the city bring produce in daily. Loud, bustling, and crowded by day; quiet and poorly lit at night. Haggling is expected and encouraged. (*Ptolus p. 308*)

**Stats:** Area 227 acres. Population ~9,000. Primary function: Commerce. Primary social class: Low to Middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Bith the Ratter** — mobile, usually Calabis Street. Rat-catcher with terrier dogs; willing to track goblin-raised wolves for those who bring them in alive. (*Ptolus p. 305*)
- **The Book Wagon** — mobile. Bookseller. (*Ptolus p. 305*)
- **Killraven's Tower (Killraven Crime League HQ)** — Kevris Killraven's base of operations. (*Ptolus p. 308*)
- **Lendarick** — dog kennel; buy or board dogs. (*Ptolus p. 309*)
- **Mitoren's Blades** — Market Street #113. Budget weapons (anything 50 gp or less). (*Ptolus p. 309*)
- **The Smoke Shop** — Carver Lane #121. Shuul technology shop: spectacles, watches, spyglasses, aelectrical lanterns, firearms. (*Ptolus p. 310*)
- **Red Stallion Pub** — Local watering hole. (*Ptolus p. 309*)
- **Wondrous Tattoos** — Magical tattoos that function like potions (same spells/levels/price +50%). (*Ptolus p. 310*)
- **The Zar'at** — Small enclave of Harrow elves; insular, violent toward orcs, cautious toward others. Leader Karetsan (wizard12). (*Ptolus p. 311*)

**Dominant Population:** Farmers, vendors, laborers, local residents who don't work the market.

**Factions Present:** Balacazars and Killraven Crime League are in open rivalry here — North Market is contested territory. The Shuul maintain the Smoke Shop openly. (*Ptolus pp. 305, 311*)

**Typical Dangers:** Pickpockets, confidence schemes, gang violence between Balacazar and Killraven factions. Rumored demon-merchants in disguise selling cursed goods. At night: quiet but potentially dangerous (muggings in the empty squares).

*(Ptolus pp. 302–311)*

## Oldtown

**Location:** The oldest part of the city, on a ridge below the Nobles' Quarter and above the South Market and Midtown. The only approach from the east is via an earthen ramp on the Emperor's Road into Dalen's Cliffs.
*(Ptolus p. 312)*

**Character:** Originally Ptolus — Dalenguard and the streets around it were the entire city. Now the administrative and governmental center. Government buildings, organizational headquarters, and residential areas sit alongside old (sometimes dilapidated) manor homes and tenements. Second-safest district after the Nobles' Quarter.

**Stats:** Area 264 acres. Population ~10,000. Primary function: Administration/Residential. Primary social class: Middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Dalenguard** — Dalenguard Road #60. Vast ancient fortress; gateway to the Nobles' Quarter; barracks for 300 Commissar's Men; home of the Commissar's Guns (24 cannons). The only land route to the Nobles' Quarter runs through here. (*Ptolus pp. 320–321*)
- **Administration Building** — City government offices; vault beneath holds ~8,000 gp in city funds, guarded and magically sealed. (*Ptolus p. 314*)
- **The Arena** — Greycannon Road #61. Built ~200 years ago; holds gladiatorial combats (60% of events), sports (targetball, horse racing, athletics, 40%). Legal wagering with 10% Imperial cut. (*Ptolus pp. 315–316*)
- **The Imperial University** — Major academic institution. (*Ptolus p. 322*)
- **Delver's Guild Library and Maproom** — Dalenguard Road #58. Members-only; collections of adventurers' handwritten notes purchased by the guild. (*Ptolus p. 322*)
- **Citadel of Might** — Four Fountains Street #70. Headquarters of the Order of Iron Might (warriors' guild); hiring hall for mercenaries. (*Ptolus p. 318*)
- **City Library** — Oldtown's public library. (*Ptolus p. 319*)
- **City Courts** — Official courthouse; a wizard patrols watching for magical influence on judges. (*Ptolus p. 319*)
- **The Inverted Pyramid** — The invisible floating headquarters of the most powerful arcanists in the world; physically present in Oldtown's airspace, invisible and inaccessible to those not invited. (*Ptolus p. 312*)
- **Kadmiel** — A tower that exists only within its own shadow. (*Ptolus p. 312*)
- **Shadow Theater** — Performance venue. (*Ptolus p. 334*)
- **The White House** — Legal social club in Oldtown; secretly owned by Aggah-Shan (lich). Connected to a chaositech machine beneath the city. (*Ptolus p. 336*)
- **Tower of Science** — Under construction by the Shuul. (*Ptolus p. 334*)

**Dominant Population:** Government workers, military (Commissar's Men and families), scholars, long-term residents.

**Factions Present:** Commissar and Imperial government, Inverted Pyramid, Delver's Guild, Order of Iron Might, Church (Ministry of Religion). Balacazar-linked corruption: Watch captain Erda Schenk (Oldtown) works for Balacazars under duress (daughter held hostage). (*Ptolus pp. 152, 312–341*)

**Typical Dangers:** Low street crime (military presence deters it). Criminal activity runs to brothels, gambling dens, and extortion rather than violent crime. Gallows Square holds public executions. Old buildings may be haunted. The Inverted Pyramid can become a threat for those who cross them.

*(Ptolus pp. 312–341)*

## Rivergate District

**Location:** Residential district in the northwest portion of the city; cliffs of Rivergate on its western edge. South of the North Market; across the King's River Gorge from Oldtown.
*(Ptolus p. 342)*

**Character:** The least "Ptolus" of all the districts — the most ordinary. Middle-class residential neighborhoods divided into insular "burrows" (cul-de-sacs where neighbors know each other's business). Ivy-covered walls, pleasant fountains, pigeon-covered statues. Quiet streets at night, well-lit but empty. People here want comfortable lives, not adventure.

**Stats:** Area 79 acres. Population ~10,000. Primary function: Residential. Primary social class: Middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Center Circle** — Good meeting place; central neighborhood square. (*Ptolus p. 344*)
- **Cooper's Store** — Wash Street #87. General store; under Balacazar control for generations. Proprietor Lyala Cooper wants to go legitimate. (*Ptolus p. 345*)
- **Darksoul House** — Tabor Street #92. Appears normal; actually a Sorn (spellcasters-for-hire) cell base of operations. (*Ptolus p. 345*)
- **Finelle's Pleasant Diversions** — Notable location. (*Ptolus p. 342*)
- **Barbarian Balloonists** — Halfling hot-air balloon enthusiasts who launch from within the city, dodging towers for sport. (*Ptolus p. 344*)
- **Golathan Naddershrike's House and Laboratory** — Alchemist or mage dwelling. (*Ptolus p. 342*)
- **Jangave Lesh's Training Hall** — Fighting school. (*Ptolus p. 342*)
- **Three-Horned Goat** — Local pub. (*Ptolus p. 342*)
- **Well of the Shadow Eyes** — Notable and strange location. (*Ptolus p. 342*)

**Dominant Population:** Middle-class human families; merchants and craftspeople who have "made it" enough to move here from the market districts.

**Factions Present:** Balacazars (through Cooper's Store and landlord rackets), Sorn cell (Darksoul House), chaos cults beginning to move into the district (Emerald Eye cult, per rumors). (*Ptolus pp. 342–350*)

**Typical Dangers:** Low. Break-ins, minor gang crime. The real threats are hidden — Balacazar pressure on businesses, a Sorn cell operating covertly, the occasional chaos cult. Myraeth Tuneweaver (owner of Myraeth's Oddities in Midtown) lives here; he is a CR 13 wizard and his home is effectively off-limits to thieves. (*Ptolus p. 213*)

*(Ptolus pp. 342–350)*

## South Market

**Location:** Newest district; from Dalen's Cliffs (west) to Carriage Row (east), city wall (south) to a rough line dividing it from Midtown (north). Heart of the district is Star Crossing Plaza.
*(Ptolus p. 350)*

**Character:** More shops than open markets — artisans create goods in the workshop behind the shop and sell out front. Specialized shops (one sells only rope, another only belts, etc.). Busy and crowded by day; quiet and empty at night. Newer streets with regular square-cut paving stones.

**Stats:** Area 238 acres. Population ~8,000. Primary function: Commerce. Primary social class: Middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Blackstock Printing** — Iron Street #277. Print shop secretly run by magical clones of a dead elf wizard named Aelian Fardream. An insane clone called "The Shadow Eyes" operates in the city as a criminal. (*Ptolus pp. 353–354*)
- **The Cock Pit** — South Street #283. Largest illegal gambling den in Ptolus; started as a cockfighting arena. Run by Aggah-Shan (lich, follower of chaos). The Game of Mrathrach is played here — a magical game that collects chaotic energy funneled to a chaositech machine below the city. 20 guards on premises. (*Ptolus pp. 355–357*)
- **Exotic Market** — Bladeburn Street #254. One-of-a-kind imports, foreign magic trinkets, unusual animals; a gnome named Inlios Pabovini (sorcerer9) effectively runs it. (*Ptolus p. 358*)
- **Fabric Market** — Woodsman Street #261. Commodities market for cloth. (*Ptolus p. 358*)
- **Mahdoth's Asylum** — Childseye Street #262. Underground facility for mad spellcasters specifically (contrasted with Darkbirth Madhouse for non-magical insane). (*Ptolus p. 361*)
- **Korben Trollone's Office (Edarth's Loans)** — Killraven Crime League boss in the South Market; keeps trained exotic animals. (*Ptolus p. 361*)
- **The Golden Tooth** — Citation Street #249. Goldsmith Hannis Goldtooth; no ready-made goods, custom work only. (*Ptolus p. 360*)
- **Faraway Scents** — Off Horseweed Street #248. Perfume shop. (*Ptolus p. 360*)
- **Navaen Bowcraft** — Bowyer. (*Ptolus p. 342*)
- **Rogue Moon Trading Company** — Large merchant company from Tarsis with Ptolus presence; currently in trouble with Imperial authorities. (*Ptolus p. 364*)
- **Ramoro's Bakery, Maran's Odd Sizes, Salora's Pots, Donnel's** — Various shops. (*Ptolus p. 342*)

**Dominant Population:** Artisans, shopkeepers, middle-class laborers, merchants.

**Factions Present:** Balacazars (crime boss Halgrim Fatherstone, weakening), Killraven Crime League (growing, through Korben Trollone), Aggah-Shan/chaos (the Cock Pit). Corrupt Watch captain Bartel Denton works for the Balacazars. (*Ptolus pp. 152, 360*)

**Typical Dangers:** Burglary is the primary crime (not mugging). Ratman activity increasing (brazen burglaries, street attacks at night). Smugglers use warehouse areas near the Guildsman District border. Mahdoth's is a hazard — escaped mad spellcasters. At night the district is near-empty.

*(Ptolus pp. 350–365)*

## Temple District

**Location:** Bounded by the King's River (south), North Gate Road (west), Carver Lane (east), and Golden Elm Way (north).
*(Ptolus p. 368)*

**Character:** "The most exotic of all the districts." Strange music, chants, incense. Self-flagellating monks, street preachers, pilgrims of a dozen faiths, holy processions. A strange hodgepodge of good and evil — benevolent temples stand near dangerous chaos cults. The Street of a Million Gods runs through the heart of the district. Even the Blessed Bridge across the King's River has shrines built into it.

**Stats:** Area 110 acres. Population ~8,000. Primary function: Religious. Primary social class: Middle.

**Notable Locations:**
- **St. Valien's Cathedral** — Largest Church of Lothian in Ptolus. Archbishop Adlam Theobold (cleric20) performs true resurrection by appointment (3-week wait minimum). Adventurers and wounded are actively turned away — this is a worship space, not a healing station. (*Ptolus pp. 376–377*)
- **Temple of Gaen** — Off Street of a Million Gods #145. Silver and crystal dome glows like the sun; second most popular deity in the city. High priests Melior Kalen and Barit Calomar (magically fused into identical beings). (*Ptolus pp. 385–386*)
- **Priory of Introspection** — Malav Street #160. Headquarters of the Sisterhood of Silence (~150 members); outsiders forbidden past the Great Hall. (*Ptolus pp. 374–375*)
- **Temple of Navashtrom** — Lachros Street #154. God of harmony through strength. (*Ptolus p. 388*)
- **Temple Observatory of the Watcher of the Skies** — Street of a Million Gods #142. 200-year-old magical telescope can observe distant planes. (*Ptolus pp. 389–390*)
- **Temple of the Rat God** — Terrock Road (near #137). Secret chaos cult with hidden cellars; ties to ratmen, Forsaken, and the Ebon Hand cult. (*Ptolus pp. 391–392*)
- **Temple of the Ebon Hand** — Terrock Road #137. Active chaos cult serving the Galchutt; kidnaps young people and transforms them into troll-like "children of the hand." On the verge of being banned by city authorities. (*Ptolus pp. 379–384*)
- **Temple of Gaen, Temple of Asche, Temple of Ahaar, Temple of Teun** — Major temples lining the Street of a Million Gods and nearby roads. (*Ptolus pp. 368–397*)
- **Temple of Teun** — Chalice Road #138. Temple of the Mother of All Machines; the Shuul use it as a base of operations. Run by ancient high priestess Maeda Von Rustal (cleric20) in a steam-powered wheelchair. (*Ptolus pp. 395–396*)
- **Celestial Conclave** — Street of a Million Gods #148. Worships the act of religious faith itself; greatest experts on all religions in the city; vast library of sacred texts. (*Ptolus p. 372*)
- **Hall of Heroes** — Sponsored by the Delver's Guild; statues of Ptolus' greatest heroes. (*Ptolus pp. 372–373*)
- **House of the Sacred Heat** — Center Street #166. Healers who use holy fire; serve those who cannot afford temple prices; only 5 gp donation for treatment. (*Ptolus pp. 373–374*)
- **Shrine of the Oracle** — Redoubt Street #165. Domed tower; home of the Brotherhood of the Oracle and Nayvras, the current Oracle (seventh daughter of a seventh daughter). (*Ptolus pp. 375*)
- **Conciliator Chapterhouse** — #102 on map. (*Ptolus p. 372*)
- **Order of the Fist Compound** — Martial monks. (*Ptolus p. 373*)
- **Stormwrought Campanile** — Malav Street #159. 100-foot belltower serving multiple religions simultaneously. (*Ptolus p. 377*)

**Dominant Population:** Priests, pilgrims, religious orders, scholars of religion, worshippers of dozens of faiths. Most of the city visits at some point.

**Factions Present:** Church of Lothian (dominant; St. Valien's), Sisterhood of Silence (Priory of Introspection), Knights of the Pale (nearby), Gaen's clergy, Shuul (Temple of Teun), chaos cults (Ebon Hand, Rat God). (*Ptolus pp. 368–397*)

**Typical Dangers:** Rival religious factions; fanatics. Active chaos cults are dangerous — the Ebon Hand is actively kidnapping. The Rat God temple has ratmen and were-rats in the cellars (CR 1–4). Chaos cultists: CR 3–8. High-level threats are the chaos cult leaders and any demons they summon.

**Special Rule:** Holy water available at any temple for 25 gp/vial in virtually unlimited quantity. Healing potions and scrolls sold as "donations" — selection depends on temple and PC's relationship with that faith. (*Ptolus p. 370*)

*(Ptolus pp. 368–397)*

## The Warrens

**Location:** East side of the city, adjacent to the Cliffs of Lost Wishes; bordered by Salt Street (west), the Cliffs (east), Seamist Street (north), and petering out south of Curtain Street.
*(Ptolus p. 400)*

**Character:** The darkest, poorest, and in some ways most dangerous part of Ptolus. A maze of ramshackle wood and stone buildings with crude additions; doorways often just fabric or a wooden barricade; windows boarded or barred. Streets unnamed, no addresses; no signs on most businesses. Disease runs rampant. Fire is a constant threat (no Fire Brigade or Watchhouse). Crowded tenements and squatters in every vacant lot.

**Stats:** Area 13 acres (smallest district). Population ~2,000. Primary function: Residential. Primary social class: Low. No Watchhouse. No Firehouse.

**Notable Locations:**
- **Chapel of the Final Resolution** — Headquarters of the Vai (assassins' guild). Contact made through a coded message through a courier named Rintha (wizard7) who travels between here and Kerrik's in Midtown. (*Ptolus p. 405*)
- **The Eight Shadows** — #314. Dilapidated building housing a shivvel den, brothel, and the Eight Shadows — eight Balacazar-employed wizards (wizard6 each) who serve as spies, enforcers, and assassins. Also traffic slaves and evil magic items. (*Ptolus pp. 407–408*)
- **Pale Dogs HQ** — Gang controlled by the mysterious Jirraith, a shapeshifter with no true appearance. Balacazar-linked. (*Ptolus pp. 406–407*)
- **Lackie's** — Unmarked pub run by Lackie (Harrow elf); serious drinking establishment. Lackie also operates as the "Beggar King" — managing 100+ beggars as an intelligence network across the city. (*Ptolus p. 407*)
- **Dark Elf Refuge** — Home to Urshanna (dark elf cleric9), who provides a base of operations for dark elves in the city. (*Ptolus p. 409*)
- **Spyncer Coil's Location** — The eccentric keeper of the Swords of Ptolus hides in the Warrens. (*Ptolus p. 411*)

**Dominant Population:** The desperately poor, criminals, gangs, diseased, beggars. Some residents are simply unlucky people surviving in terrible conditions, not criminals.

**Factions Present:** Pale Dogs (Balacazar-linked gang), Eight Shadows (Balacazar wizard-enforcers), Vai (assassins), dark elves (Urshanna's network), chaos cultists (some). No City Watch presence. (*Ptolus pp. 400–411*)

**Typical Dangers:** Disease (cackle fever, filth fever; Fortitude saves on extended stay advisable). Gang violence; muggings; the Pale Dogs are territorial. The Eight Shadows will deal with anyone who threatens Balacazar interests. Extreme fire hazard — no Fire Brigade. Dark elves armed and hostile to intruders. At CR range: street gangs CR 1–3, Eight Shadows wizards CR 6 each, Jirraith CR unknown (shapeshifter crime lord).

**Special Rule:** No Watchhouse, no Firehouse. The City Watch does not patrol here. PCs are entirely on their own. Outsiders are noticed immediately and watched. (*Ptolus p. 401*)

*(Ptolus pp. 400–411)*

## The Undercity Market

*Not a surface district — a sub-area beneath Midtown, accessed via the staircase in Delver's Square behind the statue of Abesh Runihan.*

The Undercity Market is the underground marketplace directly beneath Delver's Square. It is referenced throughout the districts book as a distinct commercial area where adventurers buy and sell goods unavailable or restricted at surface level — the Dark Market (black-market magic and slaves) operates deeper within. The Urnst brothers run an alchemy shop here. It is part of the broader Undercity/Dungeon complex described in PT7: Beneath the Streets (p. 423). It is not counted as one of the city's eleven official districts.

*(Ptolus p. 198, p. 155 [Urnst brothers reference], PT7 p. 423)*
