## The 30-second pitch

Ptolus is a fantasy city of about 75,000 people, built at the foot of an impossibly tall **Spire** of dark stone capped with a crown of swords. Beneath the city sprawls **the Dungeon** — miles of tunnels, ruins, and lost empires layered downward to and beyond a fortress called Ghul's Labyrinth. The city was the heart of the fallen **Empire of Tarsis**, then a successor of the **Imperial Church of Lothian**, and is now an autonomous polity dominated by **noble families, the Church, and the Commissar**. Cults of chaos work in its shadows; drow scheme below; demons leak in from cracks the Spire opened. It's a dungeon-crawl city — a place where every alley is a thread that ends underground.

## Tone and pace

Ptolus is *busy*. There's always something on fire, someone vanished, a rumor of buried gold, a cult sacrificing in a sewer, a noble's wedding gone wrong, a strange figure asking after a particular ring. Lean into density. The campaign book has more hooks than any campaign can use — pick the threads your party tugs.
