# Ptolus Pantheon

The Ptolus pantheon is dominated by **Lothian** (the Lawgiver), whose Church holds the largest religious power in the city. Around Lothian sit the "New Gods" and many older ones, plus the dark powers the chaos cults serve. *(Verified against Ptolus, City by the Spire — Religion chapter; deity entries give "Name, Title (Alignment). Domains.")*

When the campaign places Ptolus in Faerûn (see `campaign/setting/`), these gods remain dominant in and around the city while Faerûn's pantheon dominates elsewhere. See `campaign/setting/pantheon-coexistence.md`.

## Major gods of Ptolus (canonical)

Format: **Name, Title** (alignment) — domains. (Domains marked * are new domains defined in Ptolus PT6 "Magic": Civilization, Technology, Sleep, Light, etc.)

- **Lothian, the Lawgiver** (LG) — Good, Law, Protection, Sun. Justice, mercy, the rule of law, and healing; the dominant Church of Ptolus. Paladins of Lothian are the Knights of the Pale.
- **The Watcher of the Skies** (LN) — Knowledge, Law, Protection. A deity that knows and sees all, including the future; an impassive observer, possibly linked to the Old Man. Tied to the Imperial Eye iconography.
- **Teun, Mother of All Machines** (LN) — Civilization*, Law, Technology*. An old Prustan deity of invention and craft; revered by Grailwarden dwarves and Forge workers.
- **Tevra** (LN) — Healing, Law, Technology*. Daughter of Teun; said to sleep now, awaiting a future age.
- **Navashtrom, God of Strength and Harmony** (NG) — Good, Protection, Strength. Popular in Ptolus; his followers are often warriors and adventurers. (Most powerful local cleric: Tellian Riverborn.)
- **Tardeshou, Goddess of Truth** (LG) — Good, Knowledge, Law. Sister of Navashtrom, overshadowed by his worship.
- **Gaen, Goddess of Light** (LG) — Good, Law, Light*. A popular and powerful religion; the Temple of Gaen is among the city's largest.
- **Ahaar, Lord of the Air** (CG) — Air, Good, Protection. An elemental sky god; worshipped by Cherubim elves.
- **Phoeboul, God of Dreams** (CN) — Chaos, Knowledge, Sleep*. An elvish god, patron of the Dreaming.
- **Niveral, God of Secrets** — patron of secret-keepers. The minotaur cleric **Shibata** serves him (PT5 p. 397).
- **Asche, God of Cities** — civic patron. Spyncer Coil forged the three Swords of Ptolus after a vision from Asche.
- **Myliesha, Mistress of the Wind's Path** (CG) — Air, Good, Travel. An elven elemental deity.
- **Melann** — a nature goddess; revered by tribal centaurs (aram) and rural folk.
- **Mocharum** — a dwarven god (brother of another dwarven deity); has a temple in the Temple District.
- **Rajek the Wanderer** — an aram (centaur) god of luck, protection, and travel.

The Temple District holds dozens more (Temple of Asche, Temple of the Fifty-Three Gods of Chance, Iron Gods, Mocharum, Kharos, Ynchabolos, and others). For a specific lesser deity not listed here, check the core book's Religion chapter before putting it on screen rather than inventing one.

## The dark powers (chaos cults)

Worshipped openly only by the Cults of Chaos. The *Night of Dissolution* arc covers them in detail.

- **The Galchutt (the Dread Ones)** — sleeping primordial Lords of Chaos imprisoned beneath Ptolus; awakening them is the goal of most chaos cults.
- **The Vested of the Galchutt** — the Galchutt's first servants, made with their essence: **Baalhazor** (Demon God of Technology, patron of chaositech), **Gorgoth-Lol**, **Mrathrach**, **Thoggidrum**, and **Vladaam** (ancestor of the noble house).
- **Eslathagos Malkith** — a dark power identified with **Danar (the Dread One)**, who built the Banewarrens. *Not* a benign civic death-god — treat references to him as tied to the Banewarrens/Dread One.
- **The Fallen** — actual demons in the Necropolis (Raguel and Lilith) who direct the mortal **Forsaken**.

## Domains in Ptolus

A 3.5e cleric picks two domains, normally including at least one from the deity's list above. The Church of Lothian, for instance, expects Good plus one of Law, Protection, or Sun. (Domain spell lists: PHB pp. 184–185; new Ptolus domains in PT6.)

## Player-facing notes

- The Church of Lothian is *the* mainstream religion; multiple gods sharing one shrine is normal in cosmopolitan Ptolus.
- Open worship of chaos gods, demons, devils, or the Galchutt is illegal — punishable by death.
- Necromancy is heavily regulated; animating undead within the city is grounds for arrest by the Watch unless properly sanctioned.
