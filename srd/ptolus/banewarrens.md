# The Banewarrens

> For the Spire, Jabel Shammar, and Goth Gulgamel, see `the-spire.md`.
> The expanded adventure source for the Banewarrens is the standalone product *The Banewarrens* by Monte Cook (Malhavoc Press), which takes characters from approximately 6th to 10th level.

---

## What Are the Banewarrens?

The Banewarrens are a vast catacomb complex built by the cleric **Danar Rotansin** beneath his tower, **Mosul Pearl** (which later became **Jabel Shammar**). They predate the Spire itself -- in fact, their construction and the evil they concentrated is what caused the Spire to be created (Ptolus p. 419).

Danar's purpose was noble: he sought to rid the world of its remaining evil influences by gathering all the evil artifacts, objects of dark power, trapped essences of vanquished fiends, demonic relics, and even the last vestiges of particularly horrible diseases. He condemned all of these things as "banes" and imprisoned them. He believed that destroying banes simply released their evil back into the world -- so instead he sealed them away (Ptolus p. 75).

The Banewarrens are thus a **repository of the world's worst evil** -- not a dungeon built by evil, but a prison built to contain it.

## History

### Construction (~7,500 BE)

Danar Rotansin, a powerful and benevolent cleric, built Tremoc Korin (the Banewarrens) beneath his tower Mosul Pearl, located near the sea. The facility was built in a compartmentalized fashion using **magical machines called warding generators** and separated by **sealed doors** -- so that even the breach of a section would not compromise the entire facility. At the time, only Danar could open any of these doors (Ptolus p. 419).

His allies in this effort:
- **Saggarintys the Silver King** -- a silver dragon (later imprisoned in the Banewarrens)
- **Bastion** -- a planetar (later corrupted to darkness; now called the Malificite)
- **Averon** -- a solar (later corrupted to darkness; now called Dark Averon) (Ptolus p. 419)

### Creation of the Spire (~7,420 BE)

The evil stored within the Banewarrens grew so repugnant that the earth itself thrust Tremoc Korin away, creating a tall, impossibly high and narrow spire atop which Mosul Pearl stood. Danar repaired the damage done to Tremoc Korin by this upheaval (Ptolus pp. 75-76, 419).

### Danar's Corruption and Fall (~7,420 BE)

The proximity of so much concentrated evil -- particularly the **Book of Inverted Darkness** -- corrupted Danar. He became **Eslathagos Malkith, the Dread One**. When this transformation took place, the Banewarrens changed as well.

As the Dread One, he used his power to try to break free of the world (see "Praemal as a Prison Plane"). Despite all his might, he failed, but his attempts to reach other worlds created an extradimensional space in the Spire called **the Dread One's Path**. From this area he searched other worlds for even more evil items. Among them: **the antithesis stone**, a bane that corrupted others, turning good into evil -- which he used to transform many of his former friends and allies into malevolent forces (including Bastion into the Malificite, and Averon into Dark Averon) (Ptolus p. 419).

### After the Dread One's Defeat

Some part of Danar's good spirit managed to re-seal the Banewarrens after the Dread One's defeat. Although some banes were released when he became Eslathagos Malkith, the Banewarrens still contain vault upon vault of evil artifacts, foul creatures, and vile relics (Ptolus p. 77).

After his defeat, the Elder Gods sought to destroy the evil of Jabel Shammar forever -- but despite all their power, the best they could do was seal it off magically (Ptolus p. 501).

## Structure and Layout

### The Outer Vaults

The Banewarrens consists of a series of **Outer Vaults** that ring what is now the Spire -- remember, these halls were built before the Spire existed. The Outer Vaults are accessible from the surface and surrounding underground areas (Ptolus p. 419).

### The Inner Vaults

Within the perimeter of the Outer Vaults lie the **Inner Vaults**. These contain more dangerous banes and require deeper penetration of the complex to reach (Ptolus p. 419).

### Tremoc Korin -- The Baneheart

The very worst of the banes Danar placed in a chamber called **Tremoc Korin**, the Baneheart. Originally this lay directly beneath Mosul Pearl. When the Spire was formed, Danar used the opportunity to reconstruct Tremoc Korin, placing the banes farther apart from each other.

Today, the Baneheart occupies **most of the interior of the Spire** as a **3,000-foot shaft** filled with balconies and adjoining individual vaults -- one for each of the most terrible banes. This shaft connects the deep underground Banewarrens to Goth Gulgamel halfway up the Spire, and ultimately to Jabel Shammar at the top (Ptolus p. 419).

### The Dread One's Path

An extradimensional space created by Eslathagos Malkith within the Spire during his attempts to reach other worlds. This is the route from the deep Banewarrens into the dungeons of Jabel Shammar. A secret door at the end of the Path (Search DC 40) can be opened only with the Banewarrens key; beyond it, one can pass magically up into Jabel Shammar's dungeons. There is also an elevator in the Dread One's Path leading into the Jabel Shammar dungeons, but it can be activated only from the dungeons side (Ptolus p. 506).

## Security Systems

The Banewarrens' security was Danar's ingenious engineering:

- **Compartmentalized design:** Built so that breach of one section does not compromise the entire facility (Ptolus p. 419).
- **Warding generators:** Magical machines that power the sealing magic of each section (Ptolus p. 419).
- **Sealed doors:** Separating sections; originally only Danar could open them. Now, the Banewarrens key can open any (Ptolus p. 419).
- **Crafty traps and powerful guardians:** Beyond the seals (Ptolus p. 419).

### The Banewarrens Key

An artifact made from **the Dread One's own hand** that can open any of the sealed doors in the Banewarrens. It is also one of the three items (along with Parnaith's mirrored sphere and the Dread One's staff) that can be used to access the Entropy Sphere in Goth Gulgamel and reach Jabel Shammar.

**Current location:** The vaults of **House Vladaam** in Ptolus. The current members of House Vladaam do not recognize its significance (Ptolus p. 419).

## What's Inside

The Banewarrens contain everything Danar gathered over a lifetime of hunting the world's evil:

- Evil artifacts and objects of dark power
- Trapped essences of vanquished fiends and demon gods
- Demonic relics
- Dark magical items
- Last vestiges of horrible diseases
- Trapped creatures of enormous power (including Saggarintys the Silver King, now imprisoned within)
- The antithesis stone (which corrupts good into evil)
- The Black Grail (sought by the Pactlords of the Quaan; Ptolus p. 131)

The Orcus connection: The book does not explicitly connect the Banewarrens to Orcus specifically. Demonic banes are present generically.

---

## Location Relative to the Dungeon

The Banewarrens sit beneath Ptolus, in the deep underground. Their Outer Vaults connect to:
- **Ghul's Labyrinth** (multiple connection points)
- Natural caverns and other underground structures
- The Baneheart shaft running up through the Spire

The Banewarrens are specifically called out as one distinct portion of the vast underground beneath Ptolus, alongside Dwarvenhearth, Ghul's Labyrinth, the natural caverns, and the Undercity (Ptolus p. 414).

## Campaign Implications

**When do PCs encounter this?** The Banewarrens are intended as a mid-campaign site, taking characters from approximately 6th to 10th level. They are designed to follow the introductory Ptolus urban adventures and precede the high-level Goth Gulgamel / Jabel Shammar content (Ptolus p. 488).

**The Banewarrens adventure** by Monte Cook is the dedicated sourcebook for running this location, with room-by-room detail, all relevant NPCs, and the full adventure arc.

**Why does it matter?**
- Contains the only key (the Banewarrens key) to open the sealed doors -- and ultimately to reach Jabel Shammar.
- Contains the Dread One's Path, which is one of the three known routes into Jabel Shammar.
- The Black Grail inside is actively sought by the Pactlords of the Quaan, who have a plan to break in.
- Saggarintys the Silver King is imprisoned within the Inner Vaults.
- Bastion (the Malificite) and Dark Averon are connected to the Banewarrens' history -- former allies of Danar, now corrupted by the antithesis stone.

**Factions interested in the Banewarrens:**
- **Pactlords of the Quaan** -- actively planning to break in to obtain the Black Grail (Ptolus p. 131)
- **House Vladaam** -- unknowingly possess the Banewarrens key
- Various dark elf factions have sought entry (Ptolus p. 438)
- Sokalahn (a lich) spent decades attempting to breach it before giving up (Ptolus p. 438)
- The Silver Sisters (Karanosin and Karalada) once sought entrance to both the Banewarrens and Jabel Shammar (Ptolus p. 422)

## DM Notes -- The Big Picture

The Banewarrens represent the **original sin of the Ptolus setting**: a good man tried to solve the problem of evil by locking it all in one place, and the concentration of that evil corrupted him utterly, creating the Dread One and everything that followed -- the Spire, Ghul's attraction to it, the Labyrinth, and ultimately the Cults of Chaos seeking to free the Galchutt who were drawn here by all of it.

The Galchutt connection: The Galchutt -- the imprisoned primordial entities sleeping deep beneath the Spire -- are the ultimate reason everything terrible in Ptolus is here. The Banewarrens are one layer of that onion: Danar was unconsciously drawn to this place by the Galchutt's subtle influence, just as Ghul was, just as the dwarves were, just as every evil creature is (Ptolus p. 414).
