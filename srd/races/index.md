# Races (3.5e PHB + Ptolus + Sourcebooks)

PHB races, Ptolus-specific races, and races from the five Races sourcebooks. Page citations where available. For racial prestige classes, see `srd/classes/prestige-classes.md`. For monster races as PCs, see `srd/races/monster-pcs.md`.

---

## PHB races (no level adjustment)

### Human
- **Stats**: No ability modifier.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Bonus feat** at 1st level.
- **Extra skill points**: +4 at 1st, +1 each subsequent level.
- **Favored class**: Any (no XP penalty for multiclassing).
- **Languages**: Common (bonus: any).
- **In Ptolus**: The majority of Ptolus's 75,000 residents are human, drawn from Imperial (Prustan), Palastani, Rhothan, Uraqi, and other ethnicities — names are so mixed that ethnicity rarely maps to appearance anymore.

---

### Dwarf (Grailwarden)
- **Stats**: +2 Con, –2 Cha.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 20 ft. (not slowed by armor or load).
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Stonecunning** (+2 Search for unusual stonework; automatic check within 10 ft.).
- **+2 saves vs. poison, spells, and spell-like abilities.**
- **+1 attack vs. orcs and goblinoids.** **+4 dodge AC vs. giants.**
- **+2 Appraise** (stone/metal), **+2 Craft** (stone/metal).
- **Weapon familiarity**: dwarven waraxe and urgrosh treated as martial.
- **Favored class**: Fighter.
- **Languages**: Common, Dwarven (bonus: Giant, Gnome, Goblin, Orc, Terran, Undercommon).
- **In Ptolus**: The dominant dwarves in the city are the Grailwarden dwarves from the east — serious, skilled artisans who integrate into the Imperial economy with ease. Their craftwork commands a premium throughout the Empire.

---

### Elf (Shoal Elf)
- **Stats**: +2 Dex, –2 Con.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision**, immune to magic sleep, +2 saves vs. enchantment.
- **+2 Listen, Search, Spot.** Automatic Search check within 5 ft. of secret doors.
- **Weapon proficiency**: longsword, rapier, longbow, shortbow (composite versions included).
- **Favored class**: Wizard.
- **Languages**: Common, Elven (bonus: Draconic, Gnoll, Gnome, Goblin, Orc, Sylvan).
- **In Ptolus**: "Shoal elf" is just "elf" to Ptolusites — dark-haired, sea-loving, and more accustomed to humans than elsewhere. Elves congregate in Emerald Hill (Midtown) and gather at Iridithil's Home. Unlike Core Rules, elves in Ptolus do sleep — and place special value on dreams.

---

### Gnome
- **Stats**: +2 Con, –2 Str.
- **Size**: Small (+1 AC/attack, +4 Hide, –4 grapple, ¾ carrying capacity). **Speed**: 20 ft.
- **Low-light vision**, +2 saves vs. illusions, +1 DC to own illusion spells.
- **+1 attack vs. kobolds and goblinoids.** **+4 dodge AC vs. giants.**
- **+2 Listen, +2 Craft (alchemy).**
- **Spell-like abilities** (1/day each, Cha 10+ to use): *speak with animals* (burrowing mammal), *dancing lights*, *ghost sound*, *prestidigitation*.
- **Favored class**: Bard.
- **Languages**: Common, Gnome (bonus: Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Giant, Goblin, Orc).
- **In Ptolus**: Gnomes (called "loresong faen" in old texts) love magic and music — two things Ptolus has in abundance. They're rarest of the major races; most live in Cherubar or scattered communities in Palastan and Rhoth.

---

### Half-Elf
- **Stats**: None.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision**, immune to magic sleep, +2 saves vs. enchantment.
- **+1 Listen, Search, Spot.**
- **+2 Diplomacy, +2 Gather Information.**
- **Favored class**: Any (no XP penalty for multiclassing).
- **Languages**: Common, Elven (bonus: any).
- **In Ptolus**: Half-elves are always half Shoal elf in Ptolus (Cherubim and Harrow elves don't mix with other races). Ptolus has more half-elves than virtually any other city — the cosmopolitan port culture encourages it.

---

### Half-Orc
- **Stats**: +2 Str, –2 Int, –2 Cha.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Favored class**: Barbarian.
- **Languages**: Common, Orc (bonus: Draconic, Giant, Gnoll, Goblin, Abyssal).
- **In Ptolus**: Most half-orcs trace lineage to the Ornu-Nom tribes of Palastan and Rhoth (friendly enough with the city); a few are half Toruk-Rul or Sorn-Ulth (more aggressive outsiders). Ptolus accepts half-orcs better than most Imperial cities, though they still face prejudice.

---

### Halfling
- **Stats**: +2 Dex, –2 Str.
- **Size**: Small. **Speed**: 20 ft.
- **+1 attack with thrown weapons and slings.**
- **+1 saves vs. all (racial luck), +2 vs. fear (stacks).**
- **+2 Climb, Jump, Listen, Move Silently.**
- **Favored class**: Rogue.
- **Languages**: Common, Halfling (bonus: Dwarven, Elven, Gnome, Goblin, Orc).
- **In Ptolus**: Halflings call themselves "saelas faen" (quicklings) and range from settled city-dwellers to nomads who wander the plains of Palastan. In Ptolus they're known for loyalty and surprising social overlap with litorians — both cultures value personal honor and nomadic freedom.

## Ptolus-specific races

These races appear in or around Ptolus and may be taken as PCs at DM discretion. Unless noted, a DM should consider limiting Ptolus-specific minor races to one per party.
*(Source: Ptolus, City by the Spire, pp. 49–60)*

---

### Stonelost Dwarf
Surface-dwelling dwarves driven from their ancestral home of Dwarvenhearth beneath Ptolus by Ghul hundreds of years ago. They refuse to go back — deserting the city cost them their right to it in their own minds — but won't permit anyone else in, either.
*(Ptolus, p. 49)*

- Mechanically: Use the standard **Dwarf** entry above. No changes.
- **Favored class**: Fighter.
- **In Ptolus**: Most Stonelost dwarves work as artisans, architects, engineers, or merchants. The largest concentration lives in Kaled Del, a subterranean community near a Dwarvenhearth entrance. Stonelost are a common sight in Midtown and the Guildsman District — steady, skilled, and melancholy about a home they won't reclaim.

---

### Litorian
Nomadic feline humanoids from the grasslands of Palastan. Individual litorians who break from their prides sometimes come to Ptolus seeking adventure, purpose, or to repay a lifedebt.
*(Ptolus, pp. 53–56)*

- **Abilities**: +2 Str, +2 Con, +2 Dex.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision**, **Scent** (as MM Glossary).
- **Racial skills**: +2 Intimidate, Search, Spot, Listen, and Survival.
- **Languages**: Automatic — Common, Litorian. Bonus — Gnoll, Goblin, Orcish.
- **Favored class**: Ranger.
- **Level Adjustment**: +0.
- **In Ptolus**: Visiting litorians stay at the Mane (Midtown). The most famous Ptolus litorian is Rastor, who owns Rastor's Weapons in Delver's Square. Litorians place enormous weight on personal honor, lifedebt, and loyalty — a litorian PC saved by another character may swear a lifedebt that defines their arc.

---

### Aram (Centaur)
Centaurs — called aram by themselves and "centaurs" by dwarves — are boisterous, arrogant, and boorish, yet often genuinely likeable. In Ptolus they find work hauling loads, serving as guards, or acting as messengers. Can't stand staying still.
*(Ptolus, pp. 49–52)*

- **Abilities**: +8 Str, +4 Dex, +4 Con, –2 Int, +2 Wis.
- **Size**: Large (–1 AC, –1 attack, –4 Hide, +4 grapple; double carrying capacity).
- **Space/Reach**: 10 ft./5 ft. **Speed**: 50 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Racial Hit Dice**: 4d8 monstrous humanoid (gives +4 BAB, Fort +1/Ref +4/Will +4, 7×(2+Int mod) skill points, 2 feats).
- **Natural armor**: +3.
- **Racial skills**: Listen, Move Silently, Spot, Survival (class skills from racial HD).
- **Languages**: Automatic — Aram, Common. Bonus — Elvish, Gnomish, Halfling, Sylvan.
- **Favored class**: Ranger (though fighter is equally common in the city).
- **Level Adjustment**: +2.
- **In Ptolus**: Most aram in Ptolus live in Narred (Midtown neighborhood). They're respected for their help against Ghul historically, but their inability to stay still makes them unreliable in clerk or artisan roles. Aram adventurers are rare but memorable — Turas was a founding Delver's Guild member before disappearing near Umbral Lake.

---

### Cherubim (Winged Elf)
Ancient winged elves from the western land of Cherubar, self-absorbed and timid, who come to Ptolus only rarely. Their fragile frames and fear of injury make them cautious, but their ability to fly makes them formidable adventurers despite this.
*(Ptolus, pp. 52–53)*

- **Abilities**: +4 Dex, –4 Con.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.; Fly 40 ft. (average maneuverability).
- **Low-light vision.**
- **Racial skills**: +2 Spot.
- **Languages**: Automatic — Common, Elven. Bonus — Gnomish, Halfling.
- **Favored class**: Wizard.
- **Level Adjustment**: +2.
- **Special notes**: Cherubim virtually never mate outside their own kind (no half-Cherubim). Some have elemental air creatures in their lineage. They are often mistaken for angels or aasimars.
- **In Ptolus**: Cherubim elves visit rarely; when they do, they spend time almost exclusively at Iridithil's Home in Midtown. Notable: Zaetra, a Cherubim wizard who is close friends with Doraedian Mythlord.

---

### Harrow Elf
Once Elder Elves captured and twisted by Ghul in Goth Gulgamel, Harrow elves bear the physical and spiritual corruption of their forebears — horrible birthmarks, scarred flesh, and an inborn pull toward darkness. Many have a death wish; many more overcame it and became fierce, ruthless adventurers.
*(Ptolus, pp. 53–54)*

- **Abilities**: +2 Dex, –2 Con, –2 Cha.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision.**
- **Racial skills**: +2 Craft (alchemy), +2 Intimidate, +2 Use Magic Device. Also +2 to Disguise when posing as a Shoal elf (Shoal elves get +2 Spot to see through it).
- **Spell-like abilities** (1/day each): *detect magic*, *ghost sound*, *mage hand*, *prestidigitation*, plus one 1st- or 2nd-level spell (player's choice, approved by DM).
- **Languages**: Automatic — Common, Elvish. Bonus — Elder Elvish, Gnomish, Halfling.
- **Favored class**: Monk.
- **Level Adjustment**: +1.
- **Alignment notes**: Lean toward evil but not limited to non-lawful (unlike other elves). Shorter-lived than Shoal elves (lifespan similar to humans).
- **In Ptolus**: Most Harrow elves live in the Zar'at neighborhood of North Market (PT5, p. 310). They're mistrusted by nearly everyone and despised by other elves. An occasionally-encountered type: Gaerioth Shadowhand, Harrow elf member of the Company of the Black Lantern.

---

### Lizardfolk (Assari)
Called assari in Ptolus, lizardfolk are more common to the south but a handful live legally in Ptolus, mostly thanks to the Brotherhood of Redemption. They keep to themselves and avoid the affairs of other races as much as possible.
*(Ptolus, p. 50 — brief mention; use MM stats)*

- Mechanically: Use the standard MM **Lizardfolk** stat block. Level Adjustment +1.
- **Favored class**: Druid.
- **In Ptolus**: Extremely rare as PCs; more common as unusual NPCs or dungeon inhabitants. The Brotherhood of Redemption has vouched for a small number in the city.

## Races of Destiny

*Source: Races of Destiny (2004)*

---

### Illumian
A race of contemplative humanoids created through ritual magic, bound to a mystical alphabet of glowing sigils that float around their heads. Multiclass specialists who prize versatility above all.
*(RoD, p. 51)*

- **Stats**: No ability modifier (human subtype).
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Luminous Sigils (Su)**: Head orbited by softly glowing runes (candle-light). Can suppress as standard action (loses all sigil benefits). Sigils interact oddly with glyph/rune/sigil/symbol spells: –4 save if character level < caster level; immune if ≥ caster level.
- **Power Sigils (Su)**: At 1st level, choose one power sigil (each grants +1 to checks using a linked ability score). At 2nd level in any class, gain a second different power sigil; each sigil's bonus increases to +2. Sigils: *Aesh* (+Str checks), *Hoon* (+Wis/Con checks), *Krau* (+1 caster level), *Naen* (+Int checks), *Uur* (+Dex checks), *Vaul* (+Cha checks).
- **Illumian Words (Su)**: Each pair of power sigils forms a "word" granting an extra ability (15 combinations, all favor multiclassing). E.g., Aeshkrau lets you use Str for bonus spells; Naenhoon applies metamagic via turn attempts without increasing casting time.
- **Final Utterance (Ex)**: Upon death, body releases Illumian syllables for 1 round per HD.
- **+2 saves vs. shadow descriptor spells.**
- **Superior Literacy**: Always literate; Speak Language always a class skill.
- **Favored class**: Any (highest class never counts for multiclass XP penalty; can freely re-enter paladin and monk).
- **Languages**: Common, Illumian (bonus: any).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Rare outsiders from distant cabals. Treated as exotic curiosities. No established illumian community in the city — a PC illumian is likely a scholar from an eastern fortress-monastery who came to plunder Ptolus's ancient library.

---

### Aasimar
Humans with celestial blood — distant descendants of good outsiders, born with quiet sanctity.
*(RoD, p. 91)*

- **Stats**: +2 Wis, +2 Cha.
- **Type**: Outsider (native) — must eat/sleep.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Daylight (Sp)**: 1/day (CL = character level).
- **Resistance to acid 5, cold 5, electricity 5.**
- **+2 Listen and Spot.**
- **Favored class**: Paladin.
- **Languages**: Common, Celestial (bonus: Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Gnome, Halfling, Sylvan).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: More present than expected given the city's demonic history — celestial-blooded souls feel called to oppose the Darkness Below. Many work with the Knights of the Pale or Keepers of the Veil.

---

### Tiefling
Humans with infernal blood — touched by fiendish ancestry, marked by subtle wrongness others feel before they can name.
*(RoD, p. 102)*

- **Stats**: +2 Dex, +2 Int, –2 Cha.
- **Type**: Outsider (native).
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **+2 Bluff and Hide.**
- **Darkness (Sp)**: 1/day (CL = character level).
- **Resistance to cold 5, electricity 5, fire 5.**
- **Favored class**: Rogue.
- **Languages**: Common, Infernal (bonus: Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Gnome, Goblin, Halfling, Orc).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: More present here than almost any other city — the Spire and Dungeon are saturated with demonic residue, and infernal bloodlines surface with unusual frequency. Some are children of cultists; others are centuries-old lineages tied to the Age of Chaos. Cluster near the Necropolis fringes and Undercity. The Brotherhood of Redemption has worked with tieflings seeking purpose.

---

### Mongrelfolk
Humans of so thoroughly mixed lineage they carry traits from a dozen races — gnome ears, orc tusks, halfling feet. Looked down upon everywhere, resilient because of it.
*(RoD, p. 97)*

- **Stats**: +4 Con, –2 Int, –4 Cha.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision.**
- **Emulate Race (Ex)**: Automatically emulates any humanoid race for magic item use. +4 to UMD for non-humanoid races.
- **Sound Imitation (Ex)**: Mimics any voice or sound heard (DC 16 Will to detect).
- **Immunity to sleep effects.**
- **Diffuse Blood**: +2 saves vs. race-targeting spells (covers human, halfling, dwarf, elf, gnome, goblin, orc ancestry).
- **+1 saves vs. enchantment and illusion; +1 saves vs. poison.**
- **+1 on Appraise, Climb, Jump, Listen, Move Silently, Search, Spot.**
- **+4 on Hide and Sleight of Hand.**
- **Favored class**: Rogue.
- **Languages**: Common (bonus: any).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: The most downcast residents of the Warrens and Undercity. A mongrelfolk PC would come from those communities, having scratched survival as a Delver or petty thief.

---

### Half-Ogre
The offspring of ogres and humans — Large, powerful, and poorly received in polite company.
*(RoD, p. 92)*

- **Stats**: +6 Str, –2 Dex, +2 Con, –2 Int, –2 Cha.
- **Type**: Giant. **Size**: Large (–1 AC, –1 attack, –4 Hide, +4 grapple, ×2 carry). **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **+4 natural armor.**
- **Favored class**: Barbarian.
- **Languages**: Giant, Common (bonus: Draconic, Gnoll, Goblin, Orc, Abyssal).
- **LA**: +2. **ECL**: 3 (+ class levels).
- **In Ptolus**: Rare but not unknown in Guildsman District where brute strength is valued. The Brotherhood of Redemption might vouch for one seeking purpose.

---

### Sea Kin
Humans whose ancestors returned to the sea — air-breathing, aquatically adapted.
*(RoD, p. 99)*

- **Stats**: None (human subtype). **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.; Swim 30 ft.
- **Low-light vision.**
- **+4 skill points at 1st level, +1/level thereafter.**
- **+2 Escape Artist.** **Hold Breath**: Con×8 rounds before drowning risk.
- **Weapon Familiarity**: Tridents and nets as simple weapons.
- **Water Dependency**: Must fully immerse in water 1×/week or take 1 Con damage/day (Fort DC 10 + 1/day, recovers in water).
- **Favored class**: Druid. **Languages**: Common (bonus: Aquan, Elven, Sahuagin).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Sea kin dockworkers and sailors are plausible in Ptolus's harbor city. A sea kin Delver who grew up on the wharves fits naturally.

---

### Sharakim
Once human, transformed by fell magic into orc-like beings — but their human minds survive and their culture is scholarly.
*(RoD, p. 101)*

- **Stats**: +2 Str, –2 Dex, +2 Int, –2 Cha. **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.** **+1 natural armor.**
- **Shadow Affinity (Ex)**: In darkness/shadow, +1 attack, +1 AC, +2 Intimidate.
- **Favored class**: Wizard. **Languages**: Common (bonus: Dwarven, Elven, Giant, Gnoll, Goblin, Infernal, Orc).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: Mistaken for intelligent half-orcs at first glance. A sharakim scholar researching their people's original transformation — possibly linked to Ghul-era events — is a compelling PC origin.

---

### Underfolk
Cave-adapted humans — wiry, stealthy, and deeply clannish, generations removed from the surface.
*(RoD, p. 107)*

- **Stats**: None (human subtype). **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.** **+4 skill points at 1st, +1/level thereafter.** **+2 Listen.**
- **Weapon Familiarity**: Light/heavy picks as simple; net as martial.
- **Favored class**: Any. **Languages**: Common (bonus: Dwarven, Goblin, Orc, Terran, Undercommon).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Could be descendants of humans who went into the Dungeon and never came out. Some live permanently in the upper Undercity. A PC underfolk would know the Dungeon's upper levels better than any surface-dweller.

## Races of Stone

*Source: Races of Stone (2004)*

---

### Goliath
Mountain-dwelling nomads built like small giants — massively muscled, competitive, deeply honorable.
*(RoS, p. 53)*

- **Stats**: +4 Str, –2 Dex, +2 Con.
- **Type**: Monstrous Humanoid (simple weapons only; no armor proficiency). **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Powerful Build**: Treated as one size larger for grapple, bull rush, trip, size-based special attacks. Can wield Large weapons without penalty.
- **Mountain Movement**: Standing jumps as running jumps; accelerated climbing without penalty.
- **Acclimated**: Immune to altitude sickness permanently.
- **+2 Sense Motive.**
- **Favored class**: Barbarian. **Languages**: Common, Gol-Kaa (bonus: Dwarven, Giant, Gnoll, Terran).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: Extremely rare in the city. Most citizens have never seen one. Their bulk causes constant practical problems (doorways, taverns). Possible connection to Stonelost dwarves as old mountain allies.

---

### Chaos Gnome
Gnomes touched by raw chaotic magic — more powerful but unable to settle anywhere permanently.
*(RoS, p. 87)*

- **Stats**: +2 Dex, +2 Con, +2 Cha, –2 Str. **Size**: Small. **Speed**: 20 ft.
- **Low-light vision.** **Weapon Familiarity**: Gnome hooked hammers as martial.
- **Spell Power**: +1 effective CL on chaos-descriptor spells.
- **+4 dodge AC vs. giants.**
- **Favored class**: Sorcerer. **Languages**: Gnome (bonus: any).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: Even rarer than standard gnomes. Their chaotic magic makes other spellcasters nervous. One who has temporarily settled in Ptolus's arcane community would stand out even among gnomes.

---

### Dream Dwarf
A dwarf subrace touched by the mystical "earth dream" — quieter and more spiritually attuned than Grailwarden dwarves.
*(RoS, p. 85)*

- **Stats**: As standard dwarf (+2 Con, –2 Cha). All standard dwarf traits apply.
- **Dream Sight (Su)**: Sees ethereal creatures as easily as material ones; can distinguish them on sight.
- **Spell Power**: +1 CL for divination spells and earth-descriptor spells while in contact with the ground.
- **+2 Diplomacy with earth-subtype creatures.**
- **Favored class**: Druid. **Languages**: Common, Dwarven (bonus: Draconic, Elven, Gnome, Terran, Undercommon).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Dream dwarves are distinct from Grailwarden and Stonelost dwarves — meditative, less martial. One might be studying the earth dream's response to the Dungeon's corruption. Their Dream Sight is invaluable in the ghost-haunted Necropolis.

---

### Whisper Gnome
A gnome subrace adapted to stealth — quiet, cautious, gifted with silence magic. The antithesis of the typical gregarious gnome.
*(RoS, p. 95)*

- **Stats**: +2 Dex, +2 Con, –2 Str, –2 Cha. **Size**: Small. **Speed**: 30 ft. (faster than standard gnomes).
- **Low-light vision. Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Weapon Familiarity**: Gnome hooked hammers as martial.
- **+1 attack vs. kobolds and goblinoids. +4 dodge AC vs. giants.**
- **+4 Hide and Move Silently. +2 Listen and Spot.**
- **Spell-Like Abilities**: 1/day — *silence* (self-centered). If Cha 10+: 1/day each — *ghost sound*, *mage hand*, *message* (CL 1st).
- **Favored class**: Rogue. **Languages**: Common, Gnome (bonus: Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Orc, Goblin).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Excellent city rogue. Their *silence* ability is invaluable to any party. Gravitate toward the Delver's Guild from wherever gnomes cluster in the city.

---

### Feral Gargun
Goliath offshoots from the arctic north — leaner, with natural claws and cold resistance.
*(RoS, p. 88)*

- **Stats**: +4 Str, +4 Con. **Type**: Monstrous Humanoid. **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Cold Resistance 5.** **2 claws** (1d6 each). **Powerful Build** (at monster class 3rd level).
- **Goliath Blood**: Counts as goliath for all racial effects.
- **Favored class**: Barbarian. **Languages**: Gol-Kaa, Common (bonus: Giant, Goblin, Orc).
- **LA**: +2. **ECL**: 3 (or via 4-level monster class).
- **In Ptolus**: Even rarer than goliaths. A feral gargun would be from the extreme northern reaches. Their claws draw immediate attention in city crowds.

---

### Stonechild
The offspring of earth elementals and humanoids — massively built, acid/poison immune, alien in temperament.
*(RoS, p. 92)*

- **Stats**: +8 Str, +8 Con, +2 Int, –2 Cha (full). Monster class builds up gradually.
- **Type**: Outsider (Earth, Extraplanar). **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.** **Immunity to acid and poison.** **+4 natural armor.**
- **Magic Stone (Sp)**: 3×/day (CL 3rd).
- **Favored class**: Fighter. **Languages**: Common, Terran (bonus: Celestial, Dwarven, Ignan).
- **LA**: +4. **ECL**: 6 (+ 2 racial HD). Use 6-level monster class to play from 1st level.
- **In Ptolus**: Resembles an animated stone statue more than a person. One might have surfaced from the deep earth beneath the Dungeon, curious about this city built atop their domain. Very high ECL — best joining a higher-level party.

## Races of the Dragon

*Source: Races of the Dragon (2005)*

---

### Dragonborn of Bahamut
Not a separate race but a *transformation* — any race can undergo the Rite of Rebirth, renouncing their old form to be reborn as a servant of Bahamut. Retains original type/HD/LA; loses most racial traits; gains dragonborn traits.
*(RotD, p. 5)*

- **Stats gained**: +2 Con, –2 Dex (added to original racial modifiers).
- **Gains dragonblood subtype.** **+2 dodge AC vs. dragons.** **Immune to dragon frightful presence.**
- **Choose one Aspect**: Wings (glide always; fly 30 ft at 6 HD, improving through 15 HD), Resistance (energy resistance 5 scales up), or Senses (low-light + darkvision scaling 30→120 ft, blindsense at 15 HD).
- **Favored class**: Fighter (+ original retained). **Languages**: Adds Draconic; retains all original.
- **LA**: Retain original. **ECL**: Depends on original race (a dragonborn human = ECL 1).
- **In Ptolus**: Pilgrims from outside Ptolus, servants of Bahamut (map to a Lawful Good Ptolus deity or leave as foreign faith). Their draconic appearance draws considerable attention. Very rare.

---

### Spellscale
A dragon-descended humanoid race — scaled, social, impulsive, and infused with draconic arcane energy. Each day they meditate to choose a different blood quickening benefit.
*(RotD, p. 21)*

- **Stats**: +2 Cha, –2 Con. **Type**: Humanoid (dragonblood). Counts as dragon for racial effects.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft. **Low-light vision.**
- **Blood Quickening**: 10-minute daily meditation; choose one benefit from 20+ options (e.g., +1 to all save DCs, +4 on a skill, +2 Str, immunity to an effect, etc.). Represents unpredictable draconic blood.
- **Favored class**: Sorcerer. **Languages**: Common, Draconic (bonus: Dwarven, Elven, Gnome, Halfling).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Would stand out but not alarm — the Mages' Guild has seen stranger. Their social nature makes them excellent networkers. A spellscale exploring the Dungeon's draconic history (ancient dragon lairs rumored far below) is a natural PC motivation.

---

### Kobold
*(PHB/MM base stats; RotD expands significantly with feats, spells, and the Dragonwrought variant.)*
*(RotD, p. 37)*

- **Stats**: +2 Dex, –4 Str, –2 Con. **Type**: Humanoid (dragonblood, reptilian). Counts as dragon for racial effects.
- **Size**: Small. **Speed**: 30 ft. **Darkvision 60 ft.** **+1 natural armor.**
- **Light Sensitivity**: Dazzled in bright sunlight or *daylight*.
- **Craft (trapmaking)** always a class skill. **+2 on Craft (trapmaking), Profession (miner), Search.**
- **Favored class**: Sorcerer. **Languages**: Draconic (bonus: Common, Undercommon).
- **LA**: –1 (treat as +0 for PC play). **ECL**: 1.
- **Dragonwrought variant** (feat): Treated as true dragon for racial effects; lives 10× longer. Best as NPC.
- **In Ptolus**: Kobolds live in the Dungeon in substantial numbers. A PC kobold is a Dungeon-born survivor who made it to the surface — navigating city life with a chip on their shoulder and exceptional trap-sense.

## Races of the Wild

*Source: Races of the Wild (2005)*

### Raptoran
A race of winged humanoids — feathered, powerful-legged, ancient bargainers with elemental air.
*(RotW, p. 65)*

- **Stats**: None. **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft. **Low-light vision.**
- **+2 Climb and Spot.** **Weapon Familiarity**: Footbow as martial.
- **Gliding (Ex)**: Always active — negates all falling damage, 20 ft forward/5 ft descent. No gliding under medium/heavy load.
- **Flight (Ex)**: At **5 HD**, fly 40 ft (average). Con-modifier rounds before fatigue; 10 min/day total. At **10 HD**, fly without limit.
- **Dive Attack**: Charge while flying (min 30 ft, 10 ft descent) with piercing weapon for ×2 damage.
- **Pact with Wind Lords**: +1 CL on air-descriptor spells.
- **Unerring Direction**: Always knows which direction is north (non-functional on other planes).
- **Favored class**: Cleric. **Languages**: Common, Tuilvilanuue (bonus: Draconic, Elven, Gnome, Goblin, Orc, Sylvan).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Essentially unknown in the city. One visiting would be treated with wide-eyed curiosity. The Spire might draw a raptoran's attention as a potential roost. Their flight becomes a real asset in the deep Dungeon's open chasms.

### Killoren
Ancient fey beings who are living embodiments of wild nature — appearing like half-elves but fey-typed and immune to humanoid-targeting spells.
*(RotW, p. 91)*

- **Stats**: None. **Type**: Fey (no racial HD — gains HD only through class levels). Immune to *charm person*, *hold person*, etc.
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft. **Low-light vision.**
- **Immunity to sleep; +2 saves vs. enchantment.**
- **+2 Handle Animal and Survival.**
- **Cold Iron Anathema**: –2 attack rolls with cold iron weapons.
- **Manifest Nature's Might (Su)**: Each dawn choose one aspect for the day:
  - *Ancient*: +HD bonus on Knowledge (nature); extra +2 vs. enchantment.
  - *Destroyer*: 1×/hour (max Cha mod/day, min 1) — smite attack vs. aberrations/constructs/humanoids/oozes/outsiders/undead: +Cha mod to attack, +1 damage/HD.
  - *Hunter*: +2 Hide, Listen, Move Silently, Spot; +2 Initiative.
- **Favored class**: Druid. **Languages**: Common, Sylvan (bonus: Aquan, Auran, Elven, Gnome, Ignan, Terran).
- **LA**: +0. **ECL**: 1.
- **In Ptolus**: Perhaps the most exotic race that could plausibly appear. One driven toward the city by the Dungeon's corruption — their Destroyer aspect smites aberrations and undead. Their immunity to humanoid-targeting magic confuses assassins and guards alike.

### Catfolk
Nomadic feline humanoids of the open plains — quick, passionate, and emotionally intense.
*(RotW, p. 91)*

- **Stats**: +4 Dex, +2 Cha. **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 40 ft. **Low-light vision.**
- **+2 Listen and Move Silently.** **+1 natural armor.**
- **Favored class**: Ranger. **Languages**: Common, Feline (bonus: Draconic, Gnoll, Halfling, Sylvan).
- **LA**: +1. **ECL**: 2.
- **In Ptolus**: As rare as litorians. Can be confused with litorians at a glance; cultural differences are significant. A catfolk scout on their first trip to a major city is a solid PC origin via the Delver's Guild.

### Gnoll (as PC race)
*(Races of the Wild presents full playable stats.)*
*(RotW, p. 95)*

- **Stats**: +4 Str, +2 Con, –2 Int, –2 Cha. **Racial HD**: 2d8 (BAB +1, Fort +3/Ref +0/Will +0).
- **Size**: Medium. **Speed**: 30 ft. **Darkvision 60 ft.** **+1 natural armor.**
- **Favored class**: Ranger. **Languages**: Gnoll (bonus: Common, Draconic, Elven, Goblin, Orc).

- **In Ptolus**: Dungeon denizens, not city residents. A PC gnoll is an unusual exile from their pack — one who learned enough Common to trade with surface folk and found the city offered better opportunities than the wilderness. The Brotherhood of Redemption has worked with gnolls before.

## Racial Variants and Substitution Levels

*Source: Races of the Wild (2005), Races of Stone (2004), Races of Destiny (2004)*

A racial substitution level replaces one standard class level at a specific level. You permanently give up that level's standard class features and gain the substitution features instead. You can pick and choose which substitution levels to take.

---

### Elf Substitution Levels *(Races of the Wild, p. 155)*

| Class | Level | What's Replaced | What's Gained |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paladin | 1st | Smite evil (melee only) | *Ranged Smite Evil* — smite applies to bows within 30 ft |
| Paladin | 3rd | Aura of courage | *Aura of Freedom* — +4 morale vs. enchantments for allies within 10 ft |
| Paladin | 5th | Special mount | *Unicorn Mount* — unicorn serves as mount (treated 6 levels lower for mount stats) |
| Ranger | 1st | Track feat | *Elven Senses* — enhanced Listen/Spot; upgrades automatic Search near secret doors |
| Ranger | 4th | Animal companion | *Elven Archer* — bonus on ranged attacks in natural terrain |
| Ranger | 9th | Evasion | *Blade of the Forest* — bonus attacks when dual-wielding elven weapons |
| Wizard | 1st | Scribe Scroll feat | *Elven Spell Versatility* — swap one memorized spell for another of same level 1/day |
| Wizard | 5th | Bonus feat | *Ancient Lore* — bardic knowledge using Int mod + wizard levels |
| Wizard | 10th | Bonus feat | *Elven Spell Recall* — recover one expended spell/day |
