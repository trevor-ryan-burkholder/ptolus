# Prestige Classes — Supplemental (CA / CAdv / CS / PHB2)

Sources: *Complete Arcane* (CA), *Complete Adventurer* (CAdv), *Complete Scoundrel* (CS), *Player's Handbook II* (PHB2).

Format: **Requirements → HD / Levels / BAB / Saves → Key abilities → Ptolus note.**
Saves listed as F/R/W (G = good, P = poor). BAB: F = full, 3/4, 1/2.

---

## Complete Arcane Prestige Classes

### Acolyte of the Skin (CA p.25)
**Requirements:** BAB +3, Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 4, ability to cast 2nd-level arcane spells, must undergo ritual bonding with a demon skin.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Skin transformation (gradual demonic appearance), DR 3/magic → 5/magic → 5/good, fire resistance 5 → 10 → 20, Str +2 at 2nd, +2 more at 4th, telepathy 100 ft. (5th). Counts as outsider for spells.
**Ptolus:** A grotesque path favored by those trafficking with the Cults of Chaos; the Brotherhood of Redemption knows the ritual and hunts those who have undergone it. The bonded skin cannot be removed except by *wish* or *miracle*.

### Alienist (CA p.28)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (the planes) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells, must have contacted an alien intelligence.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Alien contact (1/day commune with Far Realm entity), pseudonatural familiar (template applied), alien blessing (resist compulsion), metamorphic touch (1/day polymorph self), alien mind (immunity to mind-affecting at 5th). Summons only pseudonatural creatures.
**Ptolus:** The deep levels of the Dungeon brush the Far Realm; alienists have been drawn to Ptolus since the Spire appeared. The Dreaming Apothecary is rumored to deal in reagents that accelerate the transformation.

### Argent Savant (CA p.30)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 5, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells including at least one force spell.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Force specialization (+1 CL for force spells), force armor (AC bonus scales 1-5), force blade (1d6+1 force melee), deflect missiles (as feat), maximize force (1/day free maximize on force spell at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Practitioners prize the certainty of force damage in a city where magic resistance is common among the Dungeon's deeper denizens. The Mages' Society has several members who have pursued this path.

### Blood Magus (CA p.32)
**Requirements:** Concentration 9, Knowledge (arcana) 5, Spellcraft 9, ability to cast 4th-level arcane spells, must have died and been restored.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Sanguine casting (use HP as substitute spell slots), scribe tattoo (permanent spell storage in skin), spell link (share spell effects through touch), blood to iron (harden skin, DR 1/- to 5/-), Tough as Iron (damage reduction stacks with magic items at 5th).
**Ptolus:** The resurrection houses of Ptolus occasionally see those who return changed; a blood magus usually hides the practice. Widely regarded as flirting with chaos magic.

### Effigy Master (CA p.35)
**Requirements:** Craft (any) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 4th-level arcane spells, must have created a construct.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Effigy (creates living wood/clay/stone construct companions), improved effigy (better HD and abilities with level), animate effigy (full action), imbue (grant spell effects to effigy), master craft (reduce GP/XP cost of constructs by 25% then 50%).
**Ptolus:** Construct-makers find work with both the Sages' Guild and the city guard; an effigy master could command a small force of constructed allies deep in the Dungeon without risking living companions.

### Elemental Savant (CA p.37)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Knowledge (planes) 2, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level spells of the chosen element.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Choose one element (fire/cold/acid/electricity/sonic). Minor/major/extreme resistance to immunity at 6th; +1 CL per 2 levels for chosen element; energy substitution (chosen element, free); elemental turning (as cleric turns undead but turns elementals of opposing element); at 10th, counts as elemental subtype.
**Ptolus:** Fire and cold savants are most common in Ptolus; the Dungeon's volcanic lower levels make fire immunity particularly valuable. The Inverted Pyramid accepts elemental savants as adjunct researchers.

### Enlightened Fist (CA p.39)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Improved Unarmed Strike, Still Spell, Knowledge (arcana) 4, ability to cast 2nd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** AC bonus (Wis to AC while unarmored, +1/5 levels); unarmed damage progression; arcane strike (deliver touch spell through unarmed strike as swift action); flurry of blows progression; Ki strike (magic/lawful/adamantine); spell power (+1 CL at 5th, +2 at 10th); empty strike (spell persists if foe immune at 10th).
**Ptolus:** A rare path combining monastic discipline with arcane study; practitioners sometimes emerge from Ptolus's martial arts monasteries. The Inverted Pyramid debates whether the class is philosophically coherent.

### Fatespinner (CA p.41)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells, Luck domain or ability to cast a luck-descriptor spell.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spin fate (1/day add or subtract 1d6 from any d20 roll within 30 ft. as immediate action); fickle finger (1/day force reroll on critical hit or threat within 30 ft.); deny fate (1/day negate a critical hit against you); resist fate (1/day +4 resistance on one save as immediate action); seize the moment (1/day auto-confirm critical hit).
**Ptolus:** Devotees of Asche (god of death and fate) sometimes walk this path, as do gamblers in the Guildsman District. Seeing a fatespinner at the gaming tables is considered very bad luck.

### Geometer (CA p.44)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spell glyph (inscribe a prepared spell into a geometric figure; cast later as move action); analyze glyph (read any spell glyph or magic circle as move action); greater spell glyph (holds up to 4th level at 3rd, 6th at 5th); perfect glyph (holds any level spell; cannot be dispelled except by *Mordenkainen's disjunction* at 5th).
**Ptolus:** The patterned floors of the Dungeon's ancient chambers fascinate geometers; several have theorized the entire Dungeon complex is one vast unfinished glyph. The Sages' Guild has paid well for translations.

### Green Star Adept (CA p.46)
**Requirements:** Craft (gemcutting or stonecutting) 5, Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 4th-level arcane spells, exposure to starmetal (green star fragment).
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Starmetal touch (at will create green light); construct traits gained step by step (low-light vision, darkvision, immunity to poison/disease/sleep/paralysis/stun/critical hits/mind effects, no need to eat/breathe, no need to sleep); DR 5/adamantine at 8th; at 10th fully counts as construct.
**Ptolus:** Green star fragments occasionally surface in the Dungeon's deepest veins. Adepts who complete the transformation cannot enter the Temple of Asche without disrupting services.

### Initiate of the Sevenfold Veil (CA p.48)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 10, Knowledge (planes) 5, Spellcraft 12, Spell Focus (abjuration), ability to cast 5th-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 7 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Warding (standard action; create a colored veil — red=fire 20, orange=acid 20, yellow=electricity 20, green=poison/death, blue=petrification, indigo=insanity, violet=force; each improves with level); prismatic veil (free action, all 7 veils at once at 7th); ray veil (extend veil as ranged touch attack).
**Ptolus:** The Inverted Pyramid quietly recruits those with the aptitude for the Veil's demanding scholarship; graduates are sometimes posted as wardens of the Spire approach roads.

### Mage of the Arcane Order (CA p.51)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 8, Cooperative Spell (CA feat), ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Guild membership (the Arcane Order); spell access (1/day request any spell from the guild's collective pool; guild may ask a favor); improved spell access (2/day at 3rd, 3/day at 5th); spell-sharing (lend prepared spells to guildmates); arcane might (+1 CL while other guild members adjacent).
**Ptolus:** The Ptolus equivalent is the Mages' Society; this PrC maps cleanly onto a senior Mages' Society researcher. The "guild pool" in Ptolus is roleplayed as the Society's vast library of exchange scrolls.

### Master Transmogrifist (CA p.53)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 4th-level arcane spells including at least one polymorph spell.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Extend form (double duration of polymorph spells); greater polymorph (+4 CL for polymorph subschool); perfect self (retain extraordinary abilities in polymorph form); a thousand faces (at will *alter self*); master of forms (retain all extraordinary abilities and class features in any polymorph form at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Several chaositech merchants have speculated about using transmogrifist techniques to stabilize chaos mutations. The Brotherhood of Redemption views the class with suspicion.

### Mindbender (CA p.56)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 4, ability to cast 1st-level enchantment (compulsion) [mind-affecting] arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 1 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Telepathy (100 ft. at will). A 1-level PrC purely for telepathy access; most characters take 1 level and return to their base class.
**Ptolus:** A single dip popular with beguilers and enchanters operating in Ptolus's criminal underworld. The Balacazar and Killraven organizations prize telepathic agents. Mechanically one of the cheapest PrC investments in the game.

### Seeker of the Song (CA p.58)
**Requirements:** Perform (any) 10, Knowledge (arcana) 4, Spellcraft 4, bardic music class feature, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Singing the song (replaces bardic music; grants +1 competence on attacks/saves/skill checks at 1st scaling to +5 at 10th; affects all allies within 60 ft.); imbue with song (infuse weapon with bonus damage); dragonshout (1/day sonic damage burst as swift action); reverie (heal 1d8+level HP/round for 3 rounds at 9th).
**Ptolus:** Bards drawn to the mystery of ancient Imperial music often walk this path; some seek the "original song" said to have preceded the gods' creation of the world.

### Sublime Chord (CA p.61)
**Requirements:** Perform (any) 12, Spellcraft 6, bardic music class feature, ability to spontaneously cast 2nd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 2 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spells (add to known spells list as a sorcerer casting 6th-level spells at 1st; 9th-level at 2nd; effectively grants late-progression high-level spells to bards). Casting uses Cha-based spellcasting.
**Ptolus:** Any bard wanting 6th-9th level spells will eventually look at this 2-level dip. In Ptolus, sublime chords of sufficient power attract attention from the Inverted Pyramid who consider powerful spontaneous spellcasters a resource.

### Suel Arcanamach (CA p.63)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, Knowledge (arcana) 6, Spellcraft 5, ability to cast 1st-level arcane spells, proficiency with at least one martial weapon.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Arcane defense (add Int bonus to AC while in light or no armor); spellblade (deliver touch spells through weapon strike); ignore arcane failure 10% at 1st rising to 30% at 5th and full heavy armor at 8th; mobile casting (no AoO from casting at 6th); spell-edge (+1 damage per spell level of spell stored in weapon); superior spell combat (full attack + cast at 10th).
**Ptolus:** Warrior-mages who need both close-quarters combat and arcane flexibility; in Ptolus these often serve as Dungeon delvers. Knights of the Pale respect those who have dedicated their power to anti-undead work.

### Wayfarer Guide (CA p.65)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 8, Knowledge (planes) 4, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast *dimension door* or *teleport*.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spatial sense (cannot be lost; always knows cardinal directions); safe transport (no mishap on teleport in home city); improved transport (+2 CL for teleport subschool); dimension jumper (teleport as move action 1/day rising to 3/day at 5th); mass transport (all willing creatures in contact teleport with you, no limit at 5th).
**Ptolus:** The best wayfarer guides operate as elite couriers and evacuation specialists; the Ptolus city guard has contracted several for rapid-response deployment.

### Wild Mage (CA p.67)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 5, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Wild magic surge (each spell has 1%/CL chance of triggering a wild surge from the Wild Magic table — double power, target switch, random effect, etc.); random spell (+1 CL but random chance of altering spell); tempt fate (deliberately trigger surge for higher power); chaotic surge (5th, choose to trigger a surge once/day for guaranteed +3 CL); spell trigger (10th, trigger surges in enemy spellcasters' spells).
**Ptolus:** Wild mages are simultaneously feared and fascinating in Ptolus; the Inverted Pyramid quietly tracks them as potential hazards. Chaositech merchants have theorized that wild mages and chaositech resonate poorly with each other.

## Complete Adventurer Prestige Classes

### Animal Lord (CAdv p.33)
**Requirements:** Handle Animal 8, Knowledge (nature) 5, Survival 5, wild empathy class feature, animal companion class feature, neutral component in alignment.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Choose animal type (feline, raptor, serpent, etc.); speak with type (at will); full animal companion progression; empathic link (1 mile); summon type (1/day call 1d3 examples); share types (type grants basic immunities); lord of type (chosen animals will not attack you at 10th).
**Ptolus:** Rare in an urban setting but animal lords of rats and ravens are not unknown in Ptolus's sewers and rooftops. Druids of Ptolus's outskirts sometimes pursue this path.

### Beastmaster (CAdv p.35)
**Requirements:** Handle Animal 5, Survival 2, must have 2 or more animal companions simultaneously.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Additional animal companions (up to 3 at 1st, 4 at 3rd, 5 at 5th); improved empathic link; share spells with all companions simultaneously; fast track companion (reduce training time by half).
**Ptolus:** Rangers and druids operating in the Ptolus wilderness occasionally pursue this path. A beastmaster with trained dire rat companions is a significant asset in the sewers.

### Bloodhound (CAdv p.37)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Gather Information 5, Survival 8, Track feat, must have successfully tracked and captured a fugitive.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Mark quarry (declare a target; +4 competence on Gather Info/Survival/Sense Motive vs. that target; can track by scent vs. marked quarry); tenacious pursuit (+10 speed chasing marked quarry); swift tracker; master hunter (1/day auto-confirm critical vs. marked quarry); relentless (immune to demoralization by marked quarry).
**Ptolus:** Bounty hunters working for the city guard or the Balacazar crime family both prize this PrC; Ptolus's layered districts and sewer connections make tracking expertise essential.

### Daggerspell Mage (CAdv p.39)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Concentration 4, Knowledge (arcana) 4, Two-Weapon Fighting, Weapon Focus (dagger), ability to cast 1st-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Daggerspell strike (deliver touch spell through dagger attack when TWF); daggercast (cast any spell with somatic component while holding daggers, no penalty); improved daggerspell strike (deliver two touch spells, one per dagger, at 5th); arcane fury (additional dagger attack as part of casting a spell at 9th).
**Ptolus:** Popular with arcane thieves and Dungeon delvers who need both melee capability and spellcasting. Several members of minor Ptolus adventuring companies are daggerspell mages.

### Daggerspell Shaper (CAdv p.42)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Knowledge (nature) 4, Two-Weapon Fighting, Weapon Focus (dagger), wild shape class feature.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Daggerspell wild shape (retain dagger proficiency in animal form; daggers meld into form as natural weapons at equal damage); wild strike (deliver a spell through natural attacks in wild shape); nature sense; improved wild shape progression.
**Ptolus:** Druids of the outskirts who double as assassins have explored this path. The combination of wild shape infiltration and dagger assassination is valued by very specific employers.

### Dread Pirate (CAdv p.44)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Bluff 5, Intimidate 5, Profession (sailor) 4, Speak Language (any 2), must have participated in at least 3 ship raids.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Fearsome reputation (1/week, Intimidate check replaces combat against NPC group); seamanship bonus on Profession sailor/Balance/Climb; swashbuckling (add Cha bonus to Reflex saves); motivate crew (+morale on crew skill checks); take the helm (use own skills in place of crew at 9th).
**Ptolus:** Ptolus's harbor sees significant traffic and piracy; dread pirates occasionally come ashore. A retired pirate makes for a compelling NPC patron or rival in a city campaign.

### Dungeon Delver (CAdv p.46)
**Requirements:** Disable Device 6, Knowledge (dungeoneering) 6, Search 6, Survival 4, trapfinding class feature.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Stonecunning (as dwarf; stacks if already have it); improved stonecunning (notice secret doors within 10 ft. passively); trap sense +2 (stacks with existing); swift search (Search for traps as move action); uncanny dodge (if not already possessed); trap mastery (1/day retry a failed Disable Device with +5 bonus).
**Ptolus:** One of the most fitting prestige classes for Ptolus specifically. The Dungeon below the city is the perfect training ground and working environment for dungeon delvers. Virtually every serious Dungeon exploration company has at least one.

### Exemplar (CAdv p.48)
**Requirements:** 8 or more ranks in any two skills.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Skill artistry (for two chosen skills, take 10 even when threatened; use class features while distracted); skill mastery (no retry limit); persuasive performance (use chosen skill in place of Diplomacy 1/day); swift ability (use chosen skill as swift action 3/day); inspired ability (1/day add HD to a chosen skill check result).
**Ptolus:** Underrated for skill-heavy characters operating in Ptolus's social environment; a rogue exemplar with Bluff and Gather Information can acquire intelligence on any faction in the city.

### Fochlucan Lyrist (CAdv p.50)
**Requirements:** Perform (string) 13, Knowledge (nature) 6, Speak Language (Sylvan), bardic music class feature, ability to cast 2nd-level arcane AND 2nd-level divine spells simultaneously, sneak attack or skirmish +2d6.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spells (advance both arcane and divine casting simultaneously at all 10 levels); bardic music progression; nature's gift (shapechange into woodland animals 1/day at 6th, 2/day at 10th); music of nature (use bardic music to affect animals and plants).
**Ptolus:** Demanding entry requirements make this rare in an urban campaign; better suited to campaigns with a wilderness component. A traveling lyrist passing through Ptolus makes a memorable NPC.

### Ghost-Faced Killer (CAdv p.52)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, Bluff 5, Hide 5, Move Silently 5, Intimidate 5, Stunning Fist or ki strike class feature, any evil alignment.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Frightful strike (on melee hit, Will save DC 10+level+Cha or shaken 1 round; failure by 5+ = frightened; by 10+ = panicked); ghostly defense (become incorporeal 1 round, 1/day to 3/day); unseen (always have cover for Stealth checks); death attack (as assassin, but 2-round study); vanish (30 ft. teleport as move action 1/day at 9th).
**Ptolus:** A natural for Ptolus's criminal underworld; the Killraven Crime League employs ghost-faced killers for high-priority eliminations. NPCs from this class make powerful one-scene antagonists.

### Highland Stalker (CAdv p.55)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Hide 9, Move Silently 9, Survival 4, Track feat, must have lived outdoors for at least 1 year.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Favored terrain (bonus on Survival/Hide/Move Silently/Listen/Spot/Initiative in specific terrain); additional favored terrain (3rd, 7th); camouflage (as ranger); terrifying presence (Intimidate 1/day to cause fear in favored terrain); improved favored terrain (double all bonuses at 9th).
**Ptolus:** Rangers who split time between Ptolus and the surrounding wilderness may pursue this; favored terrain "underground" makes highland stalkers useful in the Dungeon.

### Maester (CAdv p.57)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 6, Spellcraft 9, bardic knowledge class feature, ability to cast 3rd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Bardic knowledge enhancement (+1/2 Maester level to bardic knowledge checks); lore mastery (all Knowledge checks treated as trained at 3rd); research (reduce research time by half at 5th); master lore (auto-succeed on bardic knowledge checks DC 25 or lower at 5th).
**Ptolus:** An excellent fit for Ptolus's lore-heavy environment; a maester bard is the go-to resource on city history, Spire mythology, and dungeon geography. The Sages' Guild funds several maester researchers.

### Master of Many Forms (CAdv p.58)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (nature) 5, Survival 5, wild shape class feature, can wild shape into at least Small and Large size.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Shifter's speech (speak in any form); fast wild shape (wild shape as move action); improved wild shape progression (Tiny/Huge/monstrous forms by 4th, plant/vermin by 6th, magical beast by 8th, dragon by 10th); mighty wild shape (+2 natural armor in wild shape at 5th, +4 at 8th); a thousand faces (at will *alter self*); extraordinary wild shape (retain extraordinary abilities of original form).
**Ptolus:** Druids who push into shapeshifting beyond core wild shape limits will find this compelling; a master of many forms can assume dragon form in the deepest Dungeon levels.

### Nightsong Enforcer (CAdv p.60)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Intimidate 8, Move Silently 5, sneak attack +2d6, must be affiliated with a criminal organization.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Frightening sneak attack (sneak attack target also makes Will DC 10+level+Str mod or is shaken 1d4 rounds); brutal strike (add a melee attack to an Intimidate check as standard action); powerful build (treat as one size larger for grapple/bull rush/overrun); pain mastery (+2 on Concentration checks; bonus vs. pain effects); terrify (Intimidate check to cow witnesses of a successful sneak attack, 1/day).
**Ptolus:** The Killraven Crime League and Balacazar organization both employ enforcers. An NPC nightsong enforcer is an excellent recurring threat who can be sent after the party for interfering with criminal operations.

### Nightsong Infiltrator (CAdv p.63)
**Requirements:** Disable Device 8, Hide 10, Move Silently 8, Open Lock 5, Tumble 5, trapfinding class feature, must be affiliated with a criminal organization.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Swift disable (Disable Device as move action at 1st, free action at 7th); bypass trap (move through own disabled traps freely); team infiltration (+2 competence to Hide/Move Silently for allies within 30 ft.); flawless stride; ghost step (move through occupied spaces without AoO at 5th); shadow step (short-range teleport between shadows 1/day at 9th).
**Ptolus:** The guild thieves of Ptolus regard nightsong infiltrators as the elite; the Pale Tower and the Guildsman District both have cells of infiltrators who handle high-security targets.

### Shadowbane Inquisitor (CAdv p.65)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (religion) 6, Knowledge (planes) 2, Sense Motive 6, smite evil or smite chaos class feature, good alignment, worships a good deity.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Smite evil progression; detect evil (at will); darkvision 60 ft.; consecrated presence (undead within 10 ft. take 1d6 holy/round); sacred resistance (SR 5+level vs. spells from evil/chaotic creatures); divine wrath (1/day, double damage bonus on a smite attack; +1d6 fire vs. undead).
**Ptolus:** A perfect fit for Ptolus; the Knights of the Pale and the Church of Lothian both produce shadowbane inquisitors who specialize in purging undead in the Necropolis or exposing Chaos Cult infiltrators. One of the most thematically resonant PrCs for Ptolus.

### Shadowbane Stalker (CAdv p.68)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Hide 8, Move Silently 6, Knowledge (religion) 4, sneak attack +2d6, smite evil or smite chaos class feature.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Sneak attack progression; smite evil progression; favored enemy (evil outsiders); hide in plain sight (in darkness); shadow step (30 ft. teleport through shadows, 1/day to 3/day); divine sneak (deal holy damage equal to sneak attack dice vs. evil creatures); executioner's eye (1/day auto-confirm a sneak attack critical hit).
**Ptolus:** The rogue-paladin hybrid; in Ptolus this is the "holy assassin" concept — servants of the Temple of Lothian or the Keepers of the Veil who deal with threats quietly.

### Tempest (CAdv p.70)
**Requirements:** BAB +6, Dodge, Mobility, Spring Attack, Two-Weapon Fighting, proficiency with two light weapons.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Tempest defense (add Dex to AC as dodge bonus vs. melee while wielding two weapons); two-weapon fighting improvements every 2 levels through Greater and off-hand mastery; uncanny dodge; defensive flurry (1/day make a full two-weapon attack as free action after a charge).
**Ptolus:** Two-weapon fighters seeking the pinnacle of the dual-wielding style; common among gladiators in the Ptolus arena. A tempest PC is a straightforward melee powerhouse.

## Complete Scoundrel Prestige Classes

### Avenging Executioner (CS p.37)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, Intimidate 8, sneak attack +2d6, Bleeding Strike (CS feat).
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Bloody reminder (sneak attack target makes Will DC 10+level+Str mod or becomes shaken 1 round/die); dreadful strike (at 3rd, frightened instead of shaken on natural 20); murderous eye (at 5th, execute shaken/frightened target — Fort DC 10+level+Str mod or die).
**Ptolus:** Top-tier assassins used for political elimination; the Killraven League has three known avenging executioners on retainer.

### Battle Trickster (CS p.40)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, 4 or more skill tricks, Tumble 9.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Bonus skill tricks (2 bonus skill tricks per level); tactical skill (1/day use a skill trick as free action instead of swift); extra use (1/day use a once-per-encounter skill trick twice); improved feinting (Bluff check to feint is free action at 3rd; at 5th, feint denies Dex to all attacks this round).
**Ptolus:** Rogues and scouts in Ptolus's gladiatorial circuit often pursue this. See `srd/rules/skill-tricks.md` for the full trick list.

### Cloaked Dancer (CS p.43)
**Requirements:** Perform (dance) 7, Concentration 4, Move Silently 4, bardic music class feature.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Distracting dance (move action; grant allies within 30 ft. a bonus on Concentration checks); concealing dance (swift action; give one ally 20% miss chance for 1 round, 3+Cha/day); combat dance (use Tumble in place of BAB for one melee attack 1/day at 3rd); devastating dance (all enemies within 30 ft. watching the dance make Will DC 15+Cha or be fascinated at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Bard-rogues who work the entertainment circuit in Noble Quarter venues are natural fits. A cloaked dancer is an excellent social infiltrator in Ptolus's class-conscious society.

### Combat Trapsmith (CS p.46)
**Requirements:** Craft (trapmaking) 8, Disable Device 6, trapfinding class feature.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Quick trapmaking (set a simple mechanical trap as standard action); combat trap (set a trap as immediate action, 1/day to 3/day; deals 1d6+level damage or applies a condition); quick disable (Disable Device as move action); improved quick disable (swift action at 4th); trap mastery (add Wis to trap DC at 5th).
**Ptolus:** A combat trapsmith can turn any Dungeon corridor into a killing zone. Useful for parties that frequently establish defensible positions deep in the Dungeon.

### Fortune's Friend (CS p.49)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 4, Sleight of Hand 6, luck bonus from any source (or a class feature involving luck).
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Lucky start (1/day reroll initiative, take better result); fool's luck (1/day +1 luck bonus on all rolls for 1 round as free action); tempt fate (roll a 1 to gain a luck token; roll a 20 to lose one); spend luck (spend a luck token for +2 luck on a roll); fortunate soul (1/day reroll a failed save at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Particularly evocative for characters devoted to Danar (luck aspects of the Ptolus pantheon). A fortune's friend who believes their luck is divine favor makes for an interesting philosophical character.

### Gray Guard (CS p.52)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Knowledge (religion) 2, lay on hands class feature, smite evil class feature, must have violated the paladin's code and been officially absolved.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Smite evil progression; lay on hands progression; grim smite (when smiting evil, deal sneak attack damage as well, even without flanking, as long as target is evil); censure evil (1/day Intimidate check vs. evil target as swift action to render them shaken); unsanctioned tactics (may use poisons, lies, and dirty tactics without losing class features).
**Ptolus:** A fallen-and-redeemed paladin granted a special dispensation; in Ptolus this maps to a Lothian priest-warrior permitted to operate undercover among the Chaos Cults. One of the most dramatically interesting PrCs for a Ptolus campaign with a paladin PC.

### Magical Trickster (CS p.55)
**Requirements:** Sleight of Hand 8, Use Magic Device 6, bardic knowledge or any metamagic feat, ability to cast 1st-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spellcasting advancement at each level; magical sleight (deliver a touch spell via Sleight of Hand instead of touch attack, as swift action); bait and switch (substitute one spell effect for another of same level, 1/day); metamagic larceny (when using UMD on a scroll, apply one metamagic feat you know at no extra slot cost, 1/day at 4th).
**Ptolus:** Arcane rogues and bard-tricksters who blend spellcasting with sleight of hand; very fitting for the charismatic thieves of the Ptolus underworld.

### Malconvoker (CS p.57)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (arcana) 4, Knowledge (planes) 8, Spellcraft 8, ability to cast *lesser planar ally* or *lesser planar binding*.
**HD:** d4 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Deceptive summons (lie to summoned evil creatures; +2 Diplomacy and +4 binding Concentration vs. them); extended summons (+2 rounds duration); greater deceptive summons (at 5th, successfully-deceived evil creatures gain +2 Str/Con for 24 hours); improved binding (add class level to Concentration for binding at 7th); master binder (summon two evil creatures simultaneously at 10th).
**Ptolus:** Using demons against demons is theologically controversial; the Church of Lothian considers malconvokers heretical while the Keepers of the Veil regard them as a necessary evil. A malconvoker PC must navigate Ptolus's religious politics carefully.

### Master of Masks (CS p.60)
**Requirements:** Bluff 9, Disguise 9, Perform (any) 4, must have worn at least 4 different types of masks in significant situations.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Mask persona (library of up to 2+level different personas, each keyed to a mask; while wearing a mask, gain class features of that persona — assassin, gladiator, harlequin, highwayman, minstrel, nobleman, savage, savant, undead masks each grant different abilities); quick change (switch masks as move action, then swift, then free); mask of the master (all benefits simultaneously for 1 round/day at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Espionage and infiltration in Ptolus's complex social strata make this class thematically ideal; the Keepers of the Veil and the Dreaming Apothecary both deal in masks.

### Mountebank (CS p.63)
**Requirements:** Bluff 9, Diplomacy 4, Sleight of Hand 4.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Beguiling bluff (Bluff check to feint as move action; target also loses Dex to AC for 1 round on success); fast feint (swift action at 3rd); double feint (two targets simultaneously at 5th); improved feinting (successful feint makes target flat-footed vs. spells for 1 round at 7th); master bluff (auto-counter any Sense Motive check at DC+5 bonus at 10th).
**Ptolus:** Con artists and charlatans are well-represented in Ptolus; the Guildsman District's markets are full of mountebanks. An NPC mountebank is an excellent recurring antagonist or unreliable ally.

### Psibond Agent (CS p.66)
**Requirements:** Knowledge (psionics) 4, Sense Motive 6, ability to manifest 1st-level psionic powers.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Psionic advancement; bond sense (detect psionically-linked creatures within 60 ft.); psionic scan (1/day detect surface thoughts within 60 ft., Will DC 12+Cha mod); empathic feedback (linked attacker takes 1d6+level psychic damage); mind screen (immunity to mind-reading while manifesting at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Psionics are not core to Ptolus but are supported via *Expanded Psionics Handbook*. Check with your table on psionics availability. Illithid settlements in the Dungeon's deep levels make psibond agents intriguing infiltrators.

### Spellwarp Sniper (CS p.68)
**Requirements:** Precise Shot, Spellcraft 9, ability to cast 3rd-level spells, ability to create effects with a line or cone area.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spellcasting advancement at each level; regeometry (convert a cone or line spell into a ray — single target, no save, full damage as ranged touch attack); distance shot (+30 ft. to range of regeometried spells); precision shot (apply sneak attack dice to regeometried spells if target is flat-footed or within 30 ft.); warp bolt (regeometry as swift action free of charge at 5th).
**Ptolus:** Arcane archers and sniper-mages who put area spells on single targets; a wizard/spellwarp sniper is an excellent long-range artillery platform in the Dungeon's corridors.

### Uncanny Trickster (CS p.71)
**Requirements:** 5 or more class features with limited uses per day, 6 or more skill tricks.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 3 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Spellcasting advancement or class feature advancement (advance most recent class at each level); extra use (one additional daily use of a chosen limited-use class feature per level); bonus skill tricks (1 per level).
**Ptolus:** A "capstone" PrC for multiclass characters who want to enhance flexibility across existing features rather than gain new ones. Short at 3 levels, it fits cleanly into complex builds.

## Player's Handbook II Prestige Classes

*Note: PHB2 is a scanned PDF — entries are from training knowledge. Verify page numbers and exact prerequisites against print before ruling on edge cases.*

### Abjurant Champion (PHB2 p.85)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, ability to cast 1st-level arcane spells, proficiency with any martial weapon.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Abjurant armor (abjuration spells granting armor bonus to AC scale with CL: +1/2 CL to existing bonus); extended abjuration (all abjuration spells double duration at no cost); swift abjuration (cast abjuration spells as swift actions at 4th); martial arcanist (CL for abjuration = BAB); abjurant champion (at 5th, CL for all arcane spells = BAB).
**Ptolus:** One of the most popular gish prestige classes in the system. Ptolus Dungeon delvers prize this because it keeps CL high for defensive magic despite BAB investment. The Knights of the Pale have members who pursue this path.

### Arcane Dilettante (PHB2 p.88)
**Requirements:** Spellcraft 8, bardic knowledge class feature, ability to spontaneously cast 2nd-level arcane spells.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** 1/2 | **Saves:** F:P / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spellcasting advancement; borrowed spell (1/day use a spell slot to replicate a spell seen cast within 24 hours — Spellcraft DC 15+spell level; success casts it at minimum CL); improved borrowed spell (at 3rd, +1/2 level to Spellcraft check); master dilettante (at 5th, extends the window to the last week).
**Ptolus:** Bards who want broader arcane access without full wizard training; fits the "scholar-about-town" bard archetype in Ptolus's intellectual culture.

### Blade Bravo (PHB2 p.90)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Bluff 4, Tumble 9, Quick Draw, must have fought in at least 3 duels.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Dazzling blow (maximum damage on a melee attack dazes target 1d4 rounds); dancing blade (add Cha bonus to melee attack rolls when not in heavy armor); uncanny dodge; baffling riposte (Intimidate check as free action when you dodge an attack — target shaken 1 round if check exceeds attack roll); flashy fighting (at 5th, once/round make Bluff check; if it beats target's Sense Motive, next attack deals sneak attack damage even without flanking).
**Ptolus:** The Noble Quarter dueling culture and the gladiatorial circuit both produce blade bravos. A natural fit for the charismatic rogue-fighter archetype common in Ptolus adventures.

### Champion of Corellon Larethian (PHB2 p.93)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, Knowledge (religion) 2, Weapon Focus (longsword or rapier or similar elven blade), must worship Corellon Larethian, must be elf or half-elf.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:P
**Key abilities:** Elven weapon training (free weapon focus in one additional elven weapon per 2 levels); arcane attack (1/day channel a prepared arcane spell through a melee hit, adding 1d6 force damage per spell level); defensive casting (cast defensively with no Concentration check, 1/day); divine grace (add Cha to saves); Corellon's gift (at 10th, once/week convert any melee hit into a critical hit, no confirmation needed).
**Ptolus:** Corellon Larethian maps to the elven aspect of the Ptolus/FR hybrid pantheon; adjust the deity requirement to fit your campaign setting. Elven fighter-mages from Evereska or similar enclaves might pursue this on arriving in Ptolus.

### Gnome Giant Slayer (PHB2 p.96)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, gnome, must have survived combat against a creature at least two size categories larger.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Giant slaying (bonus on attack and damage against Large or larger: +1/2 levels); defensive roll (once/day when attack would reduce you to 0 HP, Ref save DC = damage dealt; success halves it); giant's bane (once/combat, +Cha to damage against Large+ creature after hitting); giant slayer's strike (at 5th, confirmed critical vs. Large+ creature stuns it 1 round unless Fort save DC 10+level+Str).
**Ptolus:** A gnome Dungeon delver facing the Dungeon's large-scale guardians and giants of the lower levels finds this very useful. A gnome giant slayer is an entertaining character concept in a city where being small is usually a social disadvantage.

### Skullclan Hunter (PHB2 p.99)
**Requirements:** BAB +5, Knowledge (religion) 6, Survival 2, Track feat, must have killed an undead creature of CR 5 or higher.
**HD:** d10 | **Levels:** 5 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:P / W:G
**Key abilities:** Favored enemy undead (as ranger; if already have undead as favored enemy, +2 to existing bonus); detect undead (at will SLA); silver strike (all wielded weapons count as silver); ghost strike (all wielded weapons count as ghost touch); death ward (permanent *death ward* effect at 3rd); skull crusher (1/day auto-confirm a critical against undead at 5th).
**Ptolus:** One of the most thematically perfect prestige classes for Ptolus; the Necropolis looms over the city and the undead threat is ever-present. The Knights of the Pale sponsors this path.

### Unseen Seer (PHB2 p.102)
**Requirements:** Divination spell known or prepared at 2nd level or higher, sneak attack +1d6.
**HD:** d6 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** 3/4 | **Saves:** F:P / R:G / W:G
**Key abilities:** Spellcasting advancement at each level; augury mastery (divination spells last 50% longer; +1 CL for divination at 4th, +2 at 7th, +3 at 10th); advance sneak attack (+1d6 at 3rd, 6th, 9th); spot the weak point (1/day Spellcraft check to identify AC vulnerabilities; deal sneak attack damage on any hit vs. that target for 1 minute); see the unseen (continuous *true seeing* at 10th).
**Ptolus:** The ultimate intelligence agent in Ptolus's complex political environment. The Commissar's intelligence service is rumored to have several unseen seers on staff.

### War Chanter (PHB2 p.105)
**Requirements:** BAB +4, Perform (any) 9, bardic music class feature.
**HD:** d8 | **Levels:** 10 | **BAB:** F | **Saves:** F:G / R:G / W:P
**Key abilities:** Bardic music progression; inspiring blow (when you confirm a critical hit, activate a bardic music ability as swift action without expending a daily use); war chant (+1 on attack and damage to all allies within 30 ft. who can hear you, scaling by level); battlesong (war chant also grants +1 morale on Fortitude saves at 5th); call to arms (1/day grant all allies a bonus standard action as free action at start of their initiative at 9th).
**Ptolus:** Bard-fighters who serve as combat leaders; in Ptolus these serve with the city guard or as commanders in Dungeon expeditions. War chanters make excellent party-face-plus-fighter characters.
