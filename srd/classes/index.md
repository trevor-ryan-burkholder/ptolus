# Classes (3.5e PHB + Ptolus)

Quick reference for all 11 core PHB classes. For full descriptions, consult `sources/D&D Books 3.5/Core Books/`. Ptolus-specific classes (if used) go in this folder as separate files.

## Save and BAB progressions

| Progression | Level 1 | per level | At 20 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Good Save | +2 | +1 per 2 levels | +12 |
| Poor Save | +0 | +1 per 3 levels | +6 |
| Full BAB | +1 | +1 per level | +20 |
| 3/4 BAB | +0 | +3 per 4 levels | +15 |
| 1/2 BAB | +0 | +1 per 2 levels | +10 |

## Class summaries

### Barbarian
- **HD**: d12
- **BAB**: Full
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Skill points**: 4 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Strength, Constitution
- **Class skills**: Climb, Craft, Handle Animal, Intimidate, Jump, Listen, Ride, Survival, Swim
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Fast movement (+10 ft.), Rage 1/day (+4 Str/Con, +2 Will, –2 AC; 3+Con mod rounds)
  - 2: Uncanny dodge
  - 3: Trap sense +1 (scales every 3 levels)
  - 4: +1 rage/day
  - 5: Improved uncanny dodge
  - 7: Damage reduction 1/— (scales to 5/— at 19th)
  - 8: +1 rage/day
  - 11: Greater rage (+6 Str/Con, +3 Will while raging)
  - 12: +1 rage/day
  - 14: Indomitable will (+4 Will vs. enchantment while raging)
  - 16: +1 rage/day
  - 17: Tireless rage (no longer fatigued after rage)
  - 20: Mighty rage (+8 Str/Con, +4 Will while raging)
- **Alignment**: Any nonlawful.
- **In Ptolus**: Barbarians come largely from the nomadic tribes of Palastan, the Eastern Hordes, or the aram herds. In the city they find work as guards, pit-fighters, and delvers willing to take the front rank of any dungeon corridor.

### Bard
- **HD**: d6
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref good, Will good
- **Skill points**: 6 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Charisma, Dexterity
- **Spellcasting**: Spontaneous, Cha-based, up to 6th level.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Bardic music — Countersong, Fascinate, Inspire courage +1
  - 1: Bardic knowledge (1d20 + bard level + Int for lore checks)
  - 3: Inspire competence
  - 6: Suggestion, Inspire courage +2
  - 9: Inspire greatness
  - 12: Song of freedom, Inspire courage +3
  - 15: Inspire heroics
  - 18: Mass suggestion, Inspire courage +4
- **Alignment**: Any nonlawful.
- **In Ptolus**: Bards are everywhere in the city — working tavern stages on Tavern Row, serving as information brokers, or acting as the social face of adventuring parties. The Ghostly Minstrel is the unofficial bard headquarters; Tarin Ursalatao performs there nightly.

### Cleric
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Wisdom, Charisma (for turning)
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Diplomacy, Heal, Knowledge (arcana, history, religion, the planes), Profession, Spellcraft
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Wis-based, up to 9th level.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Two domains (each grants bonus spell/level + domain power), Turn/Rebuke Undead (3+Cha mod/day; d20+Cha mod vs. DC 10+target HD)
  - Spell slots scale 1–9 per level.
- **Alignment**: Within one step of deity. Domain access depends on deity.
- **In Ptolus**: Ptolus is lousy with clerics — Lothian has a massive presence (nine churches citywide), and dozens of other faiths maintain temples in the Temple District and beyond. Clerics of Lothian serve double-duty as city administrators and judges; clerics of other faiths often find themselves sidestepping Church politics.

### Druid
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 4 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Wisdom, Constitution
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Wis-based, up to 9th level.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Animal companion (scales in HD with druid), Nature sense (+2 Knowledge nature/Survival), Wild empathy (Cha-based Handle Animal vs. wild animals)
  - 2: Woodland stride (no difficult terrain in natural growth)
  - 3: Trackless step
  - 4: Resist nature's lure (+4 saves vs. fey spell-likes)
  - 5: Wild shape 1/day (Small or Medium animal; daily uses and size range scale every 2 levels)
  - 9: Wild shape 3/day, can become Large animal
  - 11: Wild shape 4/day, can become Tiny or Huge animal
  - 12: Wild shape 5/day
  - 13: A Thousand Faces (alter self at will)
  - 15: Wild shape 6/day, can become elemental 1/day
  - 16: Timeless body (no age penalties)
  - 18: Wild shape 6/day, elemental 3/day
- **Alignment**: Any neutral.
- **Forbidden weapons/armor**: No metal armor; loses spellcasting if worn.
- **In Ptolus**: Druids are unusual in a city this size. Most are affiliated with the Circle of Green (now mostly defunct) or nature cults that operate out of Rivergate and the city's edges. They're more common as visiting NPCs than as city residents.

### Fighter
- **HD**: d10
- **BAB**: Full
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Strength (melee), Dexterity (ranged), Constitution
- **Class skills**: Climb, Craft, Handle Animal, Intimidate, Jump, Ride, Swim
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Bonus combat feat
  - 2: Bonus combat feat (every even level thereafter, so 2/4/6/8…/20 = 11 total bonus feats)
  - 4: Can take Weapon Specialization (+2 damage) for any weapon you have Weapon Focus in
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **In Ptolus**: The most common class for hired swords, City Watch members, and dungeon-delving muscle. Fighters dominate the lower and mid-level NPC population of Ptolus; virtually every guard house, guild hall, and noble estate has one on payroll. Rastor's Weapons in Delver's Square caters heavily to fighter types.

### Monk
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref good, Will good
- **Skill points**: 4 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Wisdom, Strength, Dexterity
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Flurry of blows (extra attack at –2/–2 full attack), unarmed strike (1d6 at 1st, scales), Stunning Fist (free feat; 1/day per 4 levels), bonus feat (Improved Grapple or Combat Reflexes)
  - 2: Evasion, bonus feat (Deflect Arrows or Combat Reflexes)
  - 3: Still mind (+2 saves vs. enchantment), bonus feat (Improved Disarm or Improved Trip)
  - 4: Ki strike (magic), slow fall 20 ft., +10 ft. speed
  - 5: Purity of body (immune disease)
  - 6: Bonus feat, flurry improves
  - 7: Wholeness of body (heal own HP/day = 2× level)
  - 9: Improved evasion
  - 10: Ki strike (lawful), bonus feat
  - 11: Diamond body (immune poison)
  - 12: Abundant step (dimension door 1/day as move)
  - 13: Diamond soul (SR 10 + monk level)
  - 15: Quivering palm (1/week; save or die delayed)
  - 17: Timeless body, tongue of the sun and moon
  - 19: Empty body (etherealness 1 min/day)
  - 20: Perfect self (outsider type, DR 10/magic)
- **Alignment**: Any lawful.
- **Restrictions**: No armor or shields; multiclass restrictions (cannot return to monk once you leave, unless permitted by DM).
- **In Ptolus**: Monks are strongly associated with the Order of the Fist, a martial-arts order headquartered in Ptolus. The Sisterhood of Silence (Ptolus's secret police auxiliary) includes monk members. Harrow elves have a cultural affinity for the monk class.

### Paladin
- **HD**: d10
- **BAB**: Full
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Strength, Charisma
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Diplomacy, Handle Animal, Heal, Knowledge (nobility, religion), Profession, Ride, Sense Motive
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Wis-based, 1st–4th level (begins at 4th character level).
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Detect evil (at will), smite evil 1/day (+Cha to hit, +level to damage vs. evil), divine grace (+Cha mod to all saves), lay on hands (Cha mod × level HP/day)
  - 2: Aura of courage (immune to fear; allies within 10 ft. +4 vs. fear), divine health (immune to disease)
  - 3: Turn undead (as cleric of level –3)
  - 4: Spells, special mount (summon celestial warhorse; scales in HD)
  - 5: Smite evil 2/day
  - 10: Smite evil 3/day
  - 15: Smite evil 4/day
  - 20: Smite evil 5/day
- **Alignment**: Lawful good only.
- **Code of conduct**: Must remain LG; loses all class features if alignment shifts or code is violated. Associates with evil creatures only reluctantly.
- **In Ptolus**: Paladins serving Lothian are the most visible; the Church posts them as crusaders against the undead and the Chaos Cults. The Knights of the Pale are a knightly order composed heavily of paladins dedicated to fighting the undead of the Necropolis.

### Ranger
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: Full
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref good, Will poor
- **Skill points**: 6 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Strength or Dexterity, Wisdom
- **Class skills**: Climb, Concentration, Craft, Handle Animal, Heal, Hide, Jump, Knowledge (dungeoneering, geography, nature), Listen, Move Silently, Profession, Ride, Search, Spot, Survival, Swim, Use Rope
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Wis-based, 1st–4th level (begins at 4th character level).
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Favored enemy (+2 bonus on Bluff/Listen/Sense Motive/Spot/Survival/damage vs. chosen type; additional types and +2 to existing every 5 levels), track (as feat), wild empathy (as druid)
  - 2: Combat style (archery: Rapid Shot; two-weapon: TWF — no prerequisites required while wearing light or no armor)
  - 3: Endurance (as feat)
  - 4: Animal companion (as druid, but −3 effective druid level)
  - 6: Improved combat style
  - 7: Woodland stride
  - 8: Swift tracker (track at full speed without penalty)
  - 9: Evasion
  - 11: Combat style mastery
  - 13: Camouflage (Hide in natural terrain without cover/concealment)
  - 17: Hide in plain sight (natural terrain only)
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **In Ptolus**: Rangers who operate in Ptolus typically specialize in humanoid or undead favored enemies. Some work the sewers and take aberration as a favored enemy. The Dungeon delving companies prize rangers for their tracking, scouting, and combat flexibility.

### Rogue
- **HD**: d6
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref good, Will poor
- **Skill points**: 8 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Dexterity, Intelligence, Charisma
- **Class skills**: Appraise, Balance, Bluff, Climb, Craft, Decipher Script, Diplomacy, Disable Device, Disguise, Escape Artist, Forgery, Gather Information, Hide, Intimidate, Jump, Knowledge (local), Listen, Move Silently, Open Lock, Perform, Profession, Search, Sense Motive, Sleight of Hand, Spot, Swim, Tumble, Use Magic Device, Use Rope
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Sneak attack +1d6 (denied Dex or flanked; range limited to 30 ft. for ranged), trapfinding (find and disable magical traps; Search/Disable Device DC 20+)
  - 2: Evasion
  - 3: Trap sense +1 (scales +1 per 3 levels), sneak attack +2d6
  - 4: Uncanny dodge (no flanking denial; Dex to AC even flat-footed)
  - 5: Sneak attack +3d6
  - 6: Special ability (see below), sneak attack +3d6
  - 7: Sneak attack +4d6
  - 8: Improved uncanny dodge (requires 4+ rogue/ranger/barbarian levels to flank you)
  - 10: Special ability
  - Every odd level: +1d6 sneak attack (cap 10d6 at 19th)
  - Every 10 levels: Special ability
- **Special abilities** (pick one each at 10th, 13th, 16th, 19th): Crippling strike, defensive roll, improved evasion, opportunist, skill mastery, slippery mind, or any bonus feat from the rogue list.
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **In Ptolus**: The criminal underbelly of Ptolus is enormous. Rogues dominate the Balacazar organization, the Killraven League, and the many smaller gangs of Midtown and the Warrens. Honest rogues work as scouts for Dungeon expeditions, locksmiths, or information brokers. The Delver's Guild itself has a strong rogue contingent.

### Sorcerer
- **HD**: d4
- **BAB**: 1/2
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Charisma
- **Class skills**: Bluff, Concentration, Craft, Knowledge (arcana), Profession, Spellcraft
- **Spellcasting**: Spontaneous, Cha-based, up to 9th level. Slower spell acquisition than wizard; cannot swap spells freely. Fewer spells known (limited list) but unlimited uses of known spells per slot.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Familiar (as wizard — bonuses depend on type; share spells, empathic link, etc.)
  - Spell slots scale 1–9 per level; spells known: 2 at 1st, gaining 1–2 per level.
  - At 4th/8th/12th/16th/20th level, may swap one known spell for another of the same level.
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **In Ptolus**: Sorcerers with "wild" arcane bloodlines are viewed with suspicion by the Inverted Pyramid, which prefers the discipline of prepared casters but cannot ignore a sorcerer's raw power. Many sorcerers in Ptolus have extraplanar blood (tiefling or aasimar ancestry being most common); a few have draconic ties via the Ptolus-adjacent dragon cults.

---

### Wizard
- **HD**: d4
- **BAB**: 1/2
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Intelligence
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Decipher Script, Knowledge (all, taken separately), Profession, Spellcraft
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Int-based, up to 9th level. Spellbook required (100 pages; 1 page/spell level, min 1). Start with 3+Int bonus 1st-level spells; gain 2 free spells per level. Can scribe any spell found. Bonus feat: Scribe Scroll (free at 1st).
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Choose a school of specialization (optional; gain +1 spell/level of that school but lose two opposition schools) or remain generalist
  - 1: Familiar (as sorcerer)
  - 5/10/15/20: Bonus feat (metamagic or item creation or Spell Mastery)
  - Bonus spells/day from high Int score.
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **In Ptolus**: The Inverted Pyramid is the dominant arcane faction; membership is effectively required for any wizard wishing to practice openly in Ptolus. Wizards also serve the noble houses (as advisors), the Mages' Society (as researchers), and the Sages' Guild. The Balacazar organization employs several rogue wizards who prefer not to deal with Pyramid politics.

### Warmage (CA p.14)
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Alignment**: Any
- **Armor**: Light (at 1st), medium (at 8th) — no arcane spell failure in these armor types
- **Spellcasting**: Spontaneous, Int-based. Warmages know all warmage spells automatically (no spells known limit — entire list is available). Heavily weighted toward evocation with some abjuration and conjuration. Spells up to 9th level.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Armored mage (light armor, no ASF), warmage edge (add Int bonus to spell damage rolls for evocation spells)
  - 3: Advanced learning (add any evocation spell from the Sor/Wiz list to warmage list, 1/level at 3rd/6th/9th/12th)
  - 8: Armored mage (medium armor, no ASF)
  - Metamagic bonus feats: Empower Spell (3rd), Enlarge Spell (6th), Maximize Spell (9th), Widen Spell (12th); each can be applied spontaneously 1/day without increasing casting time
- **In Ptolus**: The warmage concept maps perfectly onto the Church of Lothian's crusader-mages and the city watch's arcane support units. Warmages are socially acceptable in Ptolus's military culture in ways that other arcane classes are not; the Inverted Pyramid grudgingly acknowledges their utility.

### Wu Jen (CA p.18)
- **HD**: d4
- **BAB**: 1/2
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int mod
- **Alignment**: Any nonlawful
- **Spellcasting**: Prepared, Int-based, up to 9th level. Spellbook (as wizard). Start with 3+Int bonus spells; gain 2/level free.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Taboos (must choose 2 behavioral restrictions — e.g., "never touch a dead body," "always wash hands before eating"; violating a taboo means losing all spells until penance); watchful spirit (1/day reroll initiative, take better result)
  - 3: Spell secret (apply one metamagic feat to one known spell permanently, at no extra spell slot cost; gain one more every 4 levels)
  - 5: Elemental mastery (for chosen element, ignore SR of creatures of that type)
  - Additional spell secrets at 7th, 11th, 15th, 19th
- **In Ptolus**: Wu jen are culturally associated with the Shoaln people (Ptolus's Far Eastern analogue) and are uncommon in the city proper. A wu jen PC is an outsider twice over — as an arcane practitioner and as a foreigner — which creates interesting social tension in Ptolus's rigid hierarchies.

### Spellthief (CAdv p.13)

- **Skill points**: 6 + Int mod
- **Alignment**: Any
- **Armor**: Light
- **Spellcasting**: Spontaneous, Cha-based, up to 4th level. Spells stolen (see below) are added to spells known. Normal spellthief list covers abjuration/divination/enchantment/illusion/transmutation.

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1st or lower | +1d6 | 1st level (2/day) |
| 2 | 1st or lower | +1d6 | — |

### Beguiler (PHB2 p.6)
- **HD**: d6
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 6 + Int mod
- **Alignment**: Any
- **Armor**: Light (no arcane spell failure in light armor — class feature)
- **Spellcasting**: Spontaneous, Int-based, up to 9th level. Beguilers know ALL beguiler spells automatically (no list limits). Beguiler spells are exclusively enchantment and illusion.
- **Key features by level**:
  - 1: Armored mage (light, no ASF), cloaked casting (+1 DC for enchantment/illusion spells if the target is unaware you're casting at them; +2 DC at 8th)
  - 2: Trapfinding
  - 3: Surprise casting (free action to cast after a successful feint, 1/day)
  - 4: Silent spell (free metamagic on one spell/day at no cost — chosen at time of casting)
  - 10: Still spell (free metamagic on one spell/day)
  - 14: Advanced learning (add one spell of any school to known spells, 1 per 4 levels from 4th)

### Spirit Shaman *(CD p.14)*
- **HD**: d8
- **BAB**: 3/4
- **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will good
- **Skill points**: 4 + Int mod
- **Primary abilities**: Charisma, Wisdom
- **Alignment**: Any.
- **Proficiencies**: Club, dagger, dart, quarterstaff, scimitar, sickle, shortspear, sling, spear; light and medium armor; no shields.

A spirit shaman who works *with* the ghosts of old Ptolus — perhaps communicating with victims of Ghul's ancient experiments — is an unusual and compelling character concept.
