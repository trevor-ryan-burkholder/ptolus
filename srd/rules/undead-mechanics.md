# Undead Mechanics Reference

_Sources: MM p.317 undead type, PHB p.159 turning, LM Ch.1 & Ch.6_

---

## Undead Type Traits

All undead have the following unless noted otherwise (LM p.81–82):

- **No Con score** — uses Cha modifier for Concentration checks
- **Darkvision 60 ft.**
- **Immunity:** mind-affecting spells/abilities, poison, magic sleep, paralysis, stunning, disease, death effects, extra damage from critical hits, nonlethal damage, damage to physical ability scores (Str/Dex/Con), ability drain, energy drain, fatigue, exhaustion, death from massive damage
- **Not at risk from massive damage** — destroyed at 0 hp, not merely dying
- **Cannot heal naturally** if no Intelligence score; negative energy heals them
- **No food, water, or sleep required**
- **Cannot be raised/reincarnated** — only resurrection/true resurrection (turns them back to living); willing undead only
- **Proficient:** natural weapons, simple weapons, any weapons in stat block
- **Armor proficiency** only as noted in stat block

---

## Energy Interactions

- **Negative energy heals undead.** Inflict spells, negative energy effects, and energy drain all heal (or benefit) undead.
- **Positive energy damages undead.** Cure spells, _heal_, _mass heal_, holy water all damage undead as if they healed an equal amount on a living creature.
- _Disrupt undead_: 1d6 ranged touch damage to undead
- _Holy water_: 2d4 damage on direct hit, 1 on splash (vs. undead only)
- Effects that don't use positive energy (some psionic healing) have no effect on undead either way.

## Turning / Rebuking Undead (PHB p.159)

### Turn Check
- Roll **d20 + cleric level + Cha modifier** (vs. table)
- The result determines the **highest HD** undead affected by the turning attempt
- Good clerics/paladins turn; evil clerics/blackguards rebuke

| Turn Check Result | Max HD Affected |
|---|---|
| 0 or lower | Cleric level – 4 |
| 1–3 | Cleric level – 3 |
| 4–6 | Cleric level – 2 |
| 7–9 | Cleric level – 1 |
| 10–12 | Cleric level |
| 13–15 | Cleric level + 1 |
| 16–18 | Cleric level + 2 |
| 19–21 | Cleric level + 3 |
| 22+ | Cleric level + 4 |

### Turning Damage
- **2d6 + cleric level + Cha modifier** = total HD of undead affected (closest first, fewest HD first)
- Affects undead within **60 ft.** that the cleric can see
- Turned undead flee for 10 rounds (or cower if they can't flee)

### Destroy / Command
- **Turning destroys** undead with HD ≤ (cleric level – 5) automatically (no separate check needed — the turning check covers it; if you can affect them and their HD is ≤ cleric level – 5, they're destroyed/commanded)
- Evil clerics who rebuke can **command** undead with HD ≤ (cleric level – 5)
- Alternatively stated: if the turning result would affect undead AND their HD < (cleric level – 4), they are destroyed (turned) or commanded (rebuked) outright

### Turn Resistance
- Some undead have **+X turn resistance** — add this bonus to their HD only for purposes of turn checks (not for turning damage)
- Example: a 4 HD wight with +2 turn resistance is treated as 6 HD when determining whether a turning attempt affects it

---

## Commanding Undead

- An evil cleric who successfully rebukes undead can **command** those of sufficiently low HD (≤ cleric level – 5)
- Maximum total HD of commanded undead = **2 × cleric level**
- This includes any undead created via animate dead
- A cleric may direct commanded undead as a free action

## Creating Undead (PHB / MM)

| Spell | Creates | Min. Caster Level |
|---|---|---|
| _Animate dead_ | Skeletons (any), Zombies (any) | Clr 3 / Wiz 4 |
| _Create undead_ | Ghoul (HD ≤ 4), Ghast (HD ≤ 4), Shadow (HD ≤ 7), Wraith (HD ≤ 7), Mummy (HD ≤ 11), Mohrg (HD ≤ 11) | Clr 6 |
| _Create greater undead_ | Shadows, Wraiths (any), Spectres (HD ≤ 11), Devourers (HD ≤ 11) | Clr 8 |

- _Animate dead_ control limit: **2 × caster level HD** total across all animated undead
- _Plague of undead_ (LM p.70): mass animate dead, up to **4 × caster level HD** per casting; same overall control limit as animate dead

## Incorporeal Subtype (LM p.82, MM p.310)

- **No physical body.** Immune to all nonmagical attacks.
- **50% miss chance** against incorporeal from any corporeal source (except: positive/negative energy, force effects like _magic missile_, ghost touch weapons)
- **Only harmed by:** other incorporeal creatures, magic weapons (+1 or better), spells/SLAs/supernatural abilities
- **No natural armor bonus** — instead has a **deflection bonus = Cha modifier** (minimum +1)
- **Attacks ignore** natural armor, armor, and shields; deflection bonuses and force effects (mage armor) work normally
- **Can pass through solid objects** but must stay adjacent to exterior; cannot pass through force effects
- Cannot trip, grapple, or be tripped/grappled
- Moves silently; nonvisual senses (scent, blindsight) partly ineffective
- Cannot fall or take falling damage
- _Ghost touch_ weapons and armor negate the 50% miss chance fully

---

## Swarms of Undead (LM p.82)

For turning purposes, treat undead swarms as having **half their actual HD.** Turn resistance adds after halving. Every 4 HD of affected undead = 1 swarm turned/destroyed.

---

## Positive Energy Resistance / Special Turning Vulnerabilities (LM Ch.2)

- Some undead have **positive energy resistance X** — subtract X from positive energy damage per attack (similar to energy resistance)
- **Improved Turn Resistance** feat: +2 to turn resistance beyond normal stat block value
- **Elemental undead** (necromentals, desiccators, cinderspawn, voidwraiths): a cleric who can also turn that creature's elemental type gains +2 on turning checks

## Sunlight Vulnerability

| Undead | Sunlight Effect |
|---|---|
| Vampire | Takes 2d6/round; destroyed if unable to escape |
| Spectre, Wraith | Rendered powerless (1 str/round for wraith in sunlight) |
| Bodak | 1d6/round |
| Most others | No special vulnerability (but sunbeam/sunburst still deal 1d6/level extra) |

Real sunlight only — _daylight_ spell has no special anti-undead effect unless creature's entry specifies otherwise.

---

## Undead in Antimagic Field

Incorporeal undead **wink out** (cease to exist) while within an antimagic field. Corporeal undead are not affected beyond losing spell-like abilities.

---

## Raise Dead / Resurrection and Undead

- _Raise dead_, _reincarnate_ — cannot restore undead to life
- _Resurrection_, _true resurrection_ — can affect **willing** undead, converting them back to the living creature they once were
- Some undead specifically note these spells can't affect them (visage, necrotic termination victims)
