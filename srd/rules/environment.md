# Environmental Hazards (3.5e)

## Light and Darkness

| Condition | Vision | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Bright light | Normal | No penalties |
| Shadowy (dim) light | Low-light creatures see normally; others at -1 to Spot/Search | Concealment (20% miss chance) if no low-light vision |
| Darkness | Blind without darkvision | Total concealment (50% miss chance); Spot/Listen at -4 per 10 ft.; cannot read maps |
| Magical darkness | Darkvision doesn't work | As darkness above; low-light fails too |

**Darkvision (most humanoids, undead):** See in darkness up to range (usually 60 ft.) in black and white. Does not help in magical darkness (deeper darkness, etc.).

**Low-light vision (elves, gnomes, half-elves):** Twice the normal distance with dim-light sources. A candle is 5 ft. normally; 10 ft. for low-light. Still blind in total darkness.

**Light sources:**

| Source | Normal radius | Low-light radius |
|---|---|---|
| Candle | 5 ft. | 10 ft. |
| Torch | 20 ft. | 40 ft. |
| Lantern, hooded | 30 ft. | 60 ft. |
| Lantern, bullseye | 60 ft. cone | 120 ft. |
| Sunrod | 30 ft. | 60 ft. |
| Light spell | 20 ft. | 40 ft. |
| Daylight spell | 60 ft. | 120 ft. |

**Concealment miss chance:** 20% for partial (shadowy light, smoke); 50% for total (darkness, heavy fog).

## Falling

- **Damage:** 1d6 per 10 ft. fallen, max 20d6 (200 ft.).
- **Soft landing** (water, deep snow): halve damage; Fort DC 15 to avoid damage entirely on water dive.
- **Catch yourself** (Climb or Tumble): DC 15 to grab a ledge after the first 5 ft.; prevents all damage if successful.
- **Landing on someone:** Both take half the total fall damage.
- **Diagonal chutes/slides:** DM's call; typically halve damage or allow Reflex DC 15.

## Drowning and Suffocation

**Holding breath:** Can hold breath for a number of rounds equal to Constitution score. After that: Fort DC 10 (escalating +1 each round) or take 1d6 subdual damage per round. At 0 hp from drowning: full damage each round.

**Drowning:** A drowning character must be rescued and make a DC 10 Heal check (or DC 15 without tools) to revive if unconscious.

**Suffocation** (no air at all): A creature can survive for 2 rounds per point of Constitution; after that, Fort DC 10+1/round or fall unconscious. Unconscious creatures die in 1 round.

## Fire and Heat

| Hazard | Damage | Save |
|---|---|---|
| Torch/candle touch | 1 | — |
| Torch (improvised weapon) | 1d3 (fire) | — |
| Alchemist's fire | 1d6 first round, 1d6 each subsequent round | Reflex DC 15 to put out |
| Burning clothing/oil | 1d6/round | Reflex DC 15 to extinguish (or stop, drop, roll — full round) |
| Flaming oil (direct) | 1d3 splash / 2d6 direct | Reflex DC 14 vs splash |
| Campfire size | 1d6 | Reflex DC 15 to avoid if tripping into it |
| Heat danger (very hot climate) | 1d4/hour | Fort DC 15+1/previous save; fail = fatigued/exhausted/heatstroke |

**Fire damage and materials:** Wood ignites at 300°F (immediate damage); cloth and paper ignite at 200°F; metal and stone typically only melt or become hazardous in magical fire.

**Fire spells:** Fireball does 1d6/level (Reflex half); burning hands 1d4/level (Reflex half); wall of fire 2d4 within 10 ft., 1d4 within 20 ft., 2d6+level to cross. Fire resistance/immunity from outsider traits typically absorbs the first N points of fire damage per round.

## Cold and Exposure

| Hazard | Damage | Save |
|---|---|---|
| Cold climate (below freezing) | 1d6/hour (nonlethal) | Fort DC 15+1/previous save |
| Extreme cold | 1d6/10 minutes (nonlethal) | Fort DC 15+1/previous save |
| Hypothermia (0 hp from cold) | Lethal from that point | — |
| Contact with very cold surface (ice floor in dungeon) | 1d4 | — |

**Surviving cold:** Proper gear (cold-weather outfit) grants +5 to saves vs cold. Endure elements negates all nonlethal cold damage. Characters with fire resistance take no cold damage from environmental cold.

**Snow and ice movement:** Ice: Balance DC 10 to stand; Tumble or Balance DC 15 to move (full speed requires DC 17). Deep snow: half movement; treat as difficult terrain.

## Poison

All poisons deal ability damage; failed saves advance the effect. Poison types determine application:

| Type | Delivery | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Contact** | Touch skin | Single save; secondary damage 1 minute later |
| **Ingested** | Must eat/drink | Onset 10 minutes; usually weaker if only partial dose |
| **Injury** | Must deal damage | Single save; secondary 1 minute later |
| **Inhaled** | Breathe | Usually area; burst or cloud on breaking container |

**Save structure:** Primary damage (on first failed save); secondary damage (Fort save 1 minute later). Success on either save negates that component. Neutralize poison (spell or potion) stops both components.

**Saving against repeated doses:** Each exposure requires a new save. Multiple doses in the same round stack only if they have different ability scores targeted.

## Disease

**Onset:** 1–7 days (typically). An infected character makes a Fortitude save each day (or at specified interval); success = no damage, failure = ability damage/other effects.

**Recovery:** Two consecutive successful saves = disease beaten (character is cured).

| Disease | DC | Incubation | Damage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Filth fever | 12 | 1d3 days | 1d3 Dex, 1d3 Con |
| Ghoul fever | 15 | 1 day | 1d3 Con, 1d3 Dex |
| Mummy rot | 20 | 1 day | 1d6 Con |
| Mindfire | 12 | 1 day | 1d4 Int |
| Red ache | 15 | 1d3 days | 1d6 Str |
| Blinding sickness | 16 | 1d3 days | 1d4 Str; >2 Str damage = blinded |
| Shakes | 13 | 1 day | 1d8 Dex |
| Slimy doom | 14 | 1 day | 1d4 Con; fail + Con 0 = turns to slime |

**Mummy rot** is supernatural and can only be removed by remove disease from a cleric of 7th level or higher; normal immunity doesn't apply.

**Treating disease:** Heal DC 15 to diagnose; treat patient to grant +4 on next save. Heal check must be made each day before the patient's save.

## Traps (Quick Reference)

Traps have their own CR for experience purposes (CR = rated challenge of the trap). Search DC and Disable Device DC are set at trap creation. Magic traps typically have Disable Device DC 25+ and are detected via Spellcraft (trained) or detect magic.

| Trap type | Search | Disable |
|---|---|---|
| Simple mechanical | 15–20 | 20 |
| Complex mechanical | 20–25 | 25 |
| Magic device | 25–30 | 26–30 |
| Spell trap | — | n/a (dispel magic) |

*Sources: PHB 147, DMG 76–82 (traps), DMG 302–305 (extreme environments).*
