# Libris Mortis Prestige Classes

_Source: Libris Mortis (LM), Chapter 3, pp. 41–58_

---

## Master of Shrouds

- **Source**: LM p.46
- **Requirements**: Nongood; Will save +5; Concentration 5, Knowledge (religion) 5, Spellcraft 5 ranks; Augment Summoning, Spell Focus (conjuration); able to cast protection from good as divine spell; able to rebuke undead
- **HD**: d8 | **BAB**: 3/4 | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good (strong Will progression)
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Diplomacy, Hide, Knowledge (arcana), Knowledge (religion), Profession, Spellcraft
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int
- **Key features** (10 levels):
  - 1st: Extra rebuking (4 additional/day), rebuke undead levels stack with all rebuking classes
  - 2nd: Summon undead (3+Cha/day) as SLA — 2nd: 1 shadow; +1 divine spell level
  - 3rd–10th: Spell level advances +1/level (divine spellcasting class)
  - 4th: Summon undead — 1 wraith or 2 shadows
  - 5th: Improved summoning (summoned undead gain +2 attack/damage)
  - 6th: Summon undead — 1 spectre, 2 wraiths, or 4 shadows
  - 8th: Summon undead — 1 greater shadow, 2 spectres, 4 wraiths, or 4 shadows
  - 10th: Summon undead — 1 dread wraith, 2 greater shadows, 4 spectres, 4 wraiths, or 4 shadows
- **Notes**: Evil cleric/necromancer capstone. Controls an escalating suite of incorporeal undead. Pairs naturally with True Necromancer for combined arcane/divine builds. Excellent in Ptolus undercity encounters — shadows and spectres are the Dungeon's signature threats.

## Pale Master

- **Source**: LM p.47
- **Requirements**: Nongood; Knowledge (religion) 8 ranks; Skill Focus (Knowledge [religion]); able to cast command undead and vampiric touch as arcane spells; spent 3+ days locked in tomb with animate undead
- **HD**: d4 | **BAB**: 1/2 | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Diplomacy, Hide, Knowledge (any), Listen, Move Silently, Profession, Spellcraft
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int
- **Key features** (10 levels):
  - 1st: +1 arcane spellcasting level
  - 2nd: Animate dead 1/day as SLA (no material component; control limit: 2× arcane CL HD); +arcane spells
  - 3rd: Darkvision 60 ft. (or +60 ft. if already possessed)
  - 4th: Undead armor affinity (–10% arcane spell failure)
  - 5th: Control undead 1/day touch (no save; lasts 1 round/class level); Deathless vigor (+4 Fort vs. effects that work on objects)
  - 6th: Undead graft (+4 inherent Str; delivers paralyzing touch 1/day; DC 10+PM level+Cha)
  - 7th: Tough as bone (immune to disease, nonlethal damage, stunning); weakening touch (1d6 Str, no save)
  - 8th: Undead armor affinity (–20%); degenerative touch (1 negative level, no save; Fort 24 hrs later to make permanent)
  - 9th: Undead cohort (as Undead Leadership feat; single cohort only)
  - 10th: Deathless mastery (immune to poison, sleep, paralysis, death effects, crits, ability/energy drain, physical ability damage); destructive touch (Fort or 1d6 Con drain); deathless master's touch (Fort or die; corpse rises as zombie under control)
- **Notes**: Arcane necromancer who half-becomes undead over 10 levels. No divine spell access. Best wizard/sorcerer dip that doesn't sacrifice arcane progression. Loses arcane spells at 1st level only.

## True Necromancer

- **Source**: LM p.51
- **Requirements**: Nongood; Knowledge (arcana) 8, Knowledge (religion) 8 ranks; Spell Focus (necromancy); able to cast summon undead II as divine and command undead as arcane; able to rebuke undead; access to Death domain
- **HD**: d6 | **BAB**: 1/2 | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Class skills**: Concentration, Craft, Heal, Knowledge (arcana), Knowledge (religion), Profession, Search, Spellcraft
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int
- **Key features** (14 levels — unusually long PRC):
  - 1st: Rebuke undead levels stack (both arcane and divine); +arcane spells
  - 2nd: Create undead 1/day (supply components); +divine spells
  - 3rd: Necromantic prowess +1 (effective CL for necromancy/rebuking increased; stacks to +4 at 12th level)
  - 4th: Zone of desecration (permanent desecrate-like aura around caster for allied undead)
  - 5th: Create undead 2/day; +arcane and +divine
  - 7th: Major desecration (aura expands to 10 ft./class level radius)
  - 8th: Create greater undead 1/day
  - 10th: Horrid wilting (as spell; 1/day)
  - 11th: Create greater undead 2/day
  - 13th: Energy drain (as spell; 1/day)
  - 14th: Wail of the banshee (as spell; 1/day)
  - Alternates between +arcane and +divine spell levels each level
- **Notes**: Requires multiclass cleric+wizard/sorcerer investment. The longest PRC in the book (14 levels). Capstone abilities (energy drain, wail) are extremely powerful. Zone of desecration makes every allied undead in the area more resilient — powerful in extended dungeon encounters.

## Death's Chosen

- **Source**: LM p.41
- **Requirements**: Nongood; BAB +5; Knowledge (religion) 1, Spot 2 ranks; must be accepted by a sentient undead with at least as many HD as the character
- **HD**: d10 | **BAB**: Full | **Saves**: Fort good, Ref poor, Will poor (improving)
- **Class skills**: Craft, Diplomacy, Gather Information, Hide, Knowledge (religion), Listen, Move Silently, Profession, Search, Spot
- **Skill points**: 2 + Int
- **Key features** (3 levels only):
  - 1st: Shield of the master (master can exempt chosen from its own spells/abilities as free action); vigor of the chosen (needs 1/10 food/water; 2 hrs sleep; +4 Fort vs. disease; Endurance bonus feat)
  - 2nd: Will of the chosen (+2 morale on Will saves within 60 ft. of master)
  - 3rd: Bravery of the chosen (+1 morale on melee attack/damage within 30 ft. of master; increases to +2 if master at half hp); unnatural aura (animals panicked within 30 ft.)
- **Notes**: Short (3-level) martial PRC. Represents a living thrall/bodyguard for a vampire, lich, or other intelligent undead boss. Useful as a recurring NPC class — the death's chosen is the "face" of the undead patron. Fighters, barbarians, and rogues enter this class most naturally. No spell advancement.

## Dirgesinger

- **Source**: LM p.43
- **Requirements**: Nongood; Knowledge (religion) 4, Perform (any) 8 ranks; Requiem feat; bardic music class feature
- **HD**: d6 | **BAB**: 3/4 | **Saves**: Fort poor, Ref poor, Will good
- **Class skills**: Bluff, Concentration, Craft, Diplomacy, Intimidate, Knowledge (history), Knowledge (religion), Listen, Perform, Sense Motive, Speak Language
- **Skill points**: 4 + Int
- **Key features** (5 levels):
  - 1st: Dirgesong (counts as bardic music; stacks with bard for uses/day but not for effects known); Song of sorrow (enemies within hearing: –2 Will saves, –2 attack/damage; Will DC 10+Perform ranks negates)
  - 2nd: Song of bolstering (undead within 30 ft. gain turn resistance = dirgesinger class + bard levels)
  - 3rd: Song of grief (60 ft.; Will DC or confused; compulsion/mind-affecting)
  - 4th: Song of horror (60 ft.; Will DC or 1d6 Str and 1d6 Dex damage; 24-hr immunity after first save)
  - 5th: Song of awakening (animate recently slain corpse as loyal undead via Perform check DC 10+HD; creature retains class features and SLAs; only 1 at a time; lasts as long as dirgesinger performs)
- **Notes**: Bard subclass that pivots to necromancy. Song of bolstering is especially valuable in the Ptolus Necropolis — turning resistance for entire undead retinues. Song of awakening is a powerful encounter tool: animate an enemy's corpse immediately after killing it.
