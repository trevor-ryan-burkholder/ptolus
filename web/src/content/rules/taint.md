# Taint Rules
**Source:** Heroes of Horror (HoH) Chapter 4, pp. 62–80

Taint represents the corrupting influence of evil on body and soul. Two independent tracks:
- **Corruption** — physical decay (Fortitude save to resist)
- **Depravity** — mental/spiritual decay (Will save to resist)

Both are point values (0+). Severity is determined by comparing the score against the character's **Constitution** (for Corruption) or **Wisdom** (for Depravity) using Table 4-1.

---

## Acquiring Taint

**Initial exposure** to a tainted place, object, or act: 1 point (typical), 1d3 or 1d4 for severe evil.
- Save DC 20 (typical tainted site) to DC 25–30 (extremely malevolent) to resist or halve.
- Fort save vs. Corruption; Will save vs. Depravity.

**Prolonged exposure** in a tainted area: additional 1 point per 24 hours (base DC 10, +5 per consecutive 24-hr period).

**Sources:**
- Entering a tainted location (undead-linked sites → Corruption; outsider/cultist sites → Depravity)
- Contact with tainted objects
- Monster attacks (specific creature entries list taint bestowed)
- Performing evil acts
- Casting spells with the evil descriptor (variant rule: Will DC 10 + spell level or +1 Depravity)
- Casting any arcane spell as a Tainted Scholar (automatic Will save DC 10 + spell level)
- Corrupt spells (ability damage/drain paid by caster when duration expires)

**Surge save:** Any time a character gains more Corruption in one event than their current Con modifier, Fort save DC 15 + points gained: failure = nauseated 1d4 rounds (success = sickened 1d4 rounds). Same rule for Depravity vs. Wis modifier, Will save: failure = stunned 1d4 rounds (success = dazed 1d4 rounds).

---

## Taint Thresholds (Table 4-1)

Severity is keyed to the ability score (Con for Corruption, Wis for Depravity):

| Con or Wis | No Taint | Mild Taint | Moderate Taint | Severe Taint | Dead/Insane |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| 1–4 | 0 | 1 | 2–5 | 6–13 | 14+ |
| 5–8 | 0 | 1–3 | 4–11 | 12–27 | 28+ |
| 9–12 | 0 | 1–5 | 6–17 | 18–41 | 42+ |
| 13–16 | 0 | 1–7 | 8–23 | 24–55 | 56+ |
| 17–20 | 0 | 1–9 | 10–29 | 30–69 | 70+ |
| 21–24 | 0 | 1–11 | 12–35 | 36–83 | 84+ |

**Exceeding Severe threshold:** Character dies (rises as Tainted Minion in 1d6 hours) or goes irretrievably mad (gains Tainted Raver template, falls under DM control).

## Effects by Severity

### Mild Taint
- Character must be treated (restoration, heal, or greater restoration) within 24 hours or become permanently afflicted.
- Permanently afflicted: gains **1 symptom** (1 physical symptom from Corruption, 1 mental from Depravity).
- Removal requires miracle or wish to drop back to 0.

### Moderate Taint
- Must receive heal or greater restoration within 24 hours or become permanently afflicted.
- Permanently afflicted: gains **1 additional symptom** (moderate tier), keeps mild symptoms.
- Removal requires miracle or wish to drop back to mild.
- Registers as evil to detect evil.
- **Bonus feat** granted (any feat for which character qualifies).

### Severe Taint
- Must receive greater restoration within 24 hours or become permanently afflicted.
- Permanently afflicted: gains **1 severe symptom**, keeps prior symptoms.
- Removal requires miracle or wish to drop back to moderate.
- Alignment effectively evil.
- Death (Corruption) or madness (Depravity) if score exceeds the severe threshold entirely.

## Physical Symptoms (Corruption — rolled d10)

**Mild:** Dead Eye (miss chance doubles), Ear Scabs (–2 Listen), Feet Curl (–10 ft. speed), Gums Swell (DC 20 CL check for verbal spells), Lumps (DC 20 Fort to run/charge), Odor of Decay (–2 Handle Animal/Ride/wild empathy), Palsy (–2 ranged attacks), Skin Seeps (+2 Escape Artist, –2 Climb/Sleight of Hand), Skin Sloughs (–2 Diplomacy/Gather Information/Perform), Winded (staggered from round 3 onward in combat).

**Moderate:** Blood Eruption (DC 15 Fort at start of combat or sickened 4 rounds), Bones Thicken (+2 Str, –4 Dex), Chronic Illness (–3 Fort saves, bouts of vomiting), Eruption of Sores (–1 Str/Cha checks and skills), Fingers and Toes Fuse (–2 attack rolls; DC 20 CL check for somatic spells), Joint Pain (–3 Ref saves), Lips Shrink (–2 Cha checks and skills), Paralyzed Face (+1 Bluff, –1 other Cha checks), Skin Thickens (+1 natural armor, –2 Dex checks and skills), Uncontrollable Seizures (–4 initiative).

**Severe:** Great Swollen Growths (–2 AC), Internal Corruption (–2 Con; body looks normal externally), Lich Eyes (darkvision 60 ft. or +30 ft.; light blindness), Lungs Eaten Away (–1 Str/Dex/Con), Nose Rots (–2 Cha), Shriveled Flesh (–1 hp/level retroactively, –1 hp/level gained), Skin Lichen (–2 Str/Dex/Con checks and skills), Skull Deformed (–2 Int/Wis, –3 Will saves), Spine Twists (–2 Dex), Wrigglers (–2 Str, +2 Intimidate, –4 other Cha checks).

---

## Mental Symptoms (Depravity — rolled d10)

**Mild:** Aggressive (–1 AC), Bestial (+2 Handle Animal/Ride/wild empathy; –1 other Cha checks), Compulsive (DC 10 Fort daily or 1d4 nonlethal damage), Crazed (if surprised: equal chance charge or flee), Disoriented (if surprised: dazed 1 round), Mildly Phobic (gain mild phobia, Will DC 12 + CR or shaken), Neglectful (–2 Cha checks and skills), Opinionated (always flat-footed in round 1), Prophetic (constant nightmares; 10% accurate visions), Sycophantic (–2 Listen/Spot; enemies +2 Listen to hear you).

**Moderate:** Delusional (–2 Wis checks and skills), Deranged (–2 Int checks and skills), Distracted (–2 all skill checks), Hallucinating (–6 initiative), Hysterical (round 2 of combat: single action only), Jittery (if hit while flat-footed: panicked), Moderately Phobic (Will DC 14 + CR or frightened; replaces mild phobia), Solipsistic (DC 20 Will when struck or take +1 damage/die), Treacherous (DC 20 Will or must take any AoO, even vs. allies), Weak-Willed (–3 Will saves).

**Severe:** Apathetic (–2 Cha/Wis), Craven (must always cast defensively; must always fight defensively), Enthralled (–4 attack vs. evil; damage vs. evil is nonlethal only), Fatalistic (–3 all saves), Hubristic (divine healing has no effect), Murderous (must coup de grace any helpless creature, including allies), Paranoid (–1 penalty per ally that misses in combat; cumulative), Severely Phobic (Will DC 16 + CR or panicked; replaces moderate phobia), Unbalanced (automatically confused on turn 1 and every other turn), Unresponsive (–2 Wis).

## Taint and Alignment

- Moderate taint: registers as evil to detect evil; neutral at best in practice.
- Severe taint: effectively evil.
- Clerics of good deities: moderate taint triggers divine warning; severe taint = lose all spells/class features until atoned.
- Paladins: moderate taint = lose all paladin spells/abilities until taint reduced to mild.

---

## Taint and Bonus Feats

- Reaching moderate and severe taint each grant a **bonus feat** (any feat for which prerequisites are met).
- Taint feats remain accessible even if taint rises higher; become inactive if taint drops below their threshold.

---

## Removing Taint

Taint cannot be removed unless the character is willing. Crossing a threshold locks it in — only quick treatment (within 24 hours) or powerful magic can lower it past a threshold.

### Spells (each can only reduce a given character's taint once per day)
| Spell | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Atonement | Reduces both Corruption and Depravity by up to caster level (costs 500 XP; requires a quest; can cross thresholds) |
| Greater Restoration | Reduces both by caster level; can cross thresholds if within 24 hrs |
| Heal | Reduces both by 1 per 3 caster levels; can cross thresholds if within 24 hrs |
| Restoration | Reduces both by 1 per 4 caster levels; can cross mild threshold if within 24 hrs |
| Remove Curse | Reduces Depravity by 1 |
| Remove Disease | Reduces Corruption by 1 |
| Miracle / Wish | Reduces to highest point of the next lower threshold, regardless of time elapsed |

### Good Deeds
Ritual good deeds (performed under cleric supervision, 1 week, daily): reduces Depravity by 1. Quests: reduce Depravity by 1 per week required.

### Sacred Springs
1 point of Corruption removed per day of complete rest (no other activity).

### Taint-Absorbing Items
Objects of pure jade, prepared vellum, lightning-struck oak, silk sash, etc. (100 gp each). Absorbs up to 7 points of taint before decomposing. Multiple items provide diminishing returns (see Table 4-4; 5 items = 15 pts max, then drops off).

## Tainted Locations

- Initial entry: 1 point of Corruption or Depravity (Fort/Will save DC 20–30 to resist or halve).
- Prolonged stay: +1 point per 24 hours (DC 10, +5 per additional consecutive 24-hr block).
- Evil spells in tainted areas: +1 effective CL but Will save DC 15 + spell level or +1 Depravity.
- Good/healing spells in tainted areas: –1 effective CL.
- Corpses in tainted areas: animate as zombies in 1d4 hours unless burned.
- Cleansing a tainted area: hallow spell must remain intact for 1 full year (covers 40-ft. radius).

### Dread Terrain Types (selected)
- **Charnel Bog:** Spellcraft DC 15 + spell level to cast good/healing spells; turning checks –4 (rebuking +4 profane); camping = fatigued, no spell recovery. Extended stay: Will save or trapped forever.
- **Blood Rock:** Doubles threat range of attacks made while standing on it (doesn't stack with Improved Critical/keen).
- **Night Stone:** Living creatures within 5 ft. take 1d6 damage/round (death ward protects).
- **Abyssal Blackgrass:** No natural healing within 50 ft.; magical healing halved. Evil descriptor spells nearby: Fort DC 10 + spell level or +1 Corruption.

---

## Tainted Spells

**Evil Spells and Taint (Variant Rule):** Each time a character casts a spell with the [Evil] descriptor, Will save DC 10 + spell level or +1 Depravity. Default for Tainted Scholars; optional for all others.

**Corrupt Spells:** Exceedingly vile spells that cost ability damage or drain (paid when duration expires). Preparation-only (not sorcerers/bards unless they have Corrupt Arcana feat or use scrolls). No material component; no permanent duration allowed. If made into an item, user pays the ability cost.

## [Homebrew] Ptolus Integration Notes

- **Chaositech use:** Each use of a chaositech item requires a Fort save (DC 12 + item level) or +1 Corruption. Repeated use of the same item stacks. Items rated by "level" 1–5 in Ptolus; default to level 2 for unnamed items.
- **Galchutt cultist rituals:** Participating in or witnessing Galchutt cult rites requires a Will save (DC 15 for observation, DC 18 for direct participation) or +1 Depravity per ritual. Completing a full ritual counts as a tainted location exposure.
- **The Banewarrens:** Entering the Banewarrens counts as a tainted location (DC 22); prolonged exploration (each 24-hr period) DC 12. Sealed vaults may be more intense.
- **Deep Dungeon (below level 10):** Passive Corruption accumulation — 1 point per full week spent below level 10, Fort save DC 15 to resist. No save is possible below level 15 (1 point/week automatic).
- **Necropolis:** Counts as a tainted location (Corruption-focused); DC 18 on initial entry, DC 10 + consecutive days for ongoing exposure.
- **Detect Taint (spell, HoH p.128):** Cleric 1, cone 60 ft., concentration up to 10 min/level. First round: presence/absence. Second: number and strength of strongest aura. Third: location of each. Useful for tracking chaositech smugglers, Galchutt cults, etc.

## GM Guidance

**When to call for checks:**
- First time entering a clearly evil/tainted site (catacombs, desecrated temples, known Galchutt sites, Necropolis deep zones).
- After handling a tainted item (old chaositech, items from the Banewarrens).
- After dealing with Galchutt cultists (Nihilists, Forsaken, Church of Ragpicker) or their rituals.
- After performing evil acts — DM judgment. Occasional party members accidentally tainting themselves adds drama; triggering it on every orc fight does not.

**Pacing:** Introduce taint slowly. One or two points of mild Corruption/Depravity from a memorable source are more interesting than grinding through moderate taint in the background. Let symptoms be visible at the table — describe physical changes; let players roleplay mental symptoms. The first time a character's feet curl or they become Prophetic is a narrative moment.

**Telegraphing corruption:** Taint elementals, tainted minions, and other taint-heavy creatures are visible warnings. NPCs who show advanced corruption symptoms signal what the dungeon can do to the party. Let detect taint be easy to access so players can see what they're walking into.

**Removing taint:** Make cleansing available but not trivial. Restoration is accessible at mid-levels. Greater restoration and atonement are adventure hooks — a character with severe Depravity who wants to get clean may need to undertake a quest. In Ptolus, the Church of Asche (god of winter and death) and the Knights of the Pale both deal with undead-adjacent corruption; either could serve as a cleansing resource (with obligations attached).
