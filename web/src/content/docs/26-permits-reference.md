# Ptolus Permits & Licensing — Quick Reference

**File:** `runner/permits/index.html`
**Purpose:** At-the-table reference for Ptolus permit requirements, costs, issuers, and consequences. Covers canonical city licenses (firearms, business, spells) and the [homebrew] monster grade permit system from `runner/docs/08-monster-grades.md`.

---

## Canonical Names Reference

- **Administration Building:** Located in Oldtown. All standard city permits are issued here. Open during daylight hours, staffed by the Commissar's clerks.
- **Commissar:** Igor Urnst. The Commissar's office enforces permit compliance through the City Watch and the Commissar's Men.
- **City Watch:** Patrol enforcement. First responders to permit violations, unlicensed activity, and monster-grade breaches.
- **Commissar's Men:** The Commissar's personal enforcement unit. Handle serious violations, political cases, and Grade 1–2 monster situations.
- **Imperial Eyes:** Imperial intelligence. Become involved when violations touch on Imperial law, noble houses, or the Spire.
- **Council of Guilds:** Issues some guild-specific operating licenses; consulted for Grade 1 monster exceptions.
- **Noble Houses (canonical):** Abanar, Dallimothan, Erthuo, Kath, Khatru, Nagel, Rau, Sadar, Shever, Vladaam. Noble sponsorship is required for Grade 2 Special Dispensations.

## Canonical City Licenses

*(Source: Ptolus core book, Crime and Law chapter, pp. 553–559)*

### Firearms Permit
- **Required for:** Ownership or carrying of firearms within city limits.
- **Historical note:** Mandated by Empress Addares X. Predates current Commissariat structure.
- **Issuer:** Administration Building, Oldtown.
- **Cost:** 10 gp registration fee; annual renewal 5 gp.
- **Proof:** Stamped certificate listing weapon type and owner. Must be produced on demand by City Watch.
- **Without permit:** Weapon seized; 100 gp fine on first offense. Repeat: up to 500 gp + 30 days detention.

### Business Operating License
- **Required for:** Any commercial enterprise with a fixed location (shop, inn, fencing operation, apothecary, etc.).
- **Issuer:** Administration Building; some guild-related businesses require Council of Guilds co-endorsement.
- **Cost:** 25–100 gp depending on trade type; annual renewal at same rate.
- **Without license:** Business shuttered, goods seized pending fine payment; 200 gp fine + back-license fees.

### Publishing / Printing License
- **Required for:** Any printed broadsheet, book, or pamphlet distributed publicly.
- **Issuer:** Administration Building.
- **Cost:** 15 gp per title or 50 gp for a general annual license.
- **Without license:** Print run seized; 150 gp fine. Seditious content: imprisonment up to 1 year.

### Spell Use Permit (Sanctioned)
- **Required for:** Mass-destruction spells, zone-of-effect mind-affecting spells used on crowds, and certain necromantic applications in city limits.
- **Issuer:** Commissar's office (reviewed by Inverted Pyramid liaison in most cases).
- **Cost:** 500 gp per sanctioned use, paid in advance; annual "standing authorization" for registered spellcasters: 1,000 gp.
- **Without permit:** 500–800 gp fine; spellcaster placed on Watch list; repeat: imprisonment up to 5 years. Magic-related trafficking carries enhanced penalties (Ptolus p. 555).
- **Note:** Adventuring spells cast in active combat are generally not prosecuted unless they cause significant collateral damage or civilian casualties.

## Illegal Goods & Trafficking Penalties
*(Ptolus p. 555 — canonical)*

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Failure to produce required permit | 100–500 gp | None (first offense) |
| Unlicensed business operation | 200 gp + back fees | None (first offense) |
| Trafficking illegal goods (mundane) | 500 gp | 1 year |
| Trafficking illegal goods (magic-related) | 800 gp | 5 years |
| Chaositech possession or sale | Confiscation + 1,000 gp | 2–10 years |
| Unlicensed spellcasting (mass effect) | 500–800 gp | Up to 5 years |

## Monster Grade Permit System
*[homebrew] — based on `runner/docs/08-monster-grades.md`*

Creatures above a baseline threat level require permits to move through, reside in, or operate within Ptolus city limits. The grade system classifies creatures by CR.

### Grade Definitions

| Grade | CR Range | Examples |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Grade 4 | CR 1/4 – CR 1 | Goblins, kobolds, giant rats, stirges |
| Grade 3 | CR 2 – CR 5 | Ogres, trolls, wererats, gnolls |
| Grade 2 | CR 6 – CR 10 | Giants, manticores, young dragons, mind flayers |
| Grade 1 | CR 11–15 | Beholders, adult dragons, nagas |
| Special | CR 16+ | Ancient dragons, demon lords, major undead |

### Permit Types by Grade

#### Movement Permit (Grade 4)
- **Allows:** Daytime movement through city on designated routes. No overnight stay.
- **Issuer:** Administration Building (clerk-level, low scrutiny).
- **Cost:** 5 gp/month.
- **Restrictions:** Must use Undercity Market access routes; cannot enter Nobles' Quarter, Temple District (without separate clearance), or Oldtown unescorted.
- **Renewal:** Monthly, automatic if no incidents.
- **Without permit:** City Watch detains and escorts out; 25 gp fine. Repeat or aggressive: lethal response authorized.

#### Residency Permit (Grade 3)
- **Allows:** Registered residence within city limits. Can move freely in most districts during daylight.
- **Issuer:** Administration Building; requires 2 letters of reference (employer, landlord, or guild).
- **Cost:** 50 gp/year.
- **Restrictions:** Annual review. Curfew between midnight and dawn without Night Travel Endorsement (add 20 gp/year). Barred from Nobles' Quarter without escort.
- **Renewal:** Annual; revocable on incident report.
- **Without permit:** Treated as Grade 2 threat; Commissar's Men deployed; 100 gp fine + forced registration or expulsion.

#### Special Dispensation (Grade 2)
- **Allows:** Presence in city with sponsor accountability. Movement mostly unrestricted with sponsor escort.
- **Issuer:** Administration Building, countersigned by a noble house, major guild, or recognized church.
- **Cost:** 500 gp one-time fee; sponsor assumes liability.
- **Restrictions:** Sponsor must vouch for all incidents. Creature cannot operate independently for commercial or violent purposes. Must register home address with City Watch district captain.
- **Renewal:** Annual renewal (250 gp) or new dispensation required if sponsor changes.
- **Without permit:** Commissar's Men respond (1d4+2 officers); creature detained or killed; sponsor (if known) fined 1,000 gp.

#### Council Exception (Grade 1)
- **Allows:** Presence and limited operation within city, reviewed case by case.
- **Issuer:** City Council vote required. Rarely granted.
- **Cost:** Political capital + typically 2,000–5,000 gp in fees and bribes.
- **Restrictions:** Creature is effectively on permanent probation. Any incident triggers immediate Council review and possible revocation.
- **Without permit:** Commissar's Men + City Watch combined response; Imperial Eyes notified; maximum-force authorization.

#### Special Grade (CR 16+)
- **Policy:** No permit system applies. Presence in the city is treated as an active emergency.
- **Response:** All available City Watch + Commissar's Men; Inverted Pyramid notified immediately; Knights of the Pale if creature is undead or chaos-adjacent.
- **Exception:** Ancient dragons with historical ties to noble houses have been tolerated in the past, but require direct Commissar authorization renewed annually — not a formal permit, a political arrangement.

## City Watch Response by Violation

| Situation | First Responder | Escalation |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Grade 4 without Movement Permit | 1–2 Watch officers | None unless creature resists |
| Grade 3 without Residency Permit | 1d4 Watch officers | Commissar's Men if creature flees |
| Grade 2 without Dispensation | 1d4+2 Watch + sergeant | Commissar's Men deployed simultaneously |
| Grade 1 without Council Exception | Commissar's Men (full unit) | Imperial Eyes notified |
| Special Grade (any) | All available forces | Emergency declaration; Inverted Pyramid notified |
| Unlicensed firearms | 1 Watch officer | Escalates if owner resists |
| Unlicensed business | City Watch + clerk | Padlocked; court date issued |
| Illegal spell use | 1d4 Watch + Watch mage | Inverted Pyramid liaison consulted |

## Common Adventure Scenarios

**The party's monstrous ally needs to enter the city.**
→ Determine Grade by CR. If Grade 4: 5 gp, same-day Movement Permit, day travel only. If Grade 3: needs references (Delver's Guild membership counts); 50 gp, 1–2 days. If Grade 2+: needs a noble or church sponsor — who owes the party a favor?

**Party gets caught with an unpermitted firearm.**
→ 100 gp fine, weapon held as collateral. Watch captain may waive fine if party has demonstrated City Watch cooperation in the past (GM call).

**A noble house offers to sponsor a creature.**
→ They're taking on liability. They'll want something in return — probably ongoing service, deniable muscle, or access to whatever the creature knows. House Vladaam's offers should be viewed with extreme suspicion.

**The party needs to cast a destructive spell in the city.**
→ In active combat: generally tolerated if civilian casualties are avoided. After the fact: Watch may demand explanation; if collateral damage is significant, expect a fine and possible Watch interview. Pre-planned: 500 gp sanctioned use permit; the Inverted Pyramid can expedite for a favor.

## Notes for the GM

- The permit system is unevenly enforced. Nobles' Quarter and Temple District are strictly patrolled; the Warrens and Docks are lax. A Grade 3 creature operating quietly in the Warrens may never be questioned. The same creature in Midtown will draw Watch attention within hours.
- Delver's Guild membership is not a permit substitute, but Watch officers often treat Guild members with more latitude — especially if the creature is clearly a "working delver" and not a threat.
- Bribes: a Watch officer can be bribed to overlook a minor permit violation (Grade 4, unlicensed business) for 10–20 gp. Commissar's Men are harder — 100–200 gp and the bribe itself is a crime if refused.
- The Balacazars operate a shadow permit system for creatures they employ or house. This carries no legal weight but does mean the Balacazars will intervene (for a price) if City Watch moves on their assets.
