# Imperial Calendar

Reference for the calendar system used in Ptolus and the Empire of Tarsis.
*(Sources: Ptolus, City by the Spire, pp. 28–29, 86, 541, 547; "Special Days in Ptolus" sidebar)*

---

## Year numbering

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **BE** | Before the Empire | Counted backward from the founding of the Empire of Tarsis |
| **IA** | Imperial Age | Current era; Year 1 IA = founding of the Empire of Tarsis after the Pact of Brightfather's Day |

**Current year: 721 IA** (as of campaign start).

Key dating landmarks:
- **477 BE** — Lothian the wandering preacher is crucified.
- **0/1 IA** — Empire of Tarsis founded; Delian Von Tessel crowned first Emperor.
- **413 IA** — Ptolus officially founded as a city.
- **560 IA** — Edict of Deviltry issued (outlaws arcane magic).
- **615 IA** — Ptolus declared a safe haven for arcane spellcasters.
- **641 IA** — Edict of Deviltry officially overturned.
- **720 IA** — Minor Scarlet Death outbreak; Kevris Killraven arrives in Ptolus.
- **721 IA** — Campaign present.

## Calendar structure

### Months (12)

| # | Month | Season (Ptolus) | Approx. Real-World | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Newyear** | Midwinter | January | Named for the new year; cold and dark |
| 2 | **Birth** | Late winter | February | End of winter; ice begins to break |
| 3 | **Wind** | Early spring | March | Transition; frequent storms off the Whitewind Sea |
| 4 | **Rain** | Spring | April | Rainy season begins; heaviest rainfall |
| 5 | **Bloom** | Late spring | May | Flowers; most pleasant shoulder season |
| 6 | **Sun** | Early summer | June | Warmest, brightest stretch |
| 7 | **Growth** | Midsummer | July | Long days; midsummer festivals |
| 8 | **Blessing** | Late summer | August | High ~69°F / Low ~53°F; 1.2 in. rain avg |
| 9 | **Toil** | Early fall | September | High ~60°F / Low ~48°F; 1.9 in. rain avg |
| 10 | **Harvest** | Fall | October | High ~51°F / Low ~41°F; 3.3 in. rain avg |
| 11 | **Moons** | Late fall | November | High ~48°F / Low ~49°F; 5.7 in. rain avg; gloomy |
| 12 | **Yearsend** | Early winter | December | High ~46°F / Low ~31°F; 6.0 in. rain avg; harshest month |

**Days per month**: The Ptolus book does not specify explicitly. [homebrew] Assume 28 days per month (= 336-day year) for a simple tracking calendar, or use 30/30/31 groupings if you want seasonal alignment. The weather table in PT4 implies roughly 28–31 day months.

**Ptolus climate note**: "Ptolus lies in a cool, rainy coastal region with harsh winters." The joke among locals: "Around here, all the months are called Rain." Most years see no snow even in Yearsend — it rains instead. The sea breeze off the Whitewind is a constant gentle presence. *(Ptolus, p. 547)*

## Holy days and special days

### City-wide observances
*(Ptolus, pp. 28–29 [Player's Guide]; "Special Days in Ptolus" sidebar in PT2)*

| Date | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1st of Newyear | **Newyear's Day** | First day of the year; celebration and good tidings. Babies born this day are considered lucky (unlike those born on 28th of Yearsend, considered cursed). |
| 5th of Rain | **Godsday** | Holiest day in most religions — even Lothianites treat it as important, though the Church officially still debates declaring it a true holy day. The Godsday Festival in the Temple District is enormous; travelers come from outside the city. Many workers get the day off. |
| 10th of Sun | **Day of Joining** | Holy in many religions; considered lucky for new ventures, alliances, and especially marriages — 10× more couples marry on this day than any other. Good day for forming adventuring parties, signing contracts. |
| 14th of Blessing | **Brightfather's Day** | Named for the historic Pact of Brightfather's Day (71 BE) when elves, dwarves, halflings, and humans united against Ghul. **High holy day of the Church of Lothian.** All faiths have adopted it as a day of feasting, family, and making peace with enemies. |
| 31st of Harvest | **Harvest Festival** | Feasting, dancing, performances of historical plays in colorful masks (often performed by children). Secular and joyful. |
| 23rd of Moons | **Festival of Cold Moons** | Called **Chaokaemus** by elves. Most humans see this as a day of ill omens and evil spirits — somber rituals to ward evil, honor the dead. Elves see it oppositely: a night of rejuvenation and good omens. Both hold festivals; the two groups usually celebrate separately. |

### Litorian observances
*(Ptolus, p. 55 sidebar)*

| Date | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 12th–14th of Toil | **Days of Memory** | Litorian warriors honor their fallen enemies' kills by offering their own victories during these three days. Any spoils of battle go to the spirits of the dead. |
| 21st of Growth | **Great Feast (midsummer)** | Litorian tribes gather for feasting and contests of drinking and storytelling. Tribal elders preside; winner is named Chieftain of the Great Feast. |
| 21st of Newyear | **Great Feast (midwinter)** | Same celebration at midwinter. |
| 2nd–5th of Moons | **Winter Fast** | Blooded litorian warriors fast for four days (water only) as proof of strength; on the 5th day they hunt and eat raw. |

## Converting "sessions from now" to IC dates

Assume the campaign starts on a specific IC date (set when Campaign begins; record in `campaign/timeline.md`).

**Quick math**: If your months are 28 days and weeks are 7 days:
- 1 week = 7 days
- 1 month = 28 days (4 weeks)
- 1 year = 336 days (12 months)

**Example session pacing**: Light exploration sessions tend to pass 1–3 days of IC time; travel sessions pass 1–7 days; dungeon delves can be 1 day or many. A "1 session = 1 week" rule of thumb works for Ptolus's urban pace.

**Date format**: Write dates as `[day] of [month], [year] IA`. Example: *14th of Blessing, 721 IA*.

**Shorthand for session notes**: Once you set a start date, use day-counts: "Session 3 ends, day 12 of campaign = 12th of [start month + 1] if started on the 1st."

## Church of Lothian holy calendar highlights

The Church of Lothian dominates civil life; their holidays affect city operations.

- **Brightfather's Day (14th of Blessing)**: Biggest holy day. The Cathedral of St. Valien holds a massive public ceremony. City Watch reduces patrols around the Temple District; crime spikes in other districts.
- **Godsday (5th of Rain)**: The Church officially resists calling it holy, but practically the city slows down. The Temple District festival draws pilgrims.
- **Theoday weekly rite**: True resurrection performed by Archbishop Adlam Theobold at St. Valien's Cathedral, by appointment only; 3+ week wait list. *(Ptolus, p. 376)*
- **High holy days**: Clerics wear gold vestments on Brightfather's Day and other major holy days (normally white and blue). *(Ptolus, p. 68)*
