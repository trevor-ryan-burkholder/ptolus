# The Dungeon

Beneath Ptolus sprawls **the Dungeon** — the single largest dungeon complex documented in the published Ptolus material. It is the central engine of the campaign: every back alley in Old City has a cellar that leads to the sewers that lead to deeper tunnels that lead to layered Imperial ruins, that lead to dwarven holds, that lead to drow city-states, that lead, eventually, to **Ghul's Labyrinth** and beneath that to whatever the Spire imprisons.

## Layers (from shallowest to deepest)

The Dungeon is conventionally divided into layers. Use these as setting for the dungeon-generator (with appropriate environment tags and CR ranges).

### Layer 1 — Sewers and Sub-Streets (CR 1–4)
The municipal sewer system, expanded over centuries on top of older drains. Tag environments as `ptolus_undercity`, `sewer`. Inhabitants: rats, dire rats, kobolds, choker, stirges, otyughs, occasional ratmen, occasional cult shrine.

### Layer 2 — Old Imperial Cellars and Catacombs (CR 2–6)
Walled-up basements, forgotten temples, Imperial-era passages. Tag `ptolus_undercity`, `dungeon`. Inhabitants: wights, ghouls, doppelgangers in hiding, lost things from the Empire of Tarsis.

### Layer 3 — Ghul's Outerworks (CR 4–8)
The outer fortifications of Ghul's Labyrinth — orc and goblin holdfasts, drow patrols, bugbear warbands, ogre mercenaries. Tag `ptolus_dungeon`. Inhabitants: orcs, hobgoblins, ogres, drow, drider scouts, occasional troll, occasional gricks.

### Layer 4 — Ghul's Labyrinth Proper (CR 6–10)
Ghul's fortress-complex — vast halls, traps, sigil-warded chambers. Tag `ptolus_dungeon`. Inhabitants: drow houses, derro, mind flayers, demon-bound guardians, undead lords.

### Layer 5 — The Underdeep (CR 8–14)
Beyond Ghul's, the deeper passages connect to a true Underdark — drow city-states, mind flayer enclaves, ancient elemental cults. Tag `underground`, `ptolus_dungeon`. Inhabitants: drow nobility, mind flayer councils, illithid thralls, deeper aberrations, demons that have leaked through.

### Layer 6 — The Womb of the Spire
The deepest known reach — what the Cults of Chaos try to find. The Galchutt are imprisoned somewhere down here. Rumored only; few have seen it and returned.

## Common entrances

- **The Cliffs west of the city** — natural caves and abandoned mines.
- **Old City cellars** — many of the oldest buildings have basements that aren't quite shut.
- **The Sewers** — accessible by manhole anywhere in the lower districts.
- **The Spire's base** — rents and portals around the Spire foundation (see `the-spire.md`).
- **The Necropolis tunnels** — official tombs that connect to forgotten Imperial catacombs.
- **The Banewarrens** — a special complex with its own rules; sealed access. *(See *Ptolus* core book, ~p. 600+, and the *Banewarrens* adventure.)*

## In play

The Dungeon is *the* place to go for traditional dungeon-crawl XP. Use the encounter generator's `ptolus_dungeon` filter for general dungeon encounters; pick a layer for CR-appropriate filtering.

The Dungeon is also a **source of plot**. The Cults of Chaos are below. The drow scheme below. The Banewarrens were sealed for a reason. Ancient Imperial ruins hold lost magic. Every layer is a vertical campaign arc.

## Maps and keying

The published Ptolus core book includes maps for the upper Dungeon and Ghul's Labyrinth. Use the dungeon-generator in `/runner/dungeons/` for original ad-hoc dungeons that aren't keyed in the book.

## Hooks the Dungeon generates

- A merchant's caravan vanished into the sewers. The Watch won't go in.
- A cult has dug a new entrance from a Nobles' Quarter cellar.
- A drow noble has surfaced with a peace offering. Trust him?
- Old Imperial coins are appearing in the South Market. Someone has found something.
- An apprentice of the Inverted Pyramid heard a voice from beneath. He hasn't slept since.
- The Banewarrens have a new crack. The Knights of the Pale have requested adventurers.
