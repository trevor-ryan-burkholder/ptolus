# The Spire, Jabel Shammar, and Goth Gulgamel

## The Spire (Physical)

The Spire is the defining geographic feature of Ptolus: an impossibly tall, narrow column of dark stone that rises approximately **3,000 feet** above the highest level of the city (Ptolus p. 502). It marks the western edge of Ptolus. The Spire is not natural -- it was created by a geological upheaval when the concentrated evil of the Banewarrens became so repugnant that the earth itself thrust Tremoc Korin away from itself, forming the Spire with the fortress Mosul Pearl (later Jabel Shammar) atop it (Ptolus p. 76).

**Public knowledge:**
- The Spire is essentially unclimbable and unapproachable. Flying up to Jabel Shammar is said to be foolhardy and dangerous -- "those who try never return." Mages have been reported to simply fade away into the wind or disappear into a cloud. Asking experienced sources (Inverted Pyramid mages, Brides of Magic) confirms these popular stories are accurate (Ptolus p. 506).
- The Spire is often obscured by clouds. It poses an ever-present threat and reminder of the region's dark history (Ptolus p. 486).
- Halfway up the Spire sits the visible ruin of **Goth Gulgamel**, the fortress of the Skull-King Ghul. Sometimes at night, yellow-green lights glow in its windows, which most people correctly attribute to restless evil spirits (Ptolus p. 488).
- The Spire is also known as **the Black Tower**, **the Dread Spike**, and **the Dread Pillar** in old texts. Jabel Shammar itself is sometimes called those last two names (Ptolus p. 504).

**The Baneheart shaft:** The interior of the Spire contains a 3,000-foot shaft called Tremoc Korin (the Baneheart), which runs the entire length of the Spire. The shaft is lined with balconies and individual vaults -- the worst of the Banewarrens' stored banes. This is how Goth Gulgamel connects to the Banewarrens below (Ptolus pp. 419, 487).

## Jabel Shammar

"Almost certainly the worst place in the world." (Ptolus p. 501)

Jabel Shammar is the fortress at the absolute top of the Spire. It was built by **Eslathagos Malkith**, the Dread One, and is older than the Spire itself: the Spire was created *because of* the fortress (then called Mosul Pearl) and what lay beneath it.

### History

- **~7,500 BE:** A powerful and benevolent cleric named **Danar Rotansin** built a tower called **Mosul Pearl** (the White Tower of Danar) near the sea. He began gathering all the evil artifacts, trapped fiends, dark relics, and other "banes" he could find, imprisoning them in deep catacombs beneath his tower called **Tremoc Korin** (the Banewarrens). His allies in this effort included the silver dragon **Saggarintys the Silver King**, a planetar named **Bastion**, and a solar named **Averon** (Ptolus pp. 75-76, 419).
- **~7,420 BE:** The concentrated evil of the Banewarrens became so repugnant that the earth rejected it in a geological upheaval, creating the Spire with Mosul Pearl at its top. Danar repaired the damaged Banewarrens in the aftermath (Ptolus p. 76).
- **~7,420 BE (shortly after):** The proximity of the Galchutt and the influence of the banes -- particularly the **Book of Inverted Darkness** -- corrupted Danar. He became **Eslathagos Malkith, the Dread One**. He renamed his tower **Jabel Shammar** and began using the banes he once locked away to create evil servants. Among the creatures he fashioned in Jabel Shammar's laboratories: manticores, destrachans, goblins, and bugbears (Ptolus pp. 76, 501).
- **~7,300 BE:** The Dread One attacks the civilized world. Only runebearer heroes, blessed by the gods, stop him. After his defeat, the world's remaining forces attempt to destroy the Spire and fortress -- they fail. Instead, they seal it with powerful magic that reinforces what Danar himself had created (Ptolus p. 81 timeline).
- **The Dread One's fate:** Some part of Danar -- the good man he had been -- remained after his defeat. His spirit, now free of corruption, managed to re-seal the Banewarrens once again before being destroyed (Ptolus p. 77).

### What Jabel Shammar Is Today

Jabel Shammar is abandoned -- a ruin. Its master is dead. A few guardians remain, and wards summon more if intruders violate the fortress, but it is a lingering shade of its former self. Designed for **high-level characters** -- the climactic adventure of the Ptolus Campaign (Ptolus p. 502).

**Physical description:** The Spire rises 3,000 feet above the city, but Jabel Shammar rises another **500 feet above the pinnacle** -- extending the Spire's height by one-sixth. The fortress is about **200 feet across** for most of its height; the Spire widens slightly to almost **300 feet in diameter** at the top where it rests. The fortress consists of four towers: one central (the **Tower of Malice**) and three shorter ones (**Misbegotten Tower**, **Tower of Blasphemy**, **Forlorn Tower**). Two of the smaller towers connect to the central tower by ten-foot-wide walkways fifty feet above the rocky, jagged ground below (Ptolus pp. 502-503).

**Appearance:** Each tower appears fashioned from dark stone, but it is actually **solidified negative energy** fortified by the malevolent force called the Malignancy growing within. Spikes and blade-like protrusions cover the outer walls, particularly at the tops. Wind moans as it passes through -- deliberately designed by the Dread One. The interior design is gothic and twisted, with baroque ornamentation of a demonic nature on virtually every surface (Ptolus pp. 502-503).

**The Malignancy:** All of Jabel Shammar is unhallowed. A malevolent force called the Malignancy grows like a cancer within the fortress, slowly working toward restoring the Dread One. Each hour spent within earns characters corruption points; accumulating enough causes irreversible evil transformation. The Malignancy also grants the fortress's creatures awareness of intruders (Ptolus pp. 503-505).

### Ways Into Jabel Shammar (DM Only)

There are very few:
1. **Alchestrin's Tomb** in the Necropolis contains a memorized spell (usable once) that takes characters onto the walkway between the Tower of Malice and the Tower of Blasphemy (Ptolus p. 506).
2. **From the Banewarrens**, one can access the Dread One's Path, and from there -- if one finds a secret door at the end (Search DC 40) opened only with the Banewarrens key -- one can pass magically up into the dungeons of Jabel Shammar (Ptolus p. 506).
3. **Via the Entropy Sphere** in Goth Gulgamel, if one has the Banewarrens key, Parnaith's mirrored sphere, or the Dread One's staff, one can reach the Seeping Portal chamber in the Dungeons (Ptolus p. 506).

## Goth Gulgamel

"Castle of Darkness" -- the name combines an Elvish and a Dwarvish word, reflecting its master's theft of others' legacies (Ptolus p. 486).

Goth Gulgamel is the fortress of **Ghul: the Skull-King and Half God**, located approximately halfway up the Spire. Ghul was a half-demon sorcerer originally from Kem who claimed (falsely) to be the son of the Dread One and discovered the Entropy Sphere (Ptolus p. 81).

### Ghul's Legacy

- Ghul built Goth Gulgamel using Toruk-Rul orcs he had created (350 BE; Ptolus p. 83 timeline).
- He used the fortress's demiplane connections and the Entropy Sphere to extend his reach, carving out vast underground labyrinths that became the basis for Ghul's Labyrinth beneath Ptolus (Ptolus p. 81).
- He tortured Elder and Shoal Elves into the **Harrow Elves** and -- a fact not widely known -- also into the **Urthon Aedar** (Ptolus p. 81).
- After his defeat, the unified armies built **Dalenguard** to watch over Goth Gulgamel. Ptolus was born around that fortress (Ptolus p. 486).

### Current State

Goth Gulgamel has lain quiet for centuries since Ghul's defeat, but in the last few hundred years three groups have moved in, all unnoticed by the increasingly lax garrison of Dalenguard:

1. **The Soul Riders** -- disembodied minds using the statues in the Hall of Shadows as foci. They want secrecy and would not harm Ptolus, which supplies them with bodies to ride (Ptolus p. 489).
2. **Ochremeshk's followers** -- fire-using servitors (salamanders, hellfire giants) occupying the Path of Burning Souls, awaiting their imprisoned demon god whose freedom lies in the damaged Entropy Sphere (Ptolus pp. 486, 491).
3. **Urthon Aedar** -- the enigmatic Elder Elves use Goth Gulgamel as a waystation between Ptolus and Dreta Phantas, the Dreaming City, accessible through the Entropy Sphere. Not evil (Ptolus p. 487).

### Nature of Goth Gulgamel

Goth Gulgamel is not a normal stronghold -- it is **its own demiplane**, a half-world that worms through a realm of utter darkness (the Utterdark). Various chambers anchor themselves metaphysically in the dark; many are now lost in lightless realms forever. Thus the fortress layout does not conform to the physical size and shape of the Spire (Ptolus p. 487).

**The Dread:** Any creature of good alignment suffers a -4 morale penalty to attacks, saves, and checks while within Goth Gulgamel. Those immune to fear (paladins 3rd level+) are immune to the Dread (Ptolus p. 487).

**Extradimensional restrictions:** Because Goth Gulgamel is itself an extradimensional space, characters cannot create or access other extradimensional spaces within it (bags of holding are inaccessible, Mord's magnificent mansion does not function, etc.) (Ptolus p. 487).

**Entry:** Unlike Jabel Shammar, it is possible to fly or climb up to Goth Gulgamel and enter -- the main doors are not even locked. However, conventional wisdom holds it completely sealed. Entry without extraordinary preparation is typically a death sentence (Ptolus p. 487).

### Key Areas

- **Hall of Shadows** (Area 7): A long hallway with a 50-foot vaulted ceiling, sixteen 23-foot statues between pillars, always shadowy no matter the illumination. Current home of the soul riders and their guarded human fighters (Ptolus p. 489).
- **Path of Burning Souls** (Area 14): Enormous winding cavern with lava pits, inhabited by Ochremeshk's servitors: ten salamanders led by a noble, and six hellfire giants (Ptolus pp. 491-492).
- **Entropy Sphere** (Area 25): The source of Goth Gulgamel's power -- an anchored magical vortex of chaos created by the Dread One, now damaged. The Gates of Delirium once regulated it. A pathway through it leads to Dreta Phantas. Also allows access to Jabel Shammar with the right artifacts (Ptolus p. 499).
- **Passage to Orc Caves** (Area 15): A 3,000-foot passage descending from the Path of Burning Souls to a complex beneath the Nobles' Quarter, home to 300-400 Sorn-Ulth and Toruk-Rul orc descendants of Ghul's creations (Ptolus p. 492).

**Recommended entry level:** 15th level minimum. The Banewarrens adventure (6th-10th level) is the natural predecessor (Ptolus p. 488).

## The Banewarrens (Overview)

The Banewarrens are the vast catacomb complex built by Danar Rotansin beneath his tower, Mosul Pearl -- now Jabel Shammar. They predate the Spire. For full detail, see `banewarrens.md`.

**Key relationship to the Spire:** The Baneheart (Tremoc Korin) occupies most of the interior of the Spire as a 3,000-foot shaft. The Banewarrens' Outer Vaults ring the Spire's base, and the Inner Vaults sit within that perimeter. Goth Gulgamel is connected via the Baneheart shaft -- confirmed by anyone who has explored deeply enough in the Banewarrens (Ptolus pp. 419, 487).

## DM Notes -- Secrets

**The Spire's true origin:** The Spire was not built by Ghul. It was created by a geological upheaval when Danar concentrated so much evil beneath Mosul Pearl that the earth rejected it. Ghul merely discovered and exploited what was already there. Most citizens of Ptolus do not know this (Ptolus p. 76).

**Ghul's false claims:** Ghul claimed to be the son of the Dread One -- this is false. He had no blood relation with Eslathagos Malkith, who never had children. Ghul stole the Dread One's symbol (the black skull) and even the name Skull-King. Most historians no longer remember the symbol originally belonged to the Dread One (Ptolus p. 81).

**The Dread One may not be permanently dead:** The Malignancy growing within Jabel Shammar is slowly working toward the restoration of Eslathagos Malkith via the Sombrous Sepulcher. Each figure completely corrupted by the Malignancy shortens the time remaining. A year or so is the recommended timeline for when this becomes a campaign-climax threat (Ptolus p. 504).

**Ochremeshk:** The demon god Ochremeshk was trapped in a magical prison almost 5,000 years ago. The key to his freedom lies in the damaged Entropy Sphere in Goth Gulgamel. His followers in the Path of Burning Souls await his return (Ptolus p. 486).

**Dreta Phantas:** The Urthon Aedar use the Entropy Sphere to access Dreta Phantas, the Dreaming City -- the ancient elven city stolen by dark elves and hidden beneath the earth. This is a well-guarded secret known to very few (Ptolus p. 487).

**The Banewarrens key:** An artifact made from the Dread One's own hand that can open any of the sealed doors in the Banewarrens. It currently lies in the vaults of House Vladaam, whose current members do not recognize its significance. It is also one of the three items that can access the Entropy Sphere to reach Jabel Shammar (Ptolus p. 419).
