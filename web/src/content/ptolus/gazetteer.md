# Ptolus Gazetteer

Quick-reference guide to key named locations, organized by district. One-line descriptions, access notes, costs, and hooks for each. Page citations from *Ptolus, City by the Spire*.

**City overview**: Ptolus, pop. ~75,000. Defined by a series of sharp inclines — cliffs separate many districts. The Spire dominates the western skyline. The King's River Gorge cuts through the city center. The Nobles' Quarter is highest; the Docks are lowest, at the base of the Cliffs of Lost Wishes.

Symbol of the city: **golden hawk on blue field**. *(Ptolus, p. 145–146)*

---

## The Docks

*Small waterside district at the base of the Cliffs of Lost Wishes, below the rest of the city. Primarily ship traffic, warehousing, and sailors' services. Rough reputation at night; worst of the crime lives down here.* *(Ptolus, p. 163)*

- **Dockmaster's Tower** — The strange, reclusive Dockmaster oversees all ship traffic from this harbor-front tower. Access: Harbor officials only unless you have legitimate ship business. Hook: The Dockmaster's personal eccentricities hide a significant secret.

- **Pier Five** — One of the Docks' working piers, but notable because ships that dock here occasionally suffer from unexplained disappearances of crew. Access: Open. Hook: Something is drawing people off Pier Five at night.

- **The Sailor's Rest** — The most respectable tavern in the Docks, catering to merchant sailors rather than the rougher crowds. Ale ~3 cp/mug; bed ~2 sp/night. Access: Open, all hours. Hook: Ship captains looking to hire extra muscle often post notices here.

- **Sard's Boats** — Boat repair and rental business on the waterfront. Skiff rental ~1 gp/day. Access: Open days. Hook: Sard can arrange discreet river transport, no questions asked — for a premium.

- **Savage Shark** — The roughest tavern on the Docks; fights break out nightly. Cheap ale ~2 cp. Access: Open all hours, enter armed. Hook: The Killraven Crime League uses a back room for meetings; the Balacazars have an informant at the bar.

- **Esser's** — The only legal, licensed brothel in the Docks district. Located at #234, Salt Spray Street. Proprietor: Esser Nava (human expert3). Staff of 9. Access: Pay to enter; legal and above-board. Hook: A client witnessed something they shouldn't have and has gone missing.

- **Larel's Implements** — General goods and ship supplies; maritime equipment and basic adventuring gear. Access: Open days. Cost: Standard equipment prices plus 10% (the Docks markup).

## Guildsman District

*Industrial heart of the city — tanneries, foundries, breweries, stockyards, granaries. Most local guilds are headquartered here. Distinct smell. Rough reputation deters casual visitors after dark.* *(Ptolus, p. 172)*

- **The Grand Guildhall** — Central meeting place for all the city's major guilds; large public hall used for citywide guild business and arbitration. Access: Open to guild members; public gallery on major meeting days. Hook: Guild politics are playing out in ugly ways.

- **Guildhouse of Iron** — Ironworkers' Guild headquarters; #72 on city map. Access: Guild members and business clients. *(Ptolus, p. 172 map)*

- **Masons' Guildhall** — Stonecutters and builders; maintains records of all construction in Ptolus going back centuries. Access: Open to clients; records require a fee. Hook: Old blueprints might show passages the current owners don't know about.

- **Longdraught Brewery** — One of the largest breweries in the city; supplies most taverns in the Guildsman District and Midtown. Tours available on Kingsday. Cost: Ales sold in quantity at wholesale. Hook: A shipment has been tampered with.

- **Herbalists' Guildhall** — Guild of herbalists, physickers, and apothecaries. Sells common herbal remedies and non-magical medicines. Cost: Healing herbs 1–5 gp; basic antitoxins 5 gp. Access: Open. Hook: The Guildhall has been tracking a strange illness spreading through the district.

- **The Foundry** — Enormous smelting and metalworking complex employing hundreds; fills the district with haze on working days. Access: Public entrance; working floors require business or escort. Hook: Someone is stealing raw metal in small quantities — adding up to something significant.

- **The Stockyards** — Livestock pens supplying the city with meat; also serves as informal labor-hiring market at dawn. Access: Open, smells terrible. Cost: Day labor hired here for 1 sp/day. Hook: Someone's been poisoning livestock.

- **The Midden Heaps** — City refuse dump on the district's edge; ratmen (and worse) have been seen here in daylight hours. Access: Open, dangerous at night. Hook: Something valuable was accidentally discarded — and something else is nesting in the heaps.

- **The Skull and Sword** — Only notable tavern in the Guildsman District; caters to craftsmen and guild members. Ale ~3 cp; basic meal ~5 cp. Access: Open evenings. Hook: Guild enforcers discuss intimidation jobs here.

- **Darkbirth Madhouse** — The city's institution for the mentally disturbed, located in a fortified building on the district's north side. Access: Restricted; family may visit by appointment. Hook: A patient has information about a crime but can't communicate it clearly — or is feigning madness.

- **Tabby's Den** — Underground litorian gathering place doubling as a low-stakes gambling hall; unofficial meeting point for non-human laborers. Access: Open, mostly non-human clientele; humans welcome but conspicuous. Hook: A litorian lifedebt situation has escalated to violence.

- **Ullar's Sons' Tannery** — Long-established tanning operation; also known as a discreet place to dispose of... organic material. Access: Business clients. Hook: The tannery is being blackmailed.

## Midtown

*The heart of Ptolus — largest and most diverse district. Home to Delver's Square, Tavern Row, the Undercity Market, and several residential neighborhoods. Most temporary lodgings, entertainment, and adventurer services are here.* *(Ptolus, p. 192)*

### Delver's Square (Runihan Square)
*The central hub of adventurer life in Ptolus. Named for fighter Abesh Runihan, whose 14-foot statue stands before the central fountain. The Undercity Market entrance (a wide staircase) is just behind the statue.* *(Ptolus, p. 200)*

- **The Ghostly Minstrel** — Inn, pub, and restaurant; the most famous adventurer gathering place in Ptolus. 30 guest rooms on floors 2–3. Proprietor: Vard Hillman (keeps a low profile). Front desk: Tellith Herdsman. Bartender: Zade Kenevan (remembers everyone). House bard (recent): Tarin Ursalatao. Cost: Rooms from ~1 gp/night; meals ~5 sp; ale ~5 cp. Access: Open all hours. Hook: The central gossip exchange of Delver's Square — job postings, adventurer rumor, and political intrigue all pass through the Minstrel's taproom. *(Ptolus, p. 202)*

- **Myraeth's Oddities** — The only general magic-item buy-and-sell shop in Ptolus. Owner: Myraeth Tuneweaver (elf). Buys virtually anything recovered from the dungeon; sells constantly-rotating magic stock (potions, rings, scrolls, wands, and miscellaneous items — no weapons or armor). Access: Open days. Cost: Items priced at 50% of market value when selling to Myraeth; sells at market value. Hook: Myraeth is a member of the Inverted Pyramid — his shop is also an information node. *(Ptolus, p. 213)*

- **Rastor's Weapons** — Weapon buy/sell; owner Rastor (litorian, renowned for impeccable honor). Identifies weapons by touch without need of *identify*. Sells masterwork and +1 to +3 weapons. Does not forge — buys and sells only. Access: Open days. Cost: Market value. Hook: Rastor is deeply connected to other litorians in the city and will ask a favor before doing anything unusual for the party. *(Ptolus, p. 218)*

- **Bull and Bear Armory** — Armor shop; owners Hirus and Sholum Feek (dwarf brothers). Buys used armor, creates to order, usually has +1 studded leather/chainmail/breastplate in stock. Does not make magical armor. Delver's Guild members: 10% discount. Access: Open days. Cost: Standard + crafting fees to order. *(Ptolus, p. 216)*

- **Ebbert's Outfitters** — Adventuring supplies specialist; owner Ebbert Boltcrafter (rotund dwarf). Ropes, torches, tools, signal whistles, lockpicks, chalk, and unusual gear. Does not sell weapons, armor, or magic. Delver's Guild members: 10% discount. Access: Open days. Cost: Standard prices. Hook: Ebbert sells "delver specials" — odd kits he assembled himself for specific dungeon problems. *(Ptolus, p. 216)*

- **Danbury's** — Pub catering almost exclusively to arcanists and spellcasters; proprietor Tabor Danbury; bartender Oron Bridgemaster. A Dreaming Apothecary agent is always present issuing tokens. Accessible from Delver's Square. Cost: Drinks ~5 cp; Dreaming Apothecary tokens ~prices vary. Hook: Spellcasters trade secrets here; the Inverted Pyramid informally monitors this establishment. *(Ptolus, p. 213)*

- **Potter's** — Extremely cheap hostel for broke adventurers; proprietor Winistar "Winnie" Potter (looks decades older than her mid-thirties). Beds in shared rooms. Cost: ~1 sp/night. Hook: Down-on-their-luck adventurers with debts or secrets congregate here — a good place to find desperate NPCs. *(Ptolus, p. 215)*

- **Undercity Market** — Underground market in the cavern directly below Delver's Square, accessed by the wide staircase behind the Runihan statue. The go-to for most adventuring gear, potions, and dungeon supplies. Notable vendors: Urnst (alchemist; potions), Neridoc Bittersong (gnome wizard; scroll booth). Access: Open days; some vendors operate evenings. Cost: Competitive; adventurer pricing. Hook: Strange events in the Dungeon get reported here first by returning delvers. *(Ptolus, p. 200)*

- **St. Gustav's Chapel** — Small, somewhat dingy Lothian chapel in Delver's Square; cleric Brother Fabitor Thisk (sincere, charitable, underappreciated). Sells curative potions; provides healing services (restoration, disease removal, neutralize poison). Cost: Potions at market; services at 10–25% below Cathedral rates. Hook: St. Gustav's is the unofficial "adventurer's church" — Brother Fabitor knows more about what happens below the city than most clergy. *(Ptolus, p. 221)*

### Midtown neighborhoods

- **Emerald Hill** — Midtown neighborhood where most of Ptolus's elves choose to live; upscale, quiet, forested streets. Access: Open. Hook: Elf-on-elf tensions (Shoal vs. Harrow) occasionally boil over here.

- **Narred** — Centaur (aram) neighborhood in Midtown; wide streets and high ceilings in common spaces. Access: Open; some doorways are aram-sized. Hook: An aram owes a lifedebt and has been using it to coerce.

- **The Mane** — Midtown neighborhood preferred by visiting litorians. Access: Open. Hook: A litorian honor-feud has come to Ptolus.

### Other Midtown locations

- **Iridithil's Home** — Elven community center, high-end shop, and social club for elves and their allies. Owner: Doraedian Mythlord. Sells elven goods, ithildin-work, fine wines. Cost: Premium prices; non-elves pay full price; elves get subtle discount. Access: Open to all, but non-elves feel unwelcome. Hook: The Cherubim elf Zaetra frequently visits; Doraedian has connections to the Inverted Pyramid. *(Ptolus, p. 225)*

- **Black Swan** — Dwarven pub in Midtown; the social hub for Stonelost dwarves and their business associates. Ale ~3 cp. Access: Open. Hook: Stonelost community news travels fastest here.

- **Tavern Row** — Street lined with numerous taverns of all quality levels; runs through central Midtown. Notable establishments: The Onyx Spider, The Goat, The Griffon, North Point Restaurant. Access: Open.

- **The Cloud Theater** — Main performance venue in Midtown; stages plays, concerts, and civic events. Access: Ticketed performances (1–5 sp). Hook: A playwright has embedded coded messages about a cult in their new production.

- **Row Bathhouse** — Public bath and social space; popular with adventurers returning from the dungeon who need to wash off dungeon grime. Cost: Bath ~5 cp; private room ~5 sp. Access: Open during daylight hours. Hook: Bathhouse attendants overhear a great deal.

## The Necropolis

*The city's cemetery, enclosed by walls, and far more dangerous than it appears. Effectively safe during the day; extremely dangerous at night. The Forsaken (living demon-worshippers) and the Fallen (actual demons) occupy the Dark Reliquary. Undead of all kinds wander after dark. The City Watch does not patrol inside.* *(Ptolus, p. 215)*

- **Clasthamus Isle** — Small island within the Necropolis lagoon; site of the Stones of Thamus, magical standing stones that weaken undead once per year (on Godsday, the barrier drops). Access: Reachable by boat; dangerous. Hook: The annual Godsday weakening of the stones is when demon raids on the isle have historically occurred. *(Ptolus, p. 219)*

- **Dark Reliquary** — Enormous manor house serving as the headquarters of the Forsaken (demon-allied mortals) and the Fallen (actual fiends including demons). The Wintersouled undead are allied with them. The manor has above-ground levels and at least 3 dungeon levels below. Access: Heavily guarded; entering uninvited is a death sentence. Hook: Central antagonist location for the Night of Dissolution plot. Notable inhabitants: Raguel and Lilith (demon lords). *(Ptolus, p. 232)*

- **Hall of the Valiant** — Mausoleum for noted heroes of Ptolus; includes statues and tomb-crypts for past Guildmaster Delvers and city defenders. Access: Open days; uneasy at night. Hook: A recent hero's tomb was desecrated.

- **The Siege Tower** — Abandoned watchtower in the Necropolis, now used as a lair by intelligent undead. Access: Open but unwise. Hook: The tower's top floor contains a room of prophecy that activates under moonlight.

- **Tower of Terephon** — A crumbling wizard's tower; rumored to contain a still-functioning magical library. Access: Hazardous; the tower actively resists entry with traps. Hook: The wizard Terephon vanished 200 years ago and left dangerous experiments behind.

- **Alchestrin's Tomb** — Elaborate tomb of Alchestrin the wizard (later took over Castle Shard). Access: Sealed; known entrances are warded. Hook: Alchestrin's tomb connects to deeper Dungeon levels via a hidden passage.

- **Crypt Home of Igor Reichstav** — One of the Necropolis's odder residents: Igor maintains a crypt as his permanent home (he's alive). Access: Knock. Hook: Igor trades information about the Necropolis's inhabitants in exchange for company and interesting stories.

## Nobles' Quarter

*Atop the highest cliffs in Ptolus, accessible only through Dalenguard in Oldtown. Lush estates, clean streets, wide lawns. The City Watch here is visibly more attentive. Population ~3,000. Adventurers are unwelcome unless clearly employed by a noble or of equivalent status.* *(Ptolus, p. 280)*

- **Castle Shard** — Magical keep that has been the seat of ancient evil and is now a force for good, led by Lord Zavere. The keep itself is a sentient magical artifact (bound by a great shard of crystallized magic). Its internal space is larger than the exterior suggests. Access: By invitation only; Zavere can vouch for worthy adventurers. Hook: Castle Shard is the party's most powerful potential ally against Ptolus's evil — Zavere opposes the Vladaams and the chaos cults. Being accepted as a lord of Castle Shard is a campaign-level achievement. *(Ptolus, p. 285)*

- **Holy Palace** — Residence of the Emperor of the Church (the Prince of the Church; also claiming the temporal Imperial throne). Located on Crown Street. Heavily guarded by Church paladins and LG clerics. Access: Appointment required for audience; public entrance on holy days. Hook: The Prince of the Church has an agenda that may or may not align with the party's. *(Ptolus, p. 292)*

- **Noble Estates** — Ten noble family estates in the quarter. Most relevant to adventurers:
  - *House Vladaam*: The most corrupt family; connected to chaos cults and the Balacazars. Avoid unless you want powerful enemies. *(p. 303)*
  - *House Sadar*: Allied with Vladaam (secretly); dangerous. *(p. 295)*
  - *House Khatru*: Rival of many houses; politically aggressive. *(p. 290)*
  - *House Nagel*: Charitable, altruistic, nearly broke. *(p. 295)*
  - *House Dallimothan*: Allied with Kath and Nagel; opposed to Vladaam. *(p. 286)*
  - *House Abanar*: Wealthy merchant family, no major alliances. *(p. 282)*

- **Rosegate House** — A notable Nobles' Quarter estate used as a neutral meeting ground between rival factions. Access: By arrangement. Hook: A critical negotiation will take place here that the party may need to influence or protect. *(Ptolus, p. 298)*

## North Market

*Open-air market district; known for stalls, tents, and wagons rather than permanent shops. Farmers, individual artisans, and exotic-goods merchants. Also a residential district. The Zar'at, the Harrow elf neighborhood, is here.* *(Ptolus, p. 311)*

- **The Market (open stalls)** — Hundreds of merchants selling produce, livestock, handcrafted goods, spices, and miscellaneous wares. Access: Dawn to dusk daily. Cost: 90% of PHB prices for most mundane goods; haggling applies. Hook: A merchant is selling items "recovered" from a noble's home.

- **Red Stallion Pub** — The most popular gathering place in the North Market; workers, merchants, and middle-class residents. Ale ~3 cp; meal ~3 sp. Access: Open. Hook: Political gossip and labor organizing happen here; the City Watch maintains an informant.

- **The Smoke Shop** — Sells and repairs firearms, clockwork devices, and other declining Imperial technology. Very few smiths still capable of this work remain. Access: Open. Cost: Firearms at Ptolus equipment prices; repairs expensive and slow. Hook: The Smoke Shop is a front for Shuul (pro-technology faction) operations. *(Ptolus, p. 316)*

- **Killraven's Tower** — The base of operations for Kevris Killraven's growing criminal empire; appeared in 720 IA, one year ago. Access: Killraven's people only. Hook: Killraven is actively expanding into Balacazar territory — her operations create chaos that adventurers can exploit or become caught in. *(Ptolus, p. 315)*

- **Rogue Moon Trading Company** — Major merchant organization with its own warehouse complex; deals in commodities and overseas trade. Access: Open for business. Hook: One of the company's senior partners is deeply in debt to the Balacazars. *(Ptolus, p. 320)*

- **Star Crossing Plaza** — Large public plaza in the North Market used for civic events, festivals, and impromptu markets. The Godsday festival spills into this plaza. Access: Open. Hook: Political rallies and public announcements happen here.

- **Exotic Market** — Section of the North Market dealing in unusual and foreign goods — rare spices, foreign weapons, strange animals, unusual textiles. Access: Open. Hook: Someone is selling what appears to be chaositech under the table. *(Ptolus, p. 317 area)*

- **Zar'at** — The Harrow elf neighborhood of North Market; insular and dangerous to visit uninvited. Access: Open to all but heavily watched by residents. Hook: A Harrow elf with information about the dungeon is willing to trade — for the right price. *(Ptolus, p. 310)*

## Oldtown

*The oldest part of Ptolus and its governmental center. Sits atop Dalen's Cliffs, connected to Nobles' Quarter via Dalenguard. Government buildings, the Imperial University, and the Inverted Pyramid are all here. More orderly than most districts; City Watch presence is high.* *(Ptolus, p. 320)*

- **Dalenguard** — The great fortress of Ptolus; built after Ghul's defeat to guard Goth Gulgamel's base. Now serves as the Commissar's headquarters, barracks, and city administrative center. The only way into the Nobles' Quarter passes through Dalenguard. Access: Public sections open; military and government sections require authorization. Key NPC: Commissar Igor Urnst (appointed Imperial governor; chairs the City Council). Hook: The Commissar's Imperial Eyes spy network is headquartered here — they're watching everyone. *(Ptolus, p. 320)*

- **Inverted Pyramid** — The world's most powerful gathering of arcanists; their headquarters is an invisible floating tower somewhere above Oldtown (exact location changes). The Pyramid operates above the city's political hierarchy. Members may be any alignment. Access: Membership requires demonstrated power and a candidacy period; outsiders may petition for specific services. Thoughtstone network: Members (12th level+) can communicate citywide via thoughtstones (10,000 gp; requires activation by the Pyramid). Hook: The Inverted Pyramid is both the party's most powerful magical resource and a wildcard — their goals may or may not align with saving the city. *(Ptolus, p. 325)*

- **Administration Building** — Houses city government offices including City Courts, licensing, taxes, permits, and records. Access: Public during business hours (Theoday–Fireday). Cost: Most city licenses 5–50 gp. Hook: A crucial permit or legal record has been falsified.

- **Imperial University** — The city's university; offers training in arcane magic, history, engineering, and other academic subjects. The library is the most extensive in Ptolus. Access: Students and faculty; library access for a fee (~1 gp/day for non-members). Hook: A scholar has uncovered something in the archives that someone powerful wants buried.

- **Clock Tower** — An enormous mechanical clock built by the Grailwarden dwarves; one of the few still-functioning examples of high Imperial technology. Access: Public observation at the base; clockwork mechanism restricted. Hook: The clock has been off by minutes — significant for someone performing a time-sensitive ritual.

- **The Bladechapel** — A training facility and shrine maintained by a martial order; open to fighters and paladins for practice and worship. Access: Open to fighters. Hook: A retired veteran here knows something about a hidden dungeon entrance.

- **Kadmiel** — A tower that exists only within its own shadow; one of the stranger magical oddities of Oldtown. Access: The entrance exists only when approached from a specific direction at dawn. Hook: Inside Kadmiel, time runs differently — an hour inside may be a day outside, or vice versa.

- **Church of Lothian the Redeemer** — One of nine Lothian churches in the city; the Oldtown branch is significant for its proximity to government. Cleric duties: Official functions, witnessing of legal documents, Imperial ceremonies. Access: Open. Cost: Standard tithing expected. Hook: A government official is secretly in conflict with the Church hierarchy.

- **Citadel of Might** — Barracks and training ground for elite city defenders; associated with the Order of the Fist (monks) and similar martial orders. Access: Members and applicants. Hook: A renowned retired adventurer trains here and knows where a specific dungeon feature is located.

## Rivergate District

*Primarily residential; home to Ptolus's middle class — merchants, craftspeople, clerks, and their families. Isolated by the King's River Gorge on one side and a steep incline on the other. Quiet by Ptolus standards.* *(Ptolus, p. 381)*

- **Chapel of St. Thessina** — Small Lothian chapel serving the Rivergate community; low-key, community-focused. Access: Open. Cost: Donations welcome. Hook: A parishioner has confessed to something the cleric can't ignore.

- **Well of the Shadow Eyes** — A deep public well in Rivergate with a strange history — reflections in the water sometimes show scenes from elsewhere or elsewhen. Access: Public. Hook: A vision in the well led someone to Rivergate looking for an NPC or location.

- **Finelle's Pleasant Diversions** — An upscale entertainment establishment offering gambling, music, and companionable conversation — more refined than the city's rougher establishments. Cost: Entry 5 sp; services priced accordingly. Access: Well-dressed clientele preferred. Hook: The Conciliators (mediators organization) use Finelle's as a meeting location.

## South Market

*Newer than the North Market, distinguished by permanent shops with attached workshops. Residential as well as commercial. Somewhat more respectable than the North Market.* *(Ptolus, p. 401)*

- **Blackstock Printing** — The city's premier printing house; produces broadsheets, books, official documents. The proprietors hold a significant secret related to what they've been asked to print. Access: Business clients. Cost: Printing services 5–50 gp depending on job. Hook: A revolutionary political pamphlet is being secretly printed here. *(Ptolus, p. 404)*

- **Golden Tooth** — Shop specializing in gold goods — jewelry, art objects, and gold-plated equipment. Also provides appraisal services. Access: Open. Cost: Gold jewelry 50–500 gp; appraisal 2 gp/item. Hook: A piece of stolen noble jewelry has ended up here. *(Ptolus, p. 406)*

- **The Cock Pit** — Underground gambling den in the South Market; illegal but tolerated due to Balacazar bribes to the Watch. Pit fights and card games. Access: Word of mouth; located below a legitimate shop. Cost: Buy-in for tables from 5 gp. Hook: A debtor is hiding here; the gambling den's owner has connections to Korben Trollone. *(Ptolus, p. 408)*

- **Church of Lothian, South Market (St. Chausle's Chapel)** — Standard Lothian chapel serving the residential district. Access: Open. Hook: The resident cleric has been receiving threats from an unknown party.

## Temple District

*Dense concentration of religious buildings from dozens of faiths. The Street of a Million Gods runs through it. Strange place where benevolent temples sit next to hidden chaos cult shrines. The Sisterhood of Silence is based here.* *(Ptolus, p. 356)*

- **St. Valien's Cathedral** — The primary Cathedral of Lothian in Ptolus; the city's largest and most impressive religious building. Weekly true resurrection rite performed on Theoday by Archbishop Adlam Theobold (cleric20); appointment only; 3+ week wait. Lothian's domains: Good, Law, Protection, Sun. Clerics wear white and blue (gold on high holy days). Access: Open to all for services; restricted areas require Church credentials. Cost: Healing and services at PHB prices; resurrection by appointment (~quoted to party). Hook: The Archbishop is being pressured politically; someone wants the resurrection list interfered with. *(Ptolus, p. 376)*

- **Stormwrought Campanile** — An enormous bell tower that can produce incredible musical variations; maintained by campanologist Marved Sallin (expert5/sorcerer9) and 3–4 assistants. Various faiths pay to have their call to prayer sounded from the campanile. Access: Public ground level; bell room restricted. Cost: Various faiths pay 10–50 gp/month for ringing services. Hook: Someone has hidden something inside the campanile's bell mechanism.  *(Ptolus, p. 377)*

- **Priory of Introspection** — Headquarters of the Sisterhood of Silence — the quasi-official auxiliary law enforcement organization of Ptolus. Monastic in appearance; internal discipline is strict. Access: Petitioners may request the Sisterhood's assistance (they apprehend criminals and deal out justice without involving the Watch). Hook: The Sisterhood has a file on every major criminal in the city and knows things the City Watch doesn't. *(Ptolus, p. 359)*

- **Hall of Heroes** — Public hall featuring statues of the Great Seven (the heroes who slew Ghul) and other notable figures from Ptolus history. Open-air sections display monuments to fallen adventurers. Access: Open, free. Hook: One of the statues has been defaced; the act was a message. *(Ptolus, p. 372)*

- **Celestial Conclave** — Headquarters of the Malkuth, an organization of actual angelic beings (aasimars and celestials) who've chosen to take up residence in Ptolus to oppose its evil. Access: By introduction. Hook: The Conclave is aware of demonic activity the party hasn't detected yet.

- **Temple of Navashtrom** — Temple of Navashtrom, God of Strength and Harmony (NG); popular with the city's warriors and adventurers. Access: Open. Hook: a champion of the faith needs capable hands for a dangerous errand. *(Note: the minotaur cleric **Shibata** serves a different deity — **Niveral, God of Secrets** — and will answer one question truthfully for a price, his loyalty always to Niveral first. PT5 p. 397.)*

- **Temple of Gaen** — Temple of Gaen, Goddess of Light (LG); one of the largest, most impressive temples in the city. Associated with the champion Steron Vsool (whose heart glows visibly within his chest). Access: Open. Hook: Vsool may be the party's most reliable source of information about celestial threats in the city. *(Ptolus, p. 385–386)*

- **Temple of Ahaar** — Temple of the chaotic good god of air and freedom; one of the few non-Lothian faiths that is both powerful and openly chaotic good. Access: Open, no restrictions. Hook: Cherubim elves pay homage to Ahaar; this is where to find them if needed.

- **Street of a Million Gods** — The district's main thoroughfare; lined with temples, shrines, and chapels representing dozens of faiths including many obscure or exotic ones. Access: Open, always busy. Hook: A new shrine appeared overnight that wasn't there yesterday — dedicated to something that has no name in any pantheon the PCs recognize.

- **Temple of the Rat God** *(hidden)* — Secret chaos cult shrine operating below a legitimate business in the Temple District. Access: Cult members only; requires introduction. Hook: The rat cult has connections to the ratman population in the sewers and to a deeper chaos organization. *(Ptolus, p. 397)*

- **Temple of the Ebon Hand** *(hidden)* — Chaos cult operating in the Temple District; connected to strange disappearances in the city. Access: Cult members. Hook: The Ebon Hand is responsible for a series of kidnappings — the party may be hired to investigate. *(Ptolus, p. 399)*

## The Warrens

*The smallest and worst district — a lawless, hivelike maze of crumbling buildings, gangs, poverty, and unchecked crime. No street addresses or street names. The City Watch patrols the perimeter but does not enter.* *(Ptolus, p. 426)*

- **Jirraith's home** — The home of Jirraith, a crime lord known for having no discernible appearance (suspected illusionist or shapechanger). Controls much of the Warrens' criminal activity. Access: Finding Jirraith is itself a challenge; he doesn't want to be found. Hook: Jirraith has information about dungeon passages beneath the Warrens — and a standing offer to anyone who can retrieve a specific item from level 4 of the Dungeon. *(Ptolus, p. 430)*

- **Eight Shadows HQ** — Headquarters of a powerful assassins' guild operating citywide out of the Warrens. Access: Clients only; making contact requires a complex series of dead-drops. Cost: Assassination contract starts at 500 gp. Hook: The Eight Shadows have been hired to kill someone the party knows. *(Ptolus, p. 433)*

## Undercity locations (not a district, but accessed from the city)

- **Delver's Guild** — The main organizing body for adventurers in Ptolus; not exactly one location but offices exist in Delver's Square (surface) and the Undercity Market (below). Guildmaster Delver: Sorum Dandubal. Membership grants 10% discount at Ebbert's Outfitters and Bull and Bear. Membership requires completing a basic delve and paying dues (~10 gp/year). Hook: The Guild maintains maps of the sewer levels and upper Dungeon; access to better maps is a major quest reward. *(Ptolus, p. 200)*

- **The Sewers** — The first level of the Undercity; accessed from street grates (Open Lock DC 20). Infested with ratmen, oozes, and worse. City System Monitors (commoners) and their Rat Hunter sub-unit maintain the tunnels and pay 3 gp/ratman tail as bounty. Hook: The sewers connect to the Dungeon proper, making them the most accessible entry point for beginning delvers.

- **Banewarrens entrance** — The sealed vault beneath Ptolus containing powerful evil artifacts created by the ancient wizard Danar (later known as the Dread One). Multiple factions want it opened — and others want it to stay sealed. Access: Hidden; finding the entrance is a significant quest in itself. Hook: The Night of Dissolution centers on forces trying to open the Banewarrens. *(Ptolus, p. 486; The Banewarrens sourcebook)*
