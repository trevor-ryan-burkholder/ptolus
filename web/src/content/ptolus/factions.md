# Factions and Organizations of Ptolus

*Canonical reference extracted from Ptolus: City by the Spire (PT3: Organizations). Page citations are print book page numbers.*

## Noble Houses

Ptolus has exactly **ten noble families**. Each holds one hereditary seat on the City Council Assembly, granted by the Commissar roughly three centuries ago. All noble houses predate the city itself — most are thousands of years old. Disputes between houses are adjudicated by the **Last King** (the spirit of slain King Rissathion, imprisoned in the King's Stone at the base of the Spire) under the Ancient Rites of Custom. (Ptolus p. 87–88)

### House Abanar
**Type:** Noble house (mercantile)
**Head:** Lord Dered Abanar (male human aristocrat10) — very old, palsied, runs house by proxy from a magical floating chair; never leaves the estate
**Description:** Largest and wealthiest noble house; membership can be purchased outright (~10,000 gp for a minor title), giving it up to 100 titled members and ~200 extended associates — far more than any other house. Known for greed and ruthlessness.
**Goals:** Accumulate and protect wealth; punish thieves aggressively (bounties of 500–5,000 gp on those who steal from Abanar holdings).
**Allies:** None among noble houses. Works with Bankers' Guild. Longfingers Guild finances flow partly through House Rau, but Abanar-Rau relations are cold.
**Enemies:** House Nagel, House Khatru (political friction).
**Disposition to PCs:** Transactional. Will hire adventurers to collect bounties or escort shipments. Will attempt to ruin anyone who crosses them.
**HQ:** Estate in the Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 296).
*(Ptolus p. 88–89)*

### House Dallimothan
**Type:** Noble house (draconic lineage)
**Head:** Lord Kirstol Dallimothan — an old silver dragon who maintains a human form (CR 20 in either form). Has cycled through false identities as "his own father and grandfather" over 400+ years.
**Description:** Called "House Dragon" by commoners, though no house member acknowledges the name. The family genuinely descends from dragons; current membership includes 5 half-dragons and 6 full-blooded dragons (4 silver, 1 gold, 1 bronze). Trait weapon: the **Sword of the Dragonkings** (+5 keen bastard sword).
**Goals:** Benevolent coexistence with all sentient beings; opposition to chromatic dragons preying on the weak; acquisition of any *orbs of dragonkind* (offer 50,000 gp each, then destroy them). Never mention Father Claw by name.
**Allies:** Kath, Nagel. Kirstol is personally friendly with Andach the druid and leading Malkuth.
**Enemies:** House Vladaam (bitter rivalry). Will not work with Knights of the Golden Cross (former dragon slayers).
**Disposition to PCs:** Generally helpful if approached respectfully. May send PCs on errands (e.g., deliveries to Andach in the Necropolis).
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter; features a unique tower (PT5: p. 296).
**[DM SECRET]** Kirstol is not actually a different man from "his father Phord" and "grandfather Hallos" — he has maintained the same lineage for over 400 years.
*(Ptolus p. 89–91)*

### House Erthuo
**Type:** Noble house (scholarly, elven-aligned)
**Head:** Peliope Erthuo (female half-elf aristocrat9)
**Description:** Scholars and gentlefolk; avoids political confrontation. The family has gradually become elven in culture through marriages — currently 3 humans, 7 half-elves, 3 elves among titled members. Possesses one of the finest collections of rare books and historical artifacts in the region.
**Goals:** Scholarly preservation; neutrality in house politics.
**Allies:** Sadar (secret, due to Renn Sadar's affair with Peliope).
**Enemies:** None active.
**Disposition to PCs:** Friendly but reclusive. May hire PCs to acquire rare items or books.
**[DM SECRET]** Peliope is having a long-term affair with Lord Renn Sadar (married), which, if discovered, would drag Erthuo into Sadar's political orbit against Erthuo's interests.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 297).
*(Ptolus p. 91)*

### House Kath
**Type:** Noble house (arts and culture)
**Head:** Lady Devina Kath (female human expert14) — composer, poet, widely admired
**Description:** The glamour house of Ptolus. Members are beautiful, charming, and artistically accomplished. Administers the Imperial Academy of Music (Conservatory) and owns the Crown Theater. Recruits talented young city folk as house associates, trained free in exchange for loyalty.
**Goals:** Patronage and control of the arts; cultural influence; keeping the Knights of the Chord strong.
**Allies:** Dallimothan, Nagel, Sadar. Strong ancient ties to the Knights of the Chord.
**Enemies:** Abanar, Khatru, Rau, Vladaam.
**Disposition to PCs:** Friendly; will hire for escort/investigation work, especially protecting notable performers.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 297).
*(Ptolus p. 92)*

### House Khatru
**Type:** Noble house (military)
**Head:** Lord Dorant Khatru (male human fighter20, CR 21) — actually 89 years old but appears ~40; owns the Brooch of Khatru (minor artifact). One of the Commissar's Twelve Commanders.
**Description:** Proud warriors who believe might makes right. Maintain a private army of ~100 well-trained soldiers (also members of the Order of Iron Might). Despise spellcasters.
**Goals:** Military supremacy; support of the Empire; opposition to arcane practitioners.
**Allies:** Shever. Close to the Commissar.
**Enemies:** Abanar (minor), Kath (minor), Rau (deep enmity — held back only by the Ancient Rites of Custom), Sadar, Vladaam.
**Disposition to PCs:** Respects martial prowess; dismisses mages. Will hire muscle. Will be hostile if PCs insult their martial code.
**[DM SECRET]** Dorant's youthful appearance is from a spell cast by an elven lover 60+ years ago, granting him elven lifespan. He is unaware of the cause.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 297).
*(Ptolus p. 92–93)*

### House Nagel
**Type:** Noble house (declining)
**Head:** Lady Fransin Nagel (female human aristocrat7) — acting head while her husband is imprisoned
**Description:** An older house with a historic reputation for altruism and charity, now in dire straits. Lord Kurtlan Nagel (aristocrat11) is imprisoned on a framed murder charge engineered by House Sadar; prison warden Odsen Rom is in Sadar's pocket. The family is reduced to Kurtlan's doddering uncle Yarek, his sickly daughter Nilla, and a handful of others. Most servants have been let go.
**Goals:** Recover Lord Kurtlan; restore house honor and finances.
**Allies:** Dallimothan, Kath, Knights of the Golden Cross (historically; ties have faded). Lady Fransin is close friends with Doraedian Mythlord of Iridithil's Home.
**Enemies:** House Sadar (deep enmity).
**Disposition to PCs:** Actively seeking allies and aid. Fransin will appeal to well-known PCs for help recovering her husband.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 298). Ancient house crest is held in a Longfingers Guild vault (PT7: p. 434).
*(Ptolus p. 93)*

### House Rau
**Type:** Noble house (criminal underworld management)
**Head:** Verrana Rau (female human rogue12); son Kalbir (rogue6/sorcerer6) assists
**Description:** Known historically as pirates and rogues. Today they operate "soft crime" — financing criminal operations for a cut, managing gambling dens and bordellos, brokering underworld deals. They maintain a fleet of ships. Family sorcery runs toward illusion, enchantment, and abjuration.
**Goals:** Profit through underworld brokerage; political leverage through their council seat; expand influence without direct crime.
**Allies:** Abanar. Financial backers of the Longfingers Guild. Work with but maintain some distance from the Balacazars.
**Enemies:** Kath, Khatru (deep enmity; Khatru would kill them if not for the Ancient Rites of Custom).
**Disposition to PCs:** Will deal honestly if it profits them; will betray for a better offer. Excellent contacts in both noble and criminal worlds.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 298). Fleet based in the Docks.
*(Ptolus p. 93–94)*

### House Sadar
**Type:** Noble house (arcane / shadow magic)
**Head:** Lord Renn Sadar (male human wizard20, CR 20, Lawful Evil) — rarely appears publicly; sends proxy Naephos Worldsea (elf aristocrat4/wizard8). High-ranking Inverted Pyramid member.
**Description:** Called the House of Shadows. Family is "shadow-tainted" — innate affinity for shadow magic but averse to light. Persecuted during the Church's Days of Blood; saved in part by the Inverted Pyramid. Members may be shadowdancer mages or shadowdancer assassins. Renn wields the **shadowstaff**.
**Goals:** Locate the **box of shadows** (a Banewarren artifact lost to history before it could be interred). Expand arcane power. Maintain Inverted Pyramid influence.
**Allies:** Vladaam, Kath, Inverted Pyramid, Erthuo (secret).
**Enemies:** Nagel.
**Disposition to PCs:** Will use or manipulate PCs to locate the box of shadows or acquire arcane lore. Hostile if crossed.
**[DM SECRET]** Renn is having an affair with Peliope Erthuo. His wife Enis Sadar (aristocrat12) is oblivious and is actually a positive force — influential in the Church of Lothian's Order of Dayra, helping the underprivileged. Renn has a secret alliance with the Holy Emperor (PT5: p. 294).
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 298).
*(Ptolus p. 94–95)*

### House Shever
**Type:** Noble house (technology / machines)
**Head:** Thollos Shever (male human expert16) — skilled technologist but spends all his time in his workshop. His wife Redalla (aristocrat7) actually runs house business.
**Description:** The youngest noble house, and the one most connected to the Empire — they helped build Prustan technology. Now fading as technological knowledge declines. Thollos's brother Marcad (aristocrat3/fighter4) has no mechanical talent and can often be found at the Arena or gambling dens.
**Goals:** Revive technology and strengthen the Empire; partnering with the Shuul to this end.
**Allies:** Khatru (historically), Shuul.
**Enemies:** Vladaam (latent).
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral; will pay for recovered technological artifacts or information.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 298).
*(Ptolus p. 96)*

### House Vladaam
**Type:** Noble house (demonic / evil)
**Head:** Iristul Vladaam (male tiefling fighter8/blackguard10) — currently abroad seeking the six **hungerswords**. In his absence, daughter **Navanna Vladaam** (female tiefling fighter5/rogue6, CR 12) runs the house.
**Other members:** Gattara, Godfred (fighter13/blackguard, wields one hungersword, CR 18+), Aliaster. All are Iristul's children; all their mothers are dead.
**Description:** The oldest noble house and the most corrupt. Lineage traced to the **Natharl'nacna** (servants of the Galchutt). Funds criminal operations including theft, extortion, slavery, drug trade, and assassination. Even among the city's demons, Vladaam stands out for malevolence.
**Goals:** Awaken the Natharl'nacna/Galchutt; access Jabel Shammar atop the Spire; collect all six hungerswords (Iristul has a spell that, cast on all six at once, makes them annihilate anything they touch).
**Allies:** Sadar, Balacazars, chaos cults, Fallen, Forsaken.
**Enemies:** Dallimothan, Khatru, most other houses, Knights of the Golden Cross (ancient enmity from the founding Vladaam's clash with the order).
**Disposition to PCs:** Hostile. Navanna maintains numerous false identities in the city (including "Nicalon Regelis," a Knight of the Chord) to spy on enemies. Her agents are urban werewolves.
**[DM SECRET]** Navanna was a key antagonist in the Banewarrens (see PT7). Iristul's hungersword collection could become an endgame threat if he finds all six.
**HQ:** Estate in Nobles' Quarter (PT5: p. 298); guarded by standard werewolves.
*(Ptolus p. 96–100)*

## Criminal Organizations

### Balacazar Crime Family
**Type:** Criminal (organized crime family)
**Head:** Menon Balacazar (aging, secretive patriarch — rarely seen; controls through layers of subordinates). Second: Malkeen Balacazar (heir apparent; meets new contacts at the Onyx Spider tavern in Midtown). Other children: Maystra (has her own plans) and Fesamere ("Fes" — surprisingly not evil, nearly ready to betray the family to Killraven).
**Description:** Probably the most powerful crime family in the city, certainly the oldest. Organized crime across all districts: gambling, prostitution, extortion, thievery, enforcement. Each district has a Crime Boss and 2–3 Underbosses (see table below). Menon possesses the **Dread Cloak** (evil artifact, requires bathing it in human blood weekly) and holds minor goddess **Unnah** captive in a magic candle, coercing her warrior-cultists (the **Hussar**) to serve as enforcers.
**Goals:** Maintain dominance of Ptolus underworld; suppress Killraven threat; Menon eventually wants to retire (to Malkeen's succession).
**Allies:** House Vladaam, House Rau (political arrangement), Pale Dogs (recruiting ground), chaos cults (occasional). The Commissar maintains an uneasy tolerance — he fears he might not win a direct war.
**Enemies:** Killraven Crime League (active gang war).
**Disposition to PCs:** Will use PCs opportunistically. Low-level PCs who cross the family face assassination. High-level PCs may be courted as assets.
**[DM SECRET]** Malkeen has a "vampire of the void" bodyguard named Na'haras (CR 18) — an ancient assassin from Kem reconstituted as undead. Fesamere is a potential turncoat. The trapped goddess Unnah is real.

### Killraven Crime League
**Type:** Criminal (new criminal organization)
**Head:** Kevris Killraven — arrived in Ptolus only recently; rides a nightmare cauchemar named Tallix. Operates out of Malasir (PT5: p. 301).
**Description:** A fast-growing rival to the Balacazars. Members call themselves "us" — the name "Killraven Crime League" was coined by outsiders. Currently in an active gang war with the Balacazars. Killraven is the lynchpin: if she dies, the organization collapses.
**Goals:** Displace the Balacazars; expand control; build alliances with the Pactlords of the Quaan for nonhumanoid muscle.
**Allies:** Controls the Sorn (magical enforcers) and the Shuul (technology organization). Quiet support from Houses Shever and factions of Abanar.
**Enemies:** Balacazars (gang war), Inverted Pyramid, the Vai, Houses Sadar and Vladaam, Forsaken and chaos cults (lesser opposition).
**Disposition to PCs:** Will offer PCs work to strike at Balacazar interests. Politically acceptable to fight — unlike the Balacazars, they have no protection from the Commissar's forces.
*(Ptolus p. 121–123)*

### Pale Dogs
**Type:** Criminal (street gang)
**Head:** Jirraith — a doppelganger. No one knows his true form; he is identifiable only by a magical ruby ring. Usually communicates through intermediaries. Works directly for Menon Balacazar.
**Description:** The largest gang in the Warrens. Members have shaved heads and black-painted fingernails; some stain their teeth black. Almost exclusively young males (humans, some half-orcs and half-elves). Jirraith culls members: the most bloodthirsty are sold to the Vai, the most promising go to Balacazar service, the rest stay in the Warrens and die young.
**Goals:** Control the Warrens for the Balacazars; serve as a recruiting/proving ground.
**Disposition to PCs:** Hostile; will attack in numbers. Individual Pale Dogs are CR 1–3.
*(Ptolus p. 131–132)*

---

### Longfingers Guild
**Type:** Criminal (thieves' guild)
**Head:** Hayman Knapp (male human rogue20, CR 20) — aging guildmaster; old-fashioned honor-among-thieves philosophy.
**Description:** Once Ptolus's premier thieves' guild, now declining as most skilled thieves prefer Balacazar or Killraven employment. Still serves as a training school for young thieves and a network for those who prefer guild ethics. Membership gives a 10% cut to the guild in exchange for training, supplies, and legal cover. Finances come substantially from House Rau.
**Goals:** Maintain the guild as a viable institution; uphold the old code of thievery ethics; protect weaker thieves from gang exploitation.
**Allies:** House Rau (financial backer).
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral to friendly if PCs don't steal from guild members. Will provide fencing and underworld contacts for a cut.
**HQ:** Sprawling underground headquarters (PT7: p. 429); location in Midtown below the Undercity Market.
*(Ptolus p. 128)*

## Arcane Organizations

### Inverted Pyramid
**Type:** Arcane (secretive wizards' organization)
**Leadership:** No single leader is publicly known. Most prominent named members include Moynath Autumnsong (PT5: p. 288), **Jevicca Nor** (female human transmuter17/IP master1, CR 18, Neutral Good — based in Ptolus, accessible to PCs), and Lord Renn Sadar (Lawful Evil). The Pyramid has three associated prestige classes (PT6: p. 650).
**Description:** The preeminent arcane organization in the world. Founded to protect mages during the Church's persecution (the Days of Blood). Relocated to Ptolus in 615 IA when the Commissar offered sanctuary. Members include most of the powerful arcanists in Ptolus. Extremely secretive — the public does not know where the headquarters is. The building itself is a literal inverted pyramid floating above the city, reachable only by teleportation.
**Goals:** Preserve arcane knowledge; foster magical study; protect arcanists from religious persecution (which they fear could resume). Watches the Church of Lothian closely and with distrust.
**Allies:** House Sadar (through Renn Sadar), House Vladaam (by extension). Tolerates dealings with the Balacazars for practical reasons.
**Enemies:** The Killraven Crime League (active). Church of Lothian (wary mutual coexistence).
**Services to members:** Quarters and laboratories in the Pyramid; arcane knowledge and contacts; the **Dreaming Apothecary** (magic items ordered through a dream-state broker — payment extracted magically from wherever the client keeps their money). Higher-ranked members can make requests of lower-ranked members.
**Disposition to PCs:** Will hire capable arcanist PCs or deal with PCs who have arcane information to trade. Hostile to those who probe their secrets.
**Info checks (Gather Information or Knowledge [local]):** Existence and origins (DC 15); floating inverted pyramid headquarters, teleportation-only access (DC 25).
*(Ptolus p. 115–119)*

### Brides of Magic
**Type:** Arcane (elite female sorcerers)
**Leadership:** No hierarchy. Most prominent member is Lady Rill of Castle Shard (no authority over others).
**Description:** A tiny, elite organization of female sorcerers. Joining requires giving up all romantic and familial ties to devote oneself entirely to magic. Members meet monthly (at the full moon of Lunas) at a secret, rotating location. They share no secrets from each other.
**Goals:** Maintain the balance of magical flow in the world — neither too much good-aligned nor evil-aligned magic. Oppose any massive concentration of aligned magical power (including the Banewarrens). Prepare for a future event they believe will alter or destroy magic.
**Disposition to PCs:** Almost no one knows they exist. They do not take overt group action; instead they work subtle long-term influence. A PC who is a female sorcerer might be approached.
*(Ptolus p. 106–107)*

### Urthon Aedar
**Type:** Arcane / esoteric (Elder Elf mystics)
**Leadership:** Led by the elf wizard Khelaeson (from Dreta Phantas).
**Description:** Ancient Elder Elves, believed extinct by the world, who survived Ghul's torture at Goth Gulgamel. Ghul cursed them with visions of the future to torment them, but they endured — and escaped when Goth Gulgamel fell, using the Entropy Sphere in the Spire to reach Dreta Phantas (the stolen elven city). Their name means "Wandering Judges" in Elder Elvish. They appear suddenly in the city to take a specific action — abducting someone, removing an object, destroying a wall — then vanish. They never speak. Their real motives are unknown to observers.
**Goals:** Restore Dreta Phantas; promote the wellbeing of elves and their allies; alter key events they foresee to change future outcomes.
**Enemies:** Dark elves, the zaug, the rhodintor (servants of the Galchutt). A traitor, **Kohoath the Betrayer** (elf fighter8/wizard5/eldritch knight5), was driven mad and now works for the rhodintor.
**Disposition to PCs:** Unknown and unknowable to most. PCs may witness an Urthon Aedar action and have no idea what just happened. One human, **Dharim Boch** (fighter13), knows about them and lives among the Fate Weavers.
**[DM SECRET]** The Urthon Aedar are not extinct Elder Elves but an active group with a concrete agenda. Their signature item: the **Urthon Aedar Full Plate Armor** (minor artifact; no penalties for an elf eldritch knight with their blessing; otherwise acts as +1 full plate).
*(Ptolus p. 138–139)*

## Knightly and Religious Orders

### Knights of the Pale
**Type:** Knighthood (anti-supernatural)
**Head:** Dierna Hillerchaun (female human paladin10/Knight of the Pale10, CR 20) — based at the Bladechapel manor in Oldtown; one of the Commissar's Twelve Commanders.
**Other notable members:** Zophas Adhar (aasimar paladin/Knight of the Pale, Runewardens); Brig Stoneheart (dwarf paladin8, permanently magically enlarged); "Prince" Ironheart (human fighter13, body partially coated in iron — CR 13). Brig and Ironheart are known as the Brothers of the Heart.
**Description:** 16 knight-members plus ~32 retainers, men-at-arms, and cohorts. Small but extremely skilled. Dedicated to combating supernatural threats — primarily demons and evil spellcasters. Venerate saints of the Church of Lothian but are not officially the Church's military arm. Hold an Imperial Charter and receive direct Commissar funding (monthly stipend plus performance bonuses).
**Goals:** Combat supernatural evil; destroy or drive out demons; protect the city from otherworldly threats.
**Allies:** Work with Keepers of the Veil and the Malkuth.
**Enemies:** The Fallen (principal), chaos cults, evil spellcasters.
**Disposition to PCs:** Allied to any PCs fighting supernatural evil. Will stop PCs headed recklessly into the Necropolis or Dark Reliquary. Mid-level PCs make great temporary allies.
**HQ:** Bladechapel, Oldtown (PT5: p. 317). Private chapel with resident cleric Sister Terella Spoch (human cleric12).
*(Ptolus p. 125–127)*

### Keepers of the Veil
**Type:** Knighthood (anti-undead)
**Head:** Sir Beck Von Tibbitz (male human, tall, red-haired; head since 715 IA; one of the Commissar's Twelve Commanders). Co-leader: Phadian Gess (female human cleric9 of Lothian, CR 9) — warm-hearted, serves as seneschal of the Siege Tower.
**Description:** ~50 knights (mostly paladins and fighters, with some clerics, rogues, and arcanists) plus ~24 support personnel. Almost 800 years old — founded by Sir Vaklav Von Tibbitz (Beck's ancestor) among the Prust in Tarsis. Relocated entirely to Ptolus in 688 IA. The Von Tibbitz line has always been able to see spirits (invisible or incorporeal).
**Goals:** Destroy free-willed undead spirits that have crossed "the veil of death." Combat the Necropolis threat.
**Allies:** Knights of the Pale, Church of Lothian (strong ties). Phadian Gess is a Lothian cleric.
**Enemies:** All undead; the Fallen (occasional friction).
**Disposition to PCs:** Actively helpful to PCs fighting undead; will stop dangerous incursions into the Necropolis. Excellent mid-tier allies.
**HQ:** The Siege Tower — a small keep built into the wall around the Necropolis, with a fortified tunnel gate into the Necropolis itself. Sir Beck also has a home in Oldtown used as a secondary barracks.
*(Ptolus p. 119–121)*

---

### Knights of the Chord
**Type:** Knighthood (bardic / martial)
**Head:** Not specified by name in source.
**Description:** A small knightly order dedicated to freedom and protecting the innocent. Members have mastered the art of drawing power from music and song. Despite bard origins, these are martial warriors focused on combat, not performance. Hold an official Imperial Charter but receive no direct Commissar funding. Own a small keep in Oldtown.
**Goals:** Uphold freedom; protect the innocent; support House Kath and the arts.
**Allies:** House Kath (ancient ties — the house funds them and the knights protect it).
**[DM SECRET]** Navanna Vladaam maintains a false identity as "Nicalon Regelis," a Knight of the Chord — a supposedly devout worshipper of Jode who is poor at musical magic. Few other knights know Nicalon, who keeps to himself.
**Disposition to PCs:** Generally helpful; may hire PCs for missions involving threats to freedom or the arts.
*(Ptolus p. 123)*

### Knights of the Golden Cross
**Type:** Knighthood (ancient good-aligned order)
**Head:** Kaira Swanwing (female elf rogue5/wizard8, CR 13, Lawful Good) — silver hair and silver eyes, probably has Elder Elf ancestry. Idealistic, generous, ambitious for the order's goals not her own. HQ: a falling-down castle in Oldtown (PT5: p. 317).
**Description:** Founded millennia ago; one of the oldest organizations in the city. Extremely small. Selflessly altruistic — oppose evil for its own sake with no expectation of reward. Low profile; choose their battles carefully. Many Ptolusites distrust them for seeming too good to be true. Allied with House Nagel historically (ties have faded with time).
**Goals:** Oppose evil in all forms; specifically destroy House Vladaam (ancient enmity from the order's very founding, when they clashed with the original Vladaam — one of the Vested of the Galchutt).
**Allies:** House Nagel (historical). Kaira is friends with Rechel Pattemon of the Viridian Lords.
**Enemies:** House Vladaam (deep ancient enmity); House Dallimothan (will not work with them — former dragon slayers).
**Disposition to PCs:** Excellent allies for good-aligned PCs. Will provide information and resources to those fighting the Vladaams or similar evil.
*(Ptolus p. 124–125)*

### Brotherhood of Redemption
**Type:** Religious / civic (monks)
**Head:** Not named individually.
**Description:** An order of monks that believes killing evil beings is a waste. They seek to redeem evil rather than destroy it. ~80 years ago, they developed a process (alchemical mixtures, magical light, magical music) that actually turns evil creatures good. They pay adventurers **50 gp × CR** for living evil creatures delivered to them. Creatures are held in the **Fortress of the Redeemed** (dungeon below the Guildsman District) and subjected to the redemption process. Public-facing: the Monastery of Redemption in the Guildsman District.
**Goals:** Capture and redeem evil creatures; spread the belief in the sanctity of all life.
**Disposition to PCs:** PCs can earn money delivering live evil monsters. The brotherhood will not reveal the details of their redemption process to outsiders.
**[DM SECRET]** The Brotherhood does not believe in free will; their "redemption" is essentially magical brainwashing. They do not accept this characterization.
*(Ptolus p. 107–108)*

### Sisterhood of Silence
**Type:** Religious / civic (monk/fighter order, female only)
**Head:** Not named in source; the organization is described as having multiple Circles.
**Description:** All-female order of monks and fighters. Effectively a secondary police force — criminals fear them more than the City Watch. Members are incorruptible (never been a case of bribery). Training begins before maturity (adult women cannot join; girls who can't complete training are cared for until adulthood). Since 690 IA, hold special Imperial authority to apprehend criminals and keep peace. Patrols of Sisters are common in all districts except the Necropolis. Use a unique weapon: the **greathammer** (2-handed, 1d10 bludgeoning, ×3 crit) and special **stunning bolts** (+1 bolts, Fort DC 21 or stunned 1d4 rounds; made only for them by the Dreaming Apothecary). Have chapters outside Ptolus.
**Goals:** Uphold law; combat criminals; protect citizens (especially women, by reputation, though this is not strictly true).
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral-positive to law-abiding PCs. Will investigate crime and arrest suspects — they cannot be bribed. Will cross-examine PCs who are at a crime scene.
**HQ:** The Priory of Introspection (PT5: p. 374).
**Typical members:** 2nd Circle Sister (human monk8, CR 8); 3rd Circle Sister (more powerful).
*(Ptolus p. 134–136)*

## Civic and Delver Organizations

### Delver's Guild
**Type:** Civic / adventuring guild
**Head:** Guildmaster Delver Sorum Dandubal (male human expert4/fighter4) — rapidly becoming one of the most powerful people on the City Council.
**Description:** Founded just over 3 years ago; already one of the most powerful guilds in the city. Organizes those who explore the Dungeon below Ptolus. ~800 members; ~400 unaffiliated delvers. At any given time ~250 people are actively in the Dungeon. Guild pays 10 gp for maps of unmapped areas.
**Membership tiers:**
1. Associate Guildsman — 10 gp/year; guild information access, 10% discount at Ebbert's Outfitters.
2. Guildsman — 20 gp/year; + library, maproom, and waystations access.
3. Master Delver — 500 gp/year; + voting rights, retrieval insurance (body retrieval + raise dead paid by a special guild account).
4. Grand Master — 5,000 gp/year; + claim staking rights. Held by all 18 founding members.

**Disposition to PCs:** The natural home organization for adventuring PCs. Provides information, maps, and insurance. Will buy maps of unexplored areas.
**HQ:** Below Midtown, just off the Undercity Market (PT7: p. 429). Library and Maproom in Oldtown; librarian Shad Livbovic (human expert8). Map access: 1 gp; copying: 50–200 gp.
*(Ptolus p. 108–110)*

### The Guilds (overview)
**Type:** Civic (trade guilds)
Ptolus has numerous guilds. Key ones:
- **Bankers' Guild** — powerful; closely allied with House Abanar. Guildmaster: Chuster Nogol. Based in Oldtown.
- **Ironworkers' Guild** — one of the most powerful; controls the Goldsmiths', Silversmiths', Tinsmiths', and Coppersmiths' Guilds.
- **Masons' Guild** — notorious for corruption; Guildsman District.
- **Deathguilders** — morticians, embalmers, gravediggers, professional mourners; based in the Necropolis.
- **Longfingers Guild** — thieves' guild; see Criminal section above.
- **Order of Iron Might** — warriors' guild (see below).
- **Order of the Fist**, **Order of the Dawn**, **Naltegro Suun** — also non-standard "guilds" (see relevant entries).
*(Ptolus p. 115)*

### Order of Iron Might
**Type:** Civic (warriors' guild)
**Head (perfunctory):** Lord Dorant Khatru (rarely present). Actually run by full-time staff.
**Description:** Founded ~120 years ago as a mercenary/gladiator employment guild. 400+ members. Primarily a hiring hall for mercenaries, guards, and sellswords. Headquarters: the **Citadel of Might**, near the Arena in Oldtown. Features a training yard and crude flophouse (first-come, first-served each night). Dues: 20 gp to join + 10 gp/year. Benefits include training, discounted weapon/armor repair, and shared contacts.
**Disposition to PCs:** Good place to find mercenary work or hire muscle.
*(Ptolus p. 130–131)*

### Circle of Green
**Type:** Civic / natural (druidic council — mostly defunct)
**Description:** Over 1,700 years ago, the most powerful druidic conclave in Palastan and beyond. The Viridian Lords served as their envoys. The Circle wielded near-total authority over the wilderness. Now considered essentially defunct after centuries of decline. The Viridian Lords still patrol Palastan, but whether the Circle itself survives in any form is a matter of rumor.
**[DM SECRET]** There are hints the Circle may not be entirely gone.
*(Ptolus p. 108)*

### Viridian Lords
**Type:** Civic / natural (rangers of Palastan)
**Head (most experienced):** Rechel Pattemon (female human ranger20) — rarely comes to the city; friends with Kaira Swanwing and serves on the Commissar's Twelve Commanders.
**Description:** Rangers (some barbarians) who have bonded physically to the natural world through a ritual that infuses living plants into their flesh. Once closely tied to the Circle of Green. Their word is law in the Palastani wilderness. They enter Ptolus only occasionally and have no special authority here, though Palastani residents respect them.
**Structure:** No leader; no hierarchy. One is a Viridian Lord or one is not.
**Disposition to PCs:** Friendly to nature-aligned PCs; neutral to others. Excellent wilderness contacts/escorts outside the city.
**Typical member:** Neutral Good ranger4, CR 4.
*(Ptolus p. 140–141)*

### Fate Weavers
**Type:** Civic / esoteric (fortune tellers / prophets)
**Head:** Marija Elinek (female human expert11); associate Erred (male centaur expert7/warrior2).
**Description:** Founded generations ago by siblings Arlian and Nitham, friends of the hero Abesh Runihan, who taught them their grandmother's method of reading the skeins of destiny. Members bear their affiliation mark on their foreheads. They earn money by reading the destinies of others (individual fate, not specific predictions). Most people consider them charlatans. Many members are also Imperially-licensed prostitutes to fund their school, which hurts their reputation among respectable citizens.
**Fate Weaving:** Answers "Am I on the right path?" not "Where are the kidnappers?" DC 15 Wis-based check; DC increases with specificity and time range.
**Goals:** Preserve the gift; advise those whose fate they read; teach the next generation.
**[DM SECRET]** A human fighter named **Dharim Boch** (fighter13) lives at the school. He knows about the Urthon Aedar (a dying Urthon Aedar told him everything and gave him the signature full plate armor). He wears the armor and considers himself an honorary Urthon Aedar.
**Disposition to PCs:** Will provide destiny readings for a fee. May proactively warn PCs they believe are on a dangerous path.
**HQ:** School in the Rivergate District.
*(Ptolus p. 112)*

### Dark Leaf
**Type:** Criminal / civic (elven criminal network)
**Head:** Celdore Silverwood (rogue12) — de facto leader; commands great respect among the city's elves, nearly equal to Doraedian Mythlord.
**Description:** A very small confederacy of elves operating on the shadowy side of the law. More a loose network than a cohesive group — members never all gather together. At least two members are usually present at Iridithil's Home in Midtown. Hire themselves out as mercenary agents to major criminal interests (Balacazars, Killraven) while protecting elves from criminal victimization.
**Goals:** Ensure elves' safety in the city (by making Dark Leaf members too valuable to alienate); profit through mercenary work; maintain independence from gang loyalties.
**Allies:** Close ties to both Balacazar and Killraven networks; Celdore is personal friends with Cerrinthan Sanserra (Balacazar diplomat) and knows Killraven's key figures.
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral; elven PCs may receive warmer treatment. Celdore has also served as a high-paid neutral negotiator between criminal organizations.
**[DM SECRET]** Celdore committed a crime in the Moonsilver Forest (north of Ptolus) so serious he can never return. He will not speak of it.
*(Ptolus p. 108)*

## Supernatural and Otherworldly Organizations

### The Fallen
**Type:** Supernatural (demons)
**Head:** **Raguel** (demigod; use solar stats with the following changes: true Neutral, Charisma 30, no weapons, no wings; all evil/good restricted spells affect both alignments). Second in command: **Lilith** (unique demon; may be related to demon princes — actively schemes for more power than Raguel would take).
**Description:** An organization of "young demons" — demons born recently enough not to have remembered when the Galchutt ruled Hell. They followed their ruler Raguel into the world, knowing this traps them here forever. They reside in the **Dark Reliquary** in the Necropolis. They consider themselves superior to the Forsaken. Raguel commands them to maintain a low profile, but they do prey on travelers and rural communities to feed their appetites.
**Goals:** Raguel wants to understand why the Elder Gods sent him to rule a kingdom he didn't belong in. Lilith wants to reign in Hell next to the Galchutt once they're free. General goal: gather power.
**Allies:** The Forsaken (cohabitants in the Dark Reliquary), chaos cults (some), occasional Balacazar dealings, Vladaam family (indirect).
**Enemies:** Knights of the Pale (principal), Keepers of the Veil.
**Disposition to PCs:** Hostile. PCs who show up at the Dark Reliquary to fight them will likely die and are never heard from again. Some individual fallen might deal with powerful neutral parties.
**[DM SECRET]** The Dread Cloak (Menon Balacazar's artifact) was sold to him by Lilith, supposedly stolen from Orcus.
*(Ptolus p. 110–112)*

---

### The Forsaken
**Type:** Supernatural (death cultists / near-undead)
**Head:** **Shigmaa Urasta** (female tiefling; powerful combatant; shigmaa prestige class — PT6: p. 646).
**Description:** People who revere and live among undead in the Dark Reliquary (shared with the Fallen). Some aspire to become undead themselves (Licheloved) or simply want to be left alone with their undead companions. The distinction between Forsaken and their undead companions is nearly irrelevant — they work that closely together. Generate revenue by selling zombie slaves (through Balacazar connections) with magic items that let non-spellcasters control them.
**Goals:** Live among undead; embrace death worship; honor the gods of death (Heiran, Nareis, Maleskari). Recently allied with chaos cultists under Wuntad (Night of Dissolution plot).
**Allies:** The Fallen (cohabitants), the Vai (close ties), Balacazars (supply magic items and contraband), Zachean (drow vampire who found Dwarvenhearth). Wuntad has united the Forsaken with the chaos cults.
**Enemies:** City authorities generally; the Commissar would have sent an army to cleanse the Dark Reliquary by now if not for the Fallen's presence.
**Disposition to PCs:** Hostile unless PCs show reverence for undead. Will investigate grave-robbing by others (ironically — they rob graves themselves, which has led the Commissar to criminalize grave-robbing).
*(Ptolus p. 112–114)*

---

### The Fallen / The Forsaken combined note
Both groups occupy the **Dark Reliquary** in the Necropolis. The Fallen keep the authorities away; the Forsaken benefit from that protection. Neither group draws unnecessary attention per Raguel's orders.

### The Malkuth
**Type:** Supernatural (angels)
**Leader:** Sephranos, the Winged King (solar). If unavailable: Kadiradel (another solar). Highest-profile member (commonly mistaken for leader): Aoska (female half-celestial — no wings, single horn; one of the Commissar's Twelve Commanders).
**Description:** Angels who have voluntarily entered the world of Praemal, knowing this traps them here forever. They come because Ptolus holds the greatest concentration of evil in the world. Over the millennia they have had children (half-celestials, then aasimars) who are also welcome. Most true Malkuth keep to themselves in the **Pale Tower** in Oldtown, meditating; the half-celestials and aasimars handle mortal dealings.
**Goals:** Fight evil; protect the world from supernatural threats; hold vigil against the Galchutt's return.
**Allies:** Knights of the Pale, Keepers of the Veil.
**Enemies:** The Fallen (their direct opposite).
**Disposition to PCs:** Helpful to good-aligned PCs fighting evil. Will provide magical support and resources. Letters of credit from the Malkuth (1,000 gp) are legitimate currency.
**HQ:** Pale Tower, Oldtown (built ~300 years ago).
*(Ptolus p. 129–130)*

### The Vai
**Type:** Criminal / supernatural (assassins)
**Head:** The Twin Lords Keper — Reddis Keper and Neivis Keper. Almost no one knows these names exist (DC 30 to learn them). Rumors say one or both are recent replacements of the originals.
**Description:** Began as a cult devoted to death itself (not any specific death god). Members viewed killing as shepherding others to the "final resolution." Evolved into professional assassins over the past 60 years. Despite their feared reputation, actual membership numbers only ~35. Operate in cells of 2–4. Headquarters: the **Chapel of the Final Resolution**, somewhere below the Warrens.
**Goals:** Fulfill contracts; honor death as a philosophy; accumulate wealth and influence.
**Allies:** The Forsaken (close ties).
**Enemies:** Anyone who owes them a debt or has crossed them.
**Disposition to PCs:** Cold. Will take contracts against PCs if hired. In the original Ptolus Campaign, they offered to cancel hits on targets for double the contracted rate when cash-strapped — unusual but precedented behavior.
**Info checks (Knowledge [local]):** Assassin group exists (DC 10); worship death, kill as veneration (DC 12); base below the city (DC 15); Chapel of Final Resolution, below the Warrens (DC 20); in league with Forsaken (DC 23); led by twin brothers (DC 25); the Twin Lords Keper are extraordinarily powerful (DC 30).
**Typical member:** Rogue5/assassin2, CR 7.
*(Ptolus p. 139–141)*

### Pactlords of the Quaan
**Type:** Supernatural / monster (anti-humanoid coalition)
**Leadership:** Multiple individuals from various monstrous species; commands issued from an aboleth and a creature called the Eye of the Deep, based in underwater caves below the Docks.
**Description:** Ancient coalition of intelligent nonhumanoids who pacted in a magically binding agreement to eradicate the "main humanoid races." Membership includes aboleths, lamias, manticores, medusas, and even some dragons. No undead; virtually never includes humanoids (though some Pactslaves — dominated humanoids — serve them). Secretly operate the **Ennin**, the largest slaver operation in Ptolus (out of a cliff-built warehouse in the Docks).
**Goals (stated):** Eradicate humans, elves, dwarves, halflings, and gnomes — in that order. Near-term: acquire the **Black Grail** from the Banewarrens; accumulate power.
**Base:** Extradimensional realm called the Quaan; also an island in the Umbral Lake within the Eternity Cave underground.
**Allies:** Killraven Crime League (Kevris is building an alliance to use them).
**Enemies:** Everyone humanoid.
**Disposition to PCs:** Hostile. PCs who encounter them find them powerful, intelligent, and deeply dedicated. Occasional internal dissension due to differing racial perspectives is their only weakness.
*(Ptolus p. 130–131)*

---

### Soul Riders
**Type:** Supernatural (psychic parasites)
**Description:** ~12 individuals born with the ability to leave their own body and possess the souls of others. They have discarded their own bodies entirely and exist only as wandering soul-riders. In this state they cannot be affected by physical attacks or effects. They completely control their host's actions, emotions, and thoughts.
**Goals:** Secret world domination through controlling key individuals.
**Counters:** Banishment (forces out for 24 hours); trap the soul (imprisons rider); wish or miracle (kills); mind blank/protection from evil/magic circle against evil/holy aura (prevents possession but does nothing to a rider already in place). Willpower (Will DC 25 to resist; success means they can't try for 24 hours).
**Note:** The Commissar and powerful city figures take precautions (protection from evil spells and items). This is actually a major obstacle — those are exactly the targets the soul riders want.
**Disposition to PCs:** Terrifying long-term antagonists. PCs may notice NPCs acting strangely or that they themselves have gaps in memory.
*(Ptolus p. 137–138)*

---

### Naltegro Suun
**Type:** Criminal / civic (non-evil assassins)
**Description:** Only 13 members (rogues, assassins, fighters). Their name means "the just avenger" in an ancient western language. They are professional hired men who require proof that their target deserves their treatment before accepting a contract (not all assignments involve killing — beatings, arson, etc. are also in scope). They do not relish suffering — they are businessmen, not sadists.
**Goals:** Accept only justified contracts; adhere to their own ethical code; operate outside all criminal organization connections.
**Enemies:** No connections to other criminal organizations. City authorities are aware of them but largely tolerate them because they tend to do more good than harm.
**Disposition to PCs:** Will not take contracts against clearly innocent parties. PCs could hire them if they can demonstrate their target deserves the treatment.
**HQ:** Secret trap-filled meeting hall beneath a tower in northern Oldtown, near the King's River Gorge (PT5: p. 332); usually empty.
*(Ptolus p. 130–131)*

---

### The Shuul
**Type:** Civic / technological (pro-technology society)
**Description:** Dedicated to reviving Prustan technology as the Empire and its knowledge decays. Work hand-in-hand with temples of Teun and the Iron God (Prustan deities). Have gathered remaining technicians and scientists. Currently Ptolus-only, but plan to spread as a formal technological guild throughout the Empire. Their internal security force uses construct-like "Iron Angels" (use inevitable stats but focused on technology, not law).
**Goals:** Preserve and advance technological knowledge; strengthen the Empire; oppose the anti-technology influence of the Church (which they blame for suppressing science during the Days of Blood).
**Allies:** Kevris Killraven (backed by her), the Sorn (magical enforcers), House Shever. Tolerate the zaug among them due to the creatures' technological contributions (a dangerous oversight).
**Enemies:** Church of Lothian (philosophical opposition — the Shuul are anti-Church though pro-Empire).
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral to positive. Will hire PCs to acquire technological artifacts or protect their operations.
**HQ:** The Foundry (PT5: p. 185); Tower of Science (PT5: p. 334). Notable member: the Smoke Shop (PT5: p. 310).
*(Ptolus p. 132–133)*

---

### The Sorn
**Type:** Criminal / arcane (sorcerer cell network)
**Description:** A new organization backed by both Kevris Killraven and the Shuul. Almost entirely sorcerers (some wizards); no other classes eligible. Exclusive and physical — only combat-capable mages. Act as enforcers, spies, magical assassins, and combat support. Just under half are evil-aligned, the rest neutral. Always wear masks or disguise themselves. Organized into independent cells with no central headquarters or leader — lower-level members defer to higher-level ones; cells know little about each other.
**Goals:** Serve Killraven's interests; earn money through magical violence and espionage; some cells work with the Shuul, others as mercenary magical consultants.
**Note:** Their name is an Abyssal word meaning "breath" — the same root as the Sorn-Ulth (Ghul's orcs).
**Enemies:** Extensive list including the Inverted Pyramid.
**Disposition to PCs:** Neutral to hostile depending on PC alignment and affiliations.
*(Ptolus p. 137)*

## Faction Relationship Overview

The primary axis of conflict in Ptolus is **good vs. chaos/evil**:
- **Good side:** Knights of the Pale, Keepers of the Veil, Malkuth, Knights of the Golden Cross, Sisterhood of Silence, Church of Lothian, Delver's Guild, Brotherhood of Redemption
- **Chaos/evil side:** The Fallen, The Forsaken, Chaos Cults (Night of Dissolution), House Vladaam, Pactlords, Soul Riders

A separate conflict runs through the **criminal underworld**: Balacazar Crime Family vs. Killraven Crime League — active gang war.

The **Inverted Pyramid** is a swing faction: nominally neutral, but entangled with House Sadar (evil) through Renn Sadar's membership.

The **noble houses** form their own political web — see the allies/enemies table above.
