# Covenant of the Third Thought — Mechanical Reference

> **[homebrew]** The Covenant of the Third Thought does not appear in any Ptolus source (core book, Night of Dissolution, or Queen of Lies). This file is original campaign content. All canon Ptolus factions are in `factions.md`. See that file first if you need a canonical reference — the closest canon analog is the Grailwarden Dwarves (craftspsionic-adjacent) or the Chaos Cults (mind-corrupting). The Covenant of the Third Thought is created here as a new psionic faction for this campaign, in the spirit of Ptolus's existing societies.

---

## Concept

The Covenant of the Third Thought is a **secret psionic fellowship** operating out of Ptolus. Its members hold that the mind has three levels of thought: the first thought (instinct), the second thought (reason), and the **third thought** — the disciplined psionic will that perceives reality directly and shapes it without intermediary. The Covenant is neither evil nor good by alignment, but it is secretive and views non-psionic folk the way an adult might view a child — not unkindly, but with a certain condescension.

**Headquarters:** A hidden annex beneath a respectable private library in the Nobles' Quarter (exact building TBD, mark with `[session-specific]` when placed).

**Leadership:** A council of three — the Triad of Eyes. Each controls one of the three branches: Seers (clairsentience), Voices (telepathy), and Shapers (metacreativity). Stats TBD; treat each as a 14th-level Psion.

## PC Interaction Mechanics

### Becoming an Initiate

Requirements:
- Must be a manifester of any psionic class **or** have the Wild Talent feat.
- Must be vouched for by an existing member (minimum 1 month acquaintance).
- Must pass the **Unveiling** — a 1-hour telepathic interview conducted by a Covenant Seer. The PC makes an opposed Bluff vs. Sense Motive check (Seer has +18 Sense Motive). Failing to disclose a secret the Seer later discovers is grounds for expulsion.

Cost: None monetary. The initiation is a 4-hour ritual; the PC must spend 1 PP (unavailable until next rest) as a token of trust.

Rank structure (in order): Initiate → Acolyte → Voice/Eye/Hand (branch-specific) → Elder → Triad of Eyes

### Services Available by Rank

| Rank | Services |
|---|---|
| Initiate | Access to Covenant library (Psicraft and Knowledge [psionics] references; +2 circumstance on checks); purchase power stones at 10% discount |
| Acolyte | Borrow cognizance crystals for missions (returned on completion); access to a Covenant mentor (+2 on one chosen power's save DC, requires 1 week training/level gained) |
| Voice/Eye/Hand | Purchase psicrowns and dorjes at 15% discount; access to restricted powers list (see below); request backup from Covenant agents (1–3 members, 1/month) |
| Elder | Tuition in metapsionic feats (waive training time cost for one feat per year); free identify-equivalent via a Covenant Seer for psionic items |

## Preferred Disciplines

The Covenant favors two disciplines above all others:

**Telepathy** is the core discipline — considered the "truest" expression of third-thought mastery. Covenant doctrine holds that telepathy is not intrusion but *clarity*, and that unwilling targets simply haven't been enlightened yet. (The Triad privately disagrees about how far this reasoning extends.)

**Clairsentience** is the second pillar. Seers are the most respected branch, since perfect knowledge is prerequisite to perfect action.

**Metacreativity** is pragmatically valued — the Shapers branch builds items and constructs, funding the Covenant's operations.

**Psychokinesis and Psychometabolism** are accepted but considered somewhat crude — "first-thought disciplines" useful in emergencies.

**Psychoportation** is rare in the Covenant; the Nomad path is seen as lonely and undisciplined.

## Favored Psionic Items

The Covenant maintains a cache of items for loan to members on missions. Loaned items are tracked; failure to return them (without a documented reason) costs 1 rank or expulsion.

- **Cognizance crystals (5–9 PP):** Standard field kit for extended operations.
- **Dorje of Mindlink (1st-level, 50 charges):** Group communications on infiltration missions.
- **Third Eye Expose:** Issued to agents expecting psionic opposition — stripping enemy focus is a standard Covenant tactic.
- **Third Eye Sense:** Reserved for Seers of Elder rank.
- **Power Stones (Detect Psionics, Mindlink):** Bulk-purchased and distributed at Initiate rank.
- **Torc of Power Preservation:** One torc is held by the Covenant. Loaned only to Elders on critical missions.

## Psionic Creature Affiliations

The Covenant maintains cautious relationships with certain psionic entities:

**Psicrystals:** Covenant members who possess psicrystals are viewed with added respect. A psicrystal's presence in a meeting grants the manifester a +1 circumstance bonus on Diplomacy checks within the Covenant.

**Intellect Devourers:** The Covenant knows these creatures exist in the Undercity and treats them as existential threats. Any member who identifies an intellect devourer in the city is expected to report immediately (no exceptions). Covenant policy is eradication, not negotiation.

**Thought Eaters:** Covenant Seers maintain a list of sewer areas where thought eaters have been spotted. Members traveling the sewers are warned. No engagement policy — avoid and report.

**Cerebrilith:** The Covenant has encountered one in its history (details classified to Elder rank). Current Triad policy: a cerebrilith near Ptolus is an emergency requiring all available Elders. Any PC who reports a confirmed cerebrilith sighting gains 2 rank advancements immediately.

## Adventure Hooks

- A power stone intended for delivery to the Covenant was intercepted — by whom? The stone contains a 5th-level power never formally described to the PCs.
- A Covenant Acolyte has gone silent in the Dungeon. The Triad wants the body (or the person) found quietly, before city authorities connect the disappearance to the Covenant.
- An intellect devourer has been body-hopping in the Docks District. The Covenant suspects one of its own Initiates has been compromised. The PCs are asked to run a quiet telepathic survey — but one of the Covenant members running the survey is the compromised one.
- The Covenant offers the PCs Acolyte rank in exchange for retrieving a psionic artifact from the Bane Warrens. The Triad does not explain what the item does.
