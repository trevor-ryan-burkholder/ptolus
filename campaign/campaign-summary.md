# Ptolus: Spire-Forged Faerûn — Campaign Summary

**Genre:** Arcanopunk noir / urban fantasy sandbox / cosmic horror (high levels) / monster-politics / delver economy / faction warfare / late-game metaphysical apocalypse.

---

## I. Core Setting Concept

Ptolus is inserted into the Forgotten Realms on the **Nelanthir coast** as a major western port-city. It sits beneath the Spire exactly as in canon. The city retains all canonical elements: delver culture, noble politics, the Inverted Pyramid, chaositech, the Undercity, the Banewarrens, Jabel Shammar, monster integration, district structure, faction complexity.

The rest of Faerûn treats Ptolus as: **dangerous, profitable, politically volatile, vaguely cursed, impossible to ignore.**

---

## II. The Spire & Cosmic Structure

**Jabel Shammar came first.** The fortress already existed. The Banewarrens were inside it. The world tried to push the evil away. The Spire erupted upward around the fortress.

The Spire is **NOT** affected by: monster population, civic unrest, politics, crime.

The Spire responds **ONLY** to: artifact drift, planar pressure, Banewarrens resonance, apex-level metaphysical activity, tampering with Jabel Shammar.

**The city and the Spire are separate systems.**

---

## III. The Banewarrens & Orcus Endgame

- The Banewarrens are fused into the apex fortress.
- Evil artifacts drift toward Jabel Shammar.
- **Orcus is imprisoned within the fortress as part of the seal architecture.**
- Jabel Shammar is effectively a multidimensional pressure vessel.

**At level 20+, players:**
- Ascend the Spire
- Navigate Jabel Shammar's impossible geometry
- Find Karn's old party scattered through the fortress
- Discover Orcus is part of the binding system
- Face a final choice: escape / preserve the seal / destroy Orcus / redirect the Banewarrens artifacts elsewhere (possibly the Fortress of Regrets)

*This is epic-tier content only.*

---

## IV. Karn

See `campaign/npcs/karn.md`.

**Most important rule: Karn is NOT a central plot point.** He functions like All Might losing his power — removal of the adult in the room, the disappearance of certainty and safety. He disappears at level 1 and does not meaningfully matter again until level 20+. This is deliberate.

---

## V. The Opening

Campaign begins with Karn leaving a letter for the PCs, handing them his home, business, and contacts, departing alone for the Spire.

> *"You are not very concerned for Karn despite perhaps a little worry. If there's anyone who can brave the Spire alone it is him. However, that lack of concern does not extend to yourselves. Until now you felt an overwhelming sense of safety under the tutelage of Karn. With him gone, a great sense of unease washes over you. You have lost the certainty that you will win."*

**Emotional thesis of the campaign: "The safety net is gone."**

---

## VI. Campaign Structure

**Sandbox with gravitational pull** — players roam freely, the city reacts dynamically.

| Arc | Levels | Focus |
|-----|--------|-------|
| I — Welcome to Ptolus, Kid | 1–5 | Street-level survival: delver jobs, crime, factions, permits, monsters, city politics |
| II — The City Has Teeth | 6–10 | Party becomes visible: guild wars, noble politics, drow operations, chaositech, monster integration conflicts |
| III — The Undercity Wakes | 11–15 | City's true nature: aberrations, deep delves, Banewarrens outskirts, psionic anomalies, demonic factions |
| IV — The City Starts to Break | 16–19 | High-tier destabilization: planar leaks, district crises, Church fractures, Special Grade incidents, political warfare |
| V — The Spire Calls | 20+ | Mythic ascent: Karn returns, Jabel Shammar, Orcus, Banewarrens core, cosmic geometry |

---

## VII. The Monster Grade System (JJK-Style)

Grades: **Grade 4 / Grade 3 / Grade 2 / Grade 1 / Special Grade**

Governs: legal status, movement restrictions, permits, city safety protocols, Commissariat response levels.

Does **NOT** affect the Spire.

| Example | Grade |
|---------|-------|
| Goblins | Grade 4 |
| Illithids | Grade 2 |
| Beholders | Grade 1 |
| Aboleths | Special Grade |

---

## VIII. The Covenant of the Third Thought

See `campaign/factions/covenant-of-the-third-thought.md`.

Hidden psionic shadow faction. **Not evil.** Core belief: *"If Ptolus falls, the world follows."*

---

## IX. Tone

Mos Eisley + Planescape + Dark City + Dune conspiracies + Forgotten Realms high fantasy + urban horror + cosmic pressure-cooker politics.

Grounded through: bureaucracy, economics, delver logistics, monster law, faction realism.

The city feels: **dangerous, alive, politically layered, old, pressured, functional only because everyone is terrified of collapse.**

---

## X. Core Design Principle

**Ptolus itself is the game.** The content, the ecosystem, the main character.

**The Spire is the horizon** — the thing players eventually earn the right to confront. Keep it distant. Do not let it overwhelm the setting.
