# Ptolus in Forgotten Realms — Placement

**DECISION LOCKED:** Ptolus sits on the **Nelanthir coast** of Faerûn.

The city remains fully itself — its Spire, its Dungeon, its pantheon, its districts. The Nelanthir coast placement gives it access to western sea trade, geographic distance from the major FR power centers (Waterdeep, Cormyr, Sembia), and enough isolation to make it feel like its own world while remaining part of Faerûn.

The rest of Faerûn treats Ptolus as: dangerous, profitable, politically volatile, vaguely cursed, impossible to ignore.

---

## Original Candidate Notes (Pre-Decision, for reference)

## Candidate A — Vesperin Peninsula (the Vast region) — **strongest fit**

The peninsula east of Cormyr and north of Sembia, between the **Dragon Reach** (north) and the **Sea of Fallen Stars** (south). Real FR cities on this peninsula include Procampur, Tantras, and Calaunt — all established trade ports with Cormyrean and Sembian commercial ties. Add Ptolus as a new, larger sister city on the eastern tip, with the Spire dominating the cape.

**Geography**: A roughly 200-mile peninsula extending east into the Sea of Fallen Stars and Inner Sea. Earthfast Mountains immediately south. Vast Swamp inland.

**Neighbors**:
- **Cormyr** (west) — strong knightly state; supplies the "elsewhere is law-and-order" feel.
- **Dalelands** (west, beyond Cormyr) — woodland, druidic, ranger country.
- **Sembia** (south, across the sea) — merchant princes, intrigue, rival to Ptolus's trade.
- **Impiltur** (east, across the Easting Reach) — paladin-knight kingdom.
- **Aglarond / Thay** (far east) — the Red Wizards as setting-level antagonists.
- **Earthfast Mountains** (immediately south) — dwarves, orcs, classic mountain dungeons, Underdark entries that connect into Ptolus's Dungeon.

**Tone**: Cosmopolitan trade-city flavor matches Ptolus well. Faerûn's sword-and-sorcery mix sits comfortably alongside Ptolus's. The Empire of Tarsis can be aligned with the old Imperial holdings that ran across this region.

**What changes**:
- The Spire is visible from Sembian and Cormyrean coasts. (50,000 ft. = visible from ~600 miles in clear weather. *Everyone* knows it's there.)
- Trade with Suzail, Selgaunt, and Waterdeep (by sea) is routine.
- Knights of the Pale operate alongside Cormyrean Purple Dragons and Impilturan paladins.
- The Cults of Chaos may have ties to Thayan Red Wizards or to Banites elsewhere on Faerûn.

**Adventure variety**: 9/10. Trade, court intrigue, mountain dungeons, sea piracy, Underdark, foreign empires.

## Candidate B — Velen Peninsula (Tethyr coast) — **best thematic fit**

The peninsula on Faerûn's southwestern coast, west of Tethyr proper, jutting into the **Trackless Sea**. The town of Velen is the real FR settlement here. Ptolus replaces or sits adjacent to Velen on the cape, with the **Forest of Tethir** filling the peninsula's interior.

**Geography**: A ~300-mile peninsula extending west into the Trackless Sea. Forest of Tethir covers most of the interior. Cliffs along the south coast.

**Neighbors**:
- **Tethyr** (east) — kingdom with literal Imperial history; the Empire of Tethyr's collapse and resurrection map cleanly onto Ptolus's Empire of Tarsis.
- **Amn** (north) — merchant aristocracy; trade rivals.
- **Calimshan** (south, across the Shining Sea) — genie-touched, desert culture, slavers — natural foil.
- **Maztica** (far west, across the Trackless Sea) — colonial frontier (if you want voyages-of-exploration as a side-arc).
- **Forest of Tethir** — wild elves, gnolls, Shilmista-style fey, classic wilderness adventures.

**Tone**: The strongest fit for Ptolus's "fallen Empire" identity. Tethyr's own history of imperial collapse and restoration is essentially Ptolus's backstory written in Faerûn. Cosmopolitan port + ancient Imperial overhang + adjacent wild forest is the Ptolus formula.

**What changes**:
- The Spire dominates Tethyr's western coast; Riatavin and Zazesspur orient by it.
- Calimshan and Amn become principal trade rivals.
- Slavers (Pale Tower) align tightly with Calimshite slave-trade norms.
- Wild elven encounters in Forest of Tethir for ranger/druid PCs.

**Adventure variety**: 8/10. Strong on Imperial-history arcs and wilderness; lighter on Underdark and cold-climate adventures.

## Candidate C — Sword Coast North (peninsula near Waterdeep) — **best leverage of familiar territory**

A homebrewed peninsula north of Waterdeep, perhaps replacing or augmenting the **Mere of Dead Men** area or sitting between Neverwinter and Waterdeep. Ptolus becomes a major Sword Coast power.

**Geography**: A ~150-mile peninsula extending west into the Trackless Sea. Mountains immediately inland (High Forest? Sword Mountains? — pick).

**Neighbors**:
- **Waterdeep** (south) — Faerûn's most famous city; rival trade hub.
- **Neverwinter** (north) — friendlier counterpart.
- **Luskan** (further north) — pirates and Arcane Brotherhood.
- **High Forest** (east) — wilderness with elven, druidic, ancient ruins.
- **Underdark** (below) — Menzoberranzan and others connect via Ptolus's Dungeon, giving the drow plot ready-made.

**Tone**: Most leverage of familiar FR material. Players who know Faerûn from prior games immediately orient. Waterdeep's rivalry with Ptolus provides easy political tension.

**What changes**:
- Most invented geography (you're slotting a new peninsula into a well-mapped region).
- The Spire's visibility is awkward — it would be famous on the Sword Coast, but it's not in canon.
- Player suspension of disbelief works hardest here.

**Adventure variety**: 8/10. Strong on Sword Coast adventures, urban political, Underdark drow. Requires the most homebrew justification.

## Candidate D — Easting Reach Coast (near Impiltur / old Narfell)

A peninsula on the east coast of the Sea of Fallen Stars, between Impiltur (south) and the Great Glacier / former Narfell (north). Ptolus sits at the cape; the Easting Reach to the west; cold tundra to the north; Impilturan civilization to the south.

**Geography**: A frontier peninsula, ~200 miles, jutting north or east into colder waters.

**Neighbors**:
- **Impiltur** (south) — paladin-ridden kingdom; cultural ally for Knights of the Pale.
- **The fallen empire of Narfell** (west/northwest) — actual demon-summoning fallen empire; chaos cult precedent ready-made.
- **Damara/Vaasa** (west) — Bloodstone-era frontier campaigns.
- **The Great Glacier** (north) — arctic adventures, frost giants.
- **Aglarond / Thay** (south, across the Sea) — sorcerous antagonists.

**Tone**: Frontier-Imperial. Cold, rugged, ancient. Narfell's history of demon-summoning maps cleanly onto Ptolus's Cults of Chaos — the chaos cults could be Narfellian survivors operating in Ptolus's shadow.

**What changes**:
- Cold climate alters the city's flavor (snow, sea ice, brazier-warmth).
- The Spire stands on a cold cape, visible across the Great Glacier.
- Adventure tone shifts north — frost giants, white dragons, vrylokas (if you allow Ravenloft bleeds).

**Adventure variety**: 7/10. Strong on cold/frontier themes, weaker on cosmopolitan trade.

## Recommendation summary

| Candidate | Imperial flavor | Cosmopolitan port | Underdark/Dungeon | Wilderness | Homebrew burden |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Vesperin (Vast) | ✓ | ✓✓ | ✓ (Earthfast) | ✓ (Dalelands, Vast Swamp) | Low |
| B — Velen (Tethyr) | ✓✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓✓ (Forest of Tethir) | Low |
| C — Sword Coast | ✓ | ✓✓ | ✓✓ (drow) | ✓ (High Forest) | High |
| D — Easting Reach | ✓ (Narfell) | (limited) | ✓ | ✓ (Great Glacier) | Medium |

If pressed: **B (Velen / Tethyr)** for the strongest thematic match between Ptolus's lost-Empire identity and Faerûn's most parallel kingdom; **A (Vesperin / the Vast)** for the cleanest cosmopolitan-port integration with the least homebrew friction.

## What to settle before session 1

Whichever you pick, decide and write up:

1. **Travel distances** to neighboring Faerûn cities (so PCs from Waterdeep or Suzail have a real journey).
2. **Who currently rules Ptolus in this version of the timeline** — does the Commissar still hold? Is the city a vassal of a larger FR power? An autonomous polity?
3. **What the Empire of Tarsis was, in Faerûnian terms** — was it the Empire of Tethyr? An older empire that predates current FR kingdoms? Both?
4. **Pantheon coexistence** — see `pantheon-coexistence.md`.
5. **What's directly downhill in the Dungeon** — does Ptolus's Dungeon connect to Menzoberranzan? To the Underdark below the Earthfast? To a wholly separate Underdark layer?
