# Pantheon Coexistence — Ptolus Gods and Faerûn Gods

The campaign uses **both** pantheons. Ptolus gods are dominant **in and around the city**; the Faerûnian pantheon is dominant **elsewhere on Faerûn**. This document settles how that works in-world and at the table.

## The in-world story

Long before the Empire of Tarsis fell, the gods of the Ptolus pantheon were the gods of the Imperial heartland. The Empire's reach was wide but never universal — beyond its borders, the gods of Faerûn (Mystra, Tymora, Lathander, Tempus, etc.) held sway. When the Empire collapsed, the Ptolus pantheon retreated to its core territory — the peninsula and the city around the Spire. Faerûn's gods, meanwhile, expanded into the formerly Imperial provinces.

The result, in the present day:

- **In Ptolus and the peninsula**: Lothian, the Watcher of the Skies, Teun, Navashtrom, Gaen, Tardeshou, Ahaar, Phoeboul, and the rest of the Ptolus pantheon receive nearly all worship. (Eslathagos Malkith is *not* a worshipped god — he is the Dread One; see `srd/ptolus/pantheon.md`.)
- **Elsewhere on Faerûn**: Mystra, Tymora, Lathander, Selûne, Tempus, Helm, Kelemvor, etc. — the Faerûnian pantheon — receive nearly all worship.
- **In the border lands**: mixed. Travelers' shrines often acknowledge both pantheons. Trade quarters in Faerûnian cities have Lothian shrines; trade quarters in Ptolus have Tymoran shrines.

## Theological explanations on offer

Different in-world factions explain the coexistence differently. Pick the one you like best, or let the question hang:

1. **They're all real, distinct, and separate.** Two pantheons, both real, both with portfolios. The gods themselves know about each other and largely respect territorial conventions. *Easiest for players to grasp; minimal worldbuilding strain.*

2. **They're aspects of the same gods under different names.** Lothian and Torm (or Lathander) are the same being known by two names; the Watcher and Helm; Teun and Gond; etc. *Theologically clean but limits the distinctness of Ptolus's flavor.*

3. **The Ptolus gods are older and were forced into a smaller territory.** They were major regional powers before Faerûn's pantheon expanded. The Empire of Tarsis was their last stronghold. Most of them are diminished from their former state. *Adds dramatic weight to the Imperial decline.*

4. **The Ptolus gods exist because of the Spire.** Their power, or even their existence, is bound to the Spire and its environs. They can hear prayers and grant spells within the Spire's range but are weaker or silent outside it. *Tightly couples pantheon to setting; explains the geographic concentration.*

> **Recommended**: option (1) for player simplicity, with option (3) hinted at as the truth that priests of the Ptolus pantheon quietly admit when asked. The chaos cults, of course, have their own theology — they worship the imprisoned Galchutt, which neither pantheon acknowledges as gods.

## Cleric character creation

A PC cleric picks **one deity** from either pantheon. The deity determines:

- Alignment restrictions (within one step of the deity's alignment).
- Available domains (use the deity's domain list from its source — Ptolus core book for Ptolus gods, *Faiths and Pantheons* for FR gods).
- Holy symbol and turn/rebuke choice (good clerics turn undead, evil clerics rebuke; neutral choose at 1st level).

A cleric of a Ptolus god in Faerûnian territory is *unusual* but not impossible. A cleric of a Faerûnian god in Ptolus is *common* — there are small shrines to Lathander, Tymora, and Tempus in the docks and Outwall.

## Cross-pantheon mappings (option 2 reference)

If you want overlaps for narrative convenience without full deity-merging:

| Ptolus god | Faerûnian counterpart | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lothian, the Lawgiver | Torm (justice/law) or Lathander (sun) | Law, justice, healing, the sun |
| The Watcher of the Skies | Helm, or Oghma (knowledge) | Vigilance; sees and knows all, even the future |
| Teun, Mother of All Machines | Gond | Craft, invention, technology |
| Tevra | Gond (healing aspect) | Machines and healing; daughter of Teun |
| Navashtrom, God of Strength & Harmony | Tempus | Strength, the warrior's path; patron of adventurers |
| Gaen, Goddess of Light | Lathander (or Selûne) | Light |
| Tardeshou, Goddess of Truth | Tyr (or Oghma) | Truth, justice, knowledge |
| Ahaar, Lord of the Air | Akadi / Aerdrie Faenya | Air, freedom |
| Phoeboul, God of Dreams | Sehanine Moonbow | Dreams (elvish god) |
| The Sisters of Death (Heiran & Nareis) | Kelemvor (neutral) / Myrkul (evil) | Death — but the Sisters are *evil* and minor; Ptolus has no benign death-god |
| The Galchutt / chaos cults | (no analog) | Foreign primordials; not part of either pantheon |

These are loose mappings — useful when a PC wants to switch worship after leaving Ptolus, or when an NPC in Faerûn proper needs a comparable god for cultural translation. They are not in-world declarations that the gods are the same.

## The chaos cults

The Galchutt and the dark gods worshipped by Ptolus's chaos cults are **not** part of either pantheon. They predate both. Faerûn's powers know about them and consider them a Ptolus problem. If chaos cult activity ever spreads beyond Ptolus's reach — through smuggled artifacts, dormant cells in Faerûnian cities, etc. — Helm, Torm, and Tempus's churches will take an interest, but they treat the Galchutt as a foreign threat, not a familiar enemy.

## Player-facing notes for session 0

Tell your players, in plain terms:

> Ptolus has its own gods — they're the dominant religion in the city. Faerûn's gods are dominant everywhere else. If you want a Ptolus-pantheon cleric, you're playing someone whose worship is centered on the city. If you want a Faerûnian-pantheon cleric, you're an immigrant or someone who picked up the faith abroad — your home shrine is in Ptolus's Outwall or Docks, not the Cathedral of St. Gustav.

This usually settles the cleric/paladin discussion in five minutes.
